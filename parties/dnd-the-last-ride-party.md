## The Last Ride

### Members
- **Brenna Ashfall** — Half-Orc Barbarian — The smiling tip of the spear; she goes first because she wants to and the others let her because they're afraid of what happens when she stops.
- **Pip "Tallowfingers" Underbough** — Lightfoot Halfling Rogue — The mouth, the hands, and the reason they're heading toward a Coppersmile warehouse instead of an honest job.
- **Sister Almeth Vohr** — Human Cleric of the Grave (Wounded Order) — The conscience and the field medic, who keeps trying to talk Brenna out of dying and Pip out of murder, and is losing both arguments.
- **Doran Quill** — Wood Elf Ranger (Hunter) — The scout and the quiet one; tracks, cooks, watches the treeline, and is the only person Brenna actually listens to when she listens at all.
- **Mireille "Mira" Fenwick** — Human Wizard (Divination, Apprentice) — The map, the memory, and the nervous compass; she joined for "research" and is realizing what that word costs out here.
- **Toth Vellarin** — Tiefling Warlock (Archfey, Pact of the Tome) — The charm, the lockpick of conversations, and the one whose patron asks for favors at inconvenient moments.

### How They Met
A caravan job out of Hallow's Reach went catastrophically wrong six weeks ago. The merchant — a fat-pursed wool-trader named Oster Mell — had hired Doran as a guide and Almeth as a healer for what should have been a four-day haul to Greycopse. He'd picked up Mira at the last waystation because she'd offered coin to ride along ("collecting field notes on rural ley-line drift"), and Toth had attached himself uninvited at a roadside shrine by simply being charming enough that nobody told him to leave. Brenna had been three days on foot, walking nowhere in particular, and signed on as muscle the morning they rolled out. Pip was already in the wagon, under a tarp, having stowed away two stops back to get clear of a Coppersmile lieutenant who'd recognized her in a tavern.

On the third night the caravan was hit — not by bandits but by something worse, a pack of ghoul-things working in coordination under a hooded rider who never dismounted. Oster Mell died screaming with his hands in Almeth's, and Almeth could not call him back; her god was watching but not moving. Brenna walked into the middle of the pack laughing, took a bite to the shoulder that should have killed her, and didn't stop swinging until Doran's arrows and Toth's eldritch fire had carved a path out. Mira recognized the rider's sigil and went white. Pip, who had emerged from her tarp halfway through the fight, picked Oster's strongbox clean before the body cooled and then — to everyone's quiet surprise — split the contents six ways at dawn without being asked.

They buried Oster under a cairn Doran built. Almeth said the rites. Brenna stood at the edge of the circle and didn't speak. Nobody went home.

### What Holds Them Together
The hooded rider is still out there, and Mira knows the sigil — it belongs to a cult she was specifically sent by her academy to *avoid investigating*, which means somebody at the academy knew. They are, collectively, the only six people who saw what happened on that road and lived, and splitting up means dying alone of something they don't understand. There's also the practical matter: Pip's share of Oster's strongbox was generous enough to keep them fed for two months, but it's running out, and the warehouse job she keeps almost-mentioning would set all six of them up for a year. Nobody has said yes. Nobody has said no.

### The Fault Line
Almeth and Brenna are heading for a collision and everyone can see it. Almeth has figured out — from the silver pouch, from the way Brenna prays to nobody, from the scars — what Brenna is doing, and she will not let a parishioner walk into the dark on her watch, even one who hasn't asked to be a parishioner. Brenna finds Almeth's concern unbearable, partly because it's working. The day Almeth says the wrong gentle thing at the wrong campfire, Brenna will either break her jaw or break down sobbing, and neither of them knows which, and Pip has a running bet with Toth about it that Doran refuses to acknowledge exists.

### Shared Secret
They all know Mira lied about why she's with them. She said "field notes" at the first campfire and none of them bought it, and after the ghoul-rider she admitted — quietly, to Almeth, who told the others — that her academy "reassignment" had been more of an expulsion, and that the satchel she guards so carefully contains a half-translated codex she was not supposed to remove from the library. None of them have asked to see it. None of them have told her they know they're being hunted partly because of what she carries. They've simply, collectively, decided to keep walking with her. It's the closest thing to love any of them has felt in years and none of them will name it.

### First Session Hook
They've ridden into Hallow's Reach to resupply and to let Pip make contact with a fence about the warehouse job — and Brenna's brother Korv is in the market square when they arrive, recognizes her across fifty feet of crowd, and calls her name in a voice that cracks. He has a letter in his hand he was about to post. Behind him, leaning against the well and very much not a coincidence, is a well-dressed human with a pleasant smile who Pip has never met in person but has memorized from a hundred secondhand descriptions: Vance Coppersmile, in Hallow's Reach, three weeks early and four cities off his usual circuit, talking pleasantly to a hooded figure on horseback whose sigil Mira has seen before. Korv is still walking toward Brenna. Pip's hand is already on Last Word. Almeth is the only one who has noticed that Toth has gone completely, unnaturally still, as if listening to a voice none of the rest of them can hear.
