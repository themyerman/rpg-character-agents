## The Hollow Verity — Crew Brief
*Tohoku-class freighter — the ship that delivers cargo nobody wants to admit they ordered*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Ship** | The Hollow Verity (Tohoku-class transport, retrofitted with a long-range science bay nobody talks about) |
| **Condition** | Engine purrs, but something in the aft bulkhead whispers when the lights cut out |
| **What It Owes** | Forty thousand credits to a Persephone fixer named Ang Wei, plus a "specimen" still owed to a Blue Sun shell company that paid up front |

### Crew
- **Captain Marisol Vance** — Captain — Ex-Independent quartermaster who keeps the books honest and the crew lying to themselves about what's in the cold locker.
- **Theron "Tick" Okafor** — Pilot/Mechanic — Talks to the ship more than the crew, and the ship has started talking back; flies like he's dodging something only he can see.
- **Dr. Yseult Maren** — Doctor/Xenobiologist — Cashiered Alliance researcher with a steady hand, a private notebook, and a habit of staying up three nights running when the cargo is "interesting."
- **Bram Kettley** — Grifter/Muscle — Smooth-talking failed seminarian with a left hook and a tendency to laugh at the wrong moments, especially when things start moving on their own.

### How They Came Together
Marisol bought the Verity at auction on Beaumonde after her previous ship, the *Sparrow's Grace*, was impounded for running medicine through Alliance space during the postwar amnesty fiasco. She found Tick in a Higgins' Moon drunk tank, muttering coordinates that didn't exist on any star chart and offering to fly anything that would get him off-world. He hasn't explained the coordinates. She hasn't asked.

Dr. Maren came aboard six months ago on Persephone, fresh from a hasty Alliance discharge nobody will detail in writing. Ang Wei made the introduction — and the loan — and suggested that a ship doing the kind of work the Verity was about to take on would benefit from a medic who didn't ask questions and a xenobiologist who already knew the answers. Marisol agreed. She regrets agreeing. She regrets it more every week.

Bram talked his way aboard at a customs checkpoint on Boros, claiming to be Maren's cousin, then claiming to be a chaplain, then finally admitting he just needed off the planet before sundown. He's been useful. He's also been seen praying in the cargo hold, which is new.

### What Holds Them Together
The contract. Blue Sun's shell company paid half up front for a specimen retrieval, and the back half is enough to clear Ang Wei's debt and put the Verity into proper repair for the first time in two years. None of them can walk away yet — Marisol needs the money, Tick needs somewhere to be that isn't planetside, Maren needs the access only this job provides, and Bram needs the Cortex to forget his face for another six months. They are bound by ledgers and lack of options, which is the only glue strong enough out here.

### The Fault Line
Dr. Maren knows exactly what's in the cold locker, and Marisol does not — not really, not the specifics. Maren told her it was "biological samples of Alliance interest" and let the captain decide how much she wanted to know. Bram has started piecing it together from Maren's late-night notebook entries (he reads everything that isn't locked, and some things that are), and Tick already knows because the ship told him. When Marisol finds out what she's actually been hauling, and that three of her four crew kept it from her, the Verity will either fly through it or break apart on it.

### Shared Secret
Two months ago, somewhere between Whitefall and the Verity's unscheduled detour to a moon that isn't on the Alliance survey, the crew opened a sealed canister to verify the specimen was intact. They all saw it. They all agreed, without speaking, never to discuss what they saw or what it did to the deck plating before they got the lid back on. Tick has been having the dreams since. Maren has been having ideas. Marisol has been pretending she saw nothing, which is the Independent veteran's solution to most problems and works about as well now as it did at Serenity Valley.

### First Session Hook
The Verity is docked at Bellerophon's lower service ring, where Maren has arranged a meeting with a former colleague — Dr. Iden Chau, now in the private employ of a floating estate owner — who claims he can authenticate and double the specimen's value to a private buyer. The catch: the buyer wants to see it *intact and active* at his salon gathering tomorrow night, which means the crew has to smuggle the canister past three layers of estate security, perform a live presentation without letting anyone (including themselves) understand what they're presenting, and get out before Ang Wei's collector, who just landed at the same docking ring asking about a captain matching Marisol's description, catches up. Bram thinks he can talk them through the door. Tick thinks the ship doesn't want to leave the canister behind this time. The canister, for what it's worth, has started humming.
