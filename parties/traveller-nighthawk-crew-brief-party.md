## *Nighthawk* — Crew Brief
*Scout/Courier — a quiet ship carrying four people who each survived something they shouldn't have*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Ship** | *Nighthawk* (Type-S Scout/Courier — Nasrin's muster-out ship, must be made available to the Scout Service on request) |
| **Condition** | Spaceworthy but tired; the starboard maneuver drive runs hot since the misjump, and the medbay is a converted supply locker |
| **What It Owes** | Nothing to a bank — and everything to the Scout Service, which can recall the hull or the pilot at any time, and to whatever sealed file at Sector Command still has Aldric's name on it |

### Crew
- **Major Aldric "Stoneface" Vehr** — Commander / Gunner / Tactical — Retired Marine major, sole survivor of the Kessar Drop, looking for the names of the men who signed the order.
- **Nasrin "Nas" al-Qadeer** — Pilot / Astrogator / Owner — Scout veteran with the fastest hands in the sector and a ship she'd rather share than fly alone again.
- **Korven "Half-Lung" Drask** — Purser / Fixer / Muscle — Drifter who knows every dockside in the subsector and is waiting for a ghost from a twelve-year-old job to either thank him or kill him.
- **Joseph "Joe" Two-Rivers Whitethorne** — Engineer / Medic — *(STR 6, DEX 8, END 9, INT 10, EDU 6, SOC 5)* — Lakota-descended belter-stock mechanic out of the Tsiolkovsky asteroid clans; ran a salvage tug for nine years before a reactor blowout took the ship and his younger brother Cael with it. Spent two years drifting between stations, taking every engine job he could find and none of the company, until Nasrin's cargo ramp. Talks to the drive the way his grandmother talked to horses, and it listens.

### How They Came Together
Nasrin came out of her thirty-one days adrift unwilling to fly alone again, and the first person she hired was the engineer her Vilani rescuers had recommended — Joe Two-Rivers, who'd spent the two years since the Whitethorne family salvage tug ate itself — and his younger brother Cael with it — moving station to station, taking every engine job he could find and none of the company. He took the job because she asked in his grandmother's pidgin, which she'd picked up on the long ride home, and because the *Nighthawk*'s drive was making a sound that needed fixing and he had nowhere else to be that mattered.

Korven came aboard on Thassik Station, where Nasrin was looking for a cargo broker and Korven was looking for a way off-world before the wrong people noticed him breathing. He paid his passage in introductions — three fixers and a customs officer in the first week — and somehow forgot to leave at the next port. Aldric joined last, six months later on Karsten's Reach of all places, walking up the ramp in a civilian coat that didn't hide what he was. He'd spent his pension's first quarter chasing the access logs from his own classified file, and one of the trails led to a Scout-flagged courier crewed by a pilot the Service owed favors to. He offered his two Ship Shares and his Cr82,000 in exchange for a berth and the use of the ship's comms suite. Nasrin took the shares. Joe took his measure across the galley table and nodded once.

### What Holds Them Together
The *Nighthawk* is Nasrin's, but the four of them have made it work — Aldric's money keeps the fuel tanks full, Joe keeps the drive lit, Korven keeps the cargo manifests profitable and the dockside cops uninterested, and Nasrin flies. None of them has anywhere better to be, and each of them is chasing something that requires a ship that can go quietly to strange ports without filing a flight plan. That's a rarer thing than money.

### The Fault Line
Aldric is a Marine major who spent twenty-four years giving orders, and Nasrin is the legal owner of the hull and the only one with a pilot's ticket. So far Aldric has been careful to *suggest* rather than command, and Nasrin has been careful to *consider* his suggestions — but the day a job goes wrong and Aldric reverts to the man who walked off Kessar, she'll have to decide whether this is her ship or his operation. Korven, who has watched officers and captains his whole life, is quietly betting on Nasrin and quietly preparing to be wrong.

### Shared Secret
Joe's Vilani rescuers told Nasrin, on the long ride home, what they'd actually been hauling when they diverted to pick her up: sealed medical containers out of a Sector Command contract, labeled with a station designation Aldric went pale at when she mentioned it once over whiskey. **Kessar.** None of them has said the word since. Joe has worked out from the drive's radiation signature that the *Nighthawk* itself made an unscheduled stop somewhere near that system during her last Service refit, before Nasrin was given the keys. Korven has noticed that all three of his crewmates go quiet at the same moments and has elected, professionally, not to ask.

### First Session Hook
The message Korven's been waiting twelve years for finally arrives at Tsabo Down — *"I found what you were looking for. Don't come alone."* — and the station of origin is a derelict research platform in the Kessar subsector that officially does not exist. The same week, Brigadier Carrowen sends Aldric a private courier packet: a recall offer, off the books, for a "site survey" of a facility the Imperium has lost contact with. The coordinates match. Nasrin's High Passage ticket would get one person there in comfort on a liner; the *Nighthawk* could get all four of them there quietly in eleven days. Whatever's waiting on that station has been waiting a long time, it isn't entirely dead, and it remembers being Marines.
