## **Nadezhda "Nadya" Okafor-Vance**
*Independent freight broker working out of a back office on Beaumonde — the kind that doesn't have a sign on the door.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Appears to be** | A mid-tier cargo broker pushing a time-sensitive medical shipment to a Border clinic |
| **Actually is** | *(revealed only in Truth 2, 3, or 4 below)* |

**Appearance:** Mid-forties, broad-shouldered, hair pulled back so tight it pulls at her temples. She's wearing a perfectly respectable rust-colored coat over a clean blouse, and she's tucked a hand-cannon — a Callahan revolver, the long-barreled kind people carry when they want to be seen carrying it — into the front of her waistband like she forgot it was there. When she leans in to talk numbers, her breath hits you like a dead animal in a hot crawlspace. She doesn't seem to know. Nobody's told her in a while.

**The Pitch:** *"I'm not going to waste your time pretending this is easy money, dong ma? I got six crates of plasma synth and a bundle of pediatric antivirals that need to be in Hera dirt inside nine days, and the broker who was supposed to move 'em got himself shot in a card game on Tuesday. Stupid, stupid man. Now — the manifest's clean, the customs stamps are real, and I'll pay good coin to a captain who can keep a schedule and keep his mouth shut about the route. You look at me like I'm lying, but I'm not. I'm just in a hurry."*

**The Job:** Pick up six refrigerated crates and a sealed medical bundle from a bonded warehouse in Beaumonde's dockside district. Deliver to a contact named Bram Iwu at a clinic in Aberdeen township on Hera. Nine days. No opening the crates. No deviations from the filed flight plan.

**The Payment:** 1,800 platinum on pickup, 4,200 on delivery. She'll also throw in a refueling voucher good at any Blue Sun-affiliated yard in the Georgia system — worth maybe 600 more to a crew watching the gauge.

**The Truth (GM rolls 1d4 in secret — only one is real):**

1. **Straightforward** — It's exactly what she says. The complication: the previous broker's killer thinks the crates were *his* score and has put a two-man crew on the dock watching for whoever picks them up. Nothing to do with Nadya. Just inheritance of someone else's enemies.

2. **One Layer Down** — The medical bundle is real and going to a clinic. The six crates of "plasma synth" are mislabeled — they're industrial-grade ceramic precursors used to line homemade ship hulls, and Nadya knew. Not contraband exactly, but a customs inspector who looks twice will hold the ship for a week of paperwork while everything else spoils.

3. **The Real Story** — Nadya's brother runs the Aberdeen clinic and it's failing. She's been quietly skimming from larger shipments for two years to keep him stocked, and someone in her own office has finally noticed the pattern. This run is her getting clear of the books before an audit. The Callahan in her waistband is because she expects a visit, not from the crew.

4. **The Reversal** — The "pediatric antivirals" bundle is real medicine, but it was stolen four days ago from a Companion-funded charity hospice on Persephone that serves Rim refugees. Nadya bought it cheap from the thieves and is reselling it at markup to a private buyer on Hera who runs a paid clinic for ranch executives. The kids it was meant for are still sick. The clean manifest is a forgery. Bram Iwu isn't her brother — he's the buyer's accountant.

**Why They'd Take It:** Six thousand platinum is six thousand platinum, and the refueling voucher solves a real problem if the crew's been burning expensive Border-station fuel. Nine days Beaumonde-to-Hera is tight but doable, and the manifest looks clean enough to wave at any patrol that pulls them over. A Doc aboard might also note that pediatric antivirals are genuinely scarce and worth doing right.

**Connection:** *Yolanda Petrescu*, a longshoreman foreman who runs the bonded warehouse Nadya uses. Yolanda owes one of the crew (or a mutual contact) a favor from a bar fight last year. Asked over a drink, she'll say: *"Nadya pays on time and don't argue the weight. But she's been jumpy these last two months — comes down to the docks herself now, won't send her clerks. And she started carrying that ridiculous gorram pistol in March. Whatever she's into, she's into it alone, and she's scared."*
