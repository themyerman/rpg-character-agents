## **Halvar Czeszko**
*Patron — independent free trader with a cargo problem*

| | |
|---|---|
| **UPP** | 633364 |
| **Apparent Affiliation** | Owner-operator of the *Spinward Gull*, a Type-A free trader running mid-range routes |
| **Real Affiliation** | A heavily indebted merchant whose ship is leveraged to a loan shark on Regina |

**Appearance:** A stocky man in his late fifties with a salt-stained merchant's jacket two seasons out of fashion, nursing a cheap blue gin he hasn't actually been drinking. His hands shake slightly when he sets the glass down, and he keeps glancing at the bar's main entrance every time the door cycles.

**The Pitch:** *"You look like people who know how to load a hold without asking the wrong questions. I'll be straight — my engineer walked off last week and my certs lapse in nine days, so I can't lift. I've got a contract for forty tonnes of agricultural machinery sitting on pad twelve that needs to be on Pixie by the end of the month or I pay penalties I can't pay. You take it, you keep sixty percent of the delivery fee, and I keep my license. Nobody loses but the clock."*

**The Job:** Transport 40 tonnes of crated "agricultural processing equipment" from the current starport to Pixie (two jumps), deliver to a receiver named Mira Konteva at the Pixie downport bonded warehouse. Manifest and customs paperwork already filed under Czeszko's company name — crew flies as subcontracted carriers.

**The Payment:** Cr 18,000 on delivery (60% of a Cr 30,000 contract), plus Czeszko transfers his pre-cleared customs filing — meaning no inspection delays at either end. He'll also throw in a working but elderly Cargo Handler-3 loader bot as a "thank you."

**The Truth (Referee rolls 1d4 — only one is real):**

1. **Straightforward** — It really is agricultural machinery. The contract is real, the receiver is real, Czeszko is exactly the broken-down trader he appears to be. The complication: a rival hauler underbid for the same contract and lost, and they're petty enough to tip off port authority about "irregularities" — meaning the crew will face an aggressive customs hold on arrival at Pixie and need to talk their way clear.

2. **One Layer Down** — The cargo is real agricultural machinery, but the crates also conceal forty cases of unlicensed pharmaceuticals (legal at origin, restricted at Pixie) tucked into the equipment housings. Czeszko knows. He didn't tell the crew because he assumed they'd refuse, and he's gambling that the pre-filed paperwork carries them through. If caught, it's his name on the manifest — but it's their ship in impound.

3. **The Real Story** — Czeszko doesn't own the *Spinward Gull* anymore. He lost it three months ago to a Regina-based loan shark named Pavel Brait, who now uses the ship to move people — specifically, indentured workers being trafficked to corporate agri-worlds. The "machinery crates" on pad twelve contain twelve sedated humans in life-support pods. Czeszko is being forced to hire an unwitting crew to make the run because Brait's regular pilot was arrested last week, and a fresh, clean ship arriving at Pixie won't trigger the watchlist flags the *Gull* now carries. Czeszko's shaking hands aren't from drink — they're from what he's about to do to strangers.

4. **The Reversal** — Czeszko isn't Brait's victim. He's Brait's founding partner — the one who built the trafficking network fifteen years ago and quietly stepped back when Imperial attention got uncomfortable. The broken-down trader act is practiced; he's been running it for years, and fresh crews who pity him are his preferred recruitment method. The *Spinward Gull* was never at risk, the loan-shark story is standard cover for why he can't fly the run himself, and the shaking hands are a performance he's long since stopped thinking about. Mira Konteva on Pixie isn't a co-op manager — she's the network's distribution coordinator. The twelve people in the pods aren't an accident the crew stumbled into. They're the job. Czeszko will be on a ship to somewhere else before the crew clears the jump point, and whatever the crew decides to do with the cargo — deliver it, free it, sell the information — he's already been paid.

**Why They'd Take It:** The money is decent, the paperwork is already done, and the man across the table is so obviously a tired old trader in over his head that helping him feels like the right call.

**Connection:** **Mira Konteva**, the listed receiver on Pixie, is a legitimate agricultural co-op manager who has done three previous clean deliveries with Czeszko years ago — any broker the crew asks will vouch for her. She has no idea what's in the crates this time, and her name being on the manifest is exactly why the crew will believe the job is real.
