## **Velka Tonn-Aurelio**
*Independent fixer (unwitting) — A retired performer turned gallery darling whose name opens doors he doesn't know are guarded.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Faction** | Independents (socialite circuit, Procyon orbital) |
| **Appears to be** | A washed-up celebrity reinvented as a serious artist, dabbling in patronage |
| **Actually is** | *(revealed only in Truth 2, 3, or 4 below)* |

**Appearance:** Mid-forties, soft around the middle in a way he's stopped hiding, paint under his fingernails that he keeps forgetting to clean. Wears a tailored coat two seasons out of fashion. His left pupil is fixed wider than the right — old cosmetic implant burning out — and he tilts his head to compensate when he looks at you, like he's still framing the shot.

**The Pitch:** *"My wife says you're discreet, and discretion is the only currency I trust anymore. There's a man — Hadrik Voss, a critic, you wouldn't know him — who has a private collection. In it is a painting. Mine. From before. The kind of thing I made when I was someone else, and he's been threatening to show it at his next salon unless I pay him what I do not have. I want it back. I want every copy. I want the whole drawer it's sitting in, if that's what it takes. Sera — my wife — she said you'd understand a man wanting to bury his own ghost."*

**The Score:** Break into Hadrik Voss's residence in the Cleresty Spire on Procyon Station — a refined apartment with mid-tier private security and a biometric vault. Recover an old holo-print and any digital copies. Voss travels for the Lumen Festival in four nights; the window is roughly thirty hours.

**The Payment:** 4 Coin up front from Velka's gallery account, 4 Coin on completion. Plus — and he says this carefully — *"a letter of introduction to anyone you'd like to meet on this station."* He means it. His name still opens private rooms across three sectors.

**The Heat:** Low (1). Voss is a private citizen with no Hegemony ties anyone's mentioned. A clean residential job by the numbers. Which is exactly the kind of score that should make a careful crew slow down.

**The Truth (GM rolls 1d4 in secret — only one is real):**

1. **Straightforward** — The painting exists. Voss is exactly the petty blackmailer Velka describes. The complication: Voss didn't actually leave for the festival — he canceled quietly after a health scare and is home, asleep, with a panic button wired to a private response team that arrives in four minutes.

2. **One Layer Down** — The "painting" is a holo-print, yes, but it isn't from Velka's performing days. It's a piece he made last year — an abstract that, decoded through a specific lens filter, is a map of safehouses. He doesn't know what he painted. He was using reference images Sera left on his studio terminal. Voss figured it out. So did someone else who's also coming for it tonight.

3. **The Real Story** — Sera Tonn-Aurelio runs Crossfire, a guns-for-hire ring operating out of three systems. Voss isn't a blackmailer — he's a Hegemony Intelligence asset who's been building a case on Sera for eighteen months. The "painting" is evidence: ledgers, contracts, names. Sera fed Velka the blackmail story and pointed him at the crew because she needed deniable hands to burn the evidence and she'd rather her husband cry on a stranger's shoulder than ask her directly. If the crew succeeds, Crossfire stays buried. If they fail, Sera disappears in twelve hours and Velka takes the fall for hiring them.

4. **The Reversal** — Hadrik Voss is a witness. Six years ago, a Crossfire contract killed his sister and two of her colleagues — labor organizers on a Hegemony moon. Voss has spent every coin and favor he has assembling proof, and he's three days from delivering it to a sympathetic magistrate on Aklun. The "painting" is the proof. Sera knows. Velka, who genuinely loves his wife and genuinely doesn't ask questions, has been pointed at the crew like a finger at a trigger. He'll cry real tears in the pitch. He has no idea he's asking them to murder a grieving man's last six years.

**Why They'd Take It:** 8 Coin for a residential B&E is generous, and Velka's social access is the kind of Rep that opens scores the crew can't currently reach — invitations to closed auctions, intros to mid-tier nobles, a face at parties where contracts get signed. For a crew climbing out of Tier 0, this is a ladder. The pitch is sympathetic. The mark sounds like a creep. Everything about it reads as easy money and a foothold in respectable circles.

**Connection:** **Imoja Kell**, a fence who moves art through the Procyon underbelly, has done business with Velka twice. She'll say: *"He's exactly what he looks like. Sweet man, bad picker. Whatever you're doing for him, the job's not the job — his wife's the one with the knives, and she doesn't tell him where she puts them."*
