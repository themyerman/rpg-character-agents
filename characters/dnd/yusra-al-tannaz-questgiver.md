## **Yusra al-Tannaz**
*Quest Giver — a master glassblower with smoke-dark circles under her eyes*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Appears to be** | A respected guild artisan asking the party to recover stolen work |
| **Actually is** | (Revealed only in Truth 2, 3, or 4 — see below) |

**Appearance:** A wiry woman in her late forties, hands scarred with old burns and one fresh bandage across her left palm, wearing a heavy leather apron she didn't bother to take off before coming to find you. She carries a small wooden case clutched against her ribs like a child, and when she sets it down on the table she does it with both hands — too carefully.

**The Ask:** *"Three weeks ago someone broke into my workshop. They didn't take coin. They didn't take the silver leaf or the cut crystal — and that's worth a year of any of your lives, so don't tell me they were ordinary thieves. They took one piece. A commissioned reliquary, blown to a private design, meant for House Veshren's chapel. I have until the new moon to deliver it or I lose the commission and probably the guild seal with it. I've asked the watch. I've asked the guild. Nobody will help me, and I cannot — I will not — make another. Find it. Bring it back. I don't care how."*

**What They Want:** Track down the stolen glass reliquary — a hand-sized vessel of cobalt and gold leaf, shaped like a closed lotus — and return it to her workshop in the Lanternsmith district before the new moon (six days out). She believes the thief works out of a warehouse near the lower docks; a neighbor saw a hooded figure go that way.

**What They're Offering:** 200 gp in cut coin up front, another 300 on delivery, and — more interestingly — a written introduction to House Veshren's steward, who hires adventurers privately and pays in favors that open doors no amount of gold will.

**The Truth (DM rolls 1d4):**
1. **Clean** — Exactly as stated. A rival glassblower named Petrak hired two cheap knives to steal the reliquary and ruin Yusra's commission. The reliquary is in a warehouse on the docks, lightly guarded. The complication: Petrak's hired knives are skittish and will smash the reliquary the moment they think they're caught. Recovery requires stealth, not force.
2. **Complicated** — The reliquary is real, the theft is real, but Yusra blew a hidden compartment into the base at the buyer's secret request. Inside is a folded letter sealed in wax — House Veshren's correspondence with a foreign agent. Yusra knows this. She didn't mention it because she assumed you'd ask more questions or charge more. The thief was hired by House Veshren's rivals, not by a fellow glassblower.
3. **The Real Story** — Yusra's apprentice, Toma, stole the reliquary himself and faked the break-in. He's been embezzling materials for months to pay off a gambling debt to a moneylender called the Cormorant, and the reliquary was supposed to clear the books. Yusra suspects but cannot bring herself to know — the boy is her late brother's son. She wants the party to find the truth so she doesn't have to. The fresh bandage on her hand is from punching a wall the night she figured it out.
4. **The Reversal** — There was no theft. Yusra blew the reliquary, looked at what she had made, and *broke it herself* — smashed it on the workshop floor at three in the morning. The reliquary was commissioned to hold a saint's fingerbone that House Veshren acquired by murdering a rival cleric; Yusra learned this the day before delivery from the cleric's surviving daughter, who came to beg her not to finish the work. Now Yusra is panicking. She needs a plausible thief — a real one, caught with real evidence — to take the blame before House Veshren's steward comes asking. The party is being set up to "recover" a planted decoy from a warehouse where Yusra has paid a desperate man to wait. If they succeed, an innocent (well — guilty of other things) man hangs, and Yusra's hands stay clean. The daughter who came to her workshop is named in the Connection below.

**Why They'd Say Yes:** The introduction to House Veshren is the kind of patronage that turns adventurers into people whose names get remembered — and Yusra's desperation reads as honest, because most of it is.

**Connection:** **Sister Ilenya Korovan**, a young cleric at the Shrine of the Quiet Lamp, came to Yusra's workshop on the night before the reliquary was due. If the party asks anyone in the Lanternsmith district about Yusra's recent visitors, her name surfaces. She knows more than Yusra does about what House Veshren has been doing — and she is afraid.
