### Description
She is *long* — disproportionately so for 200 tons — with a needle prow and the long-baseline jump grid of a vessel built to make the run in three days flat and brag about it on arrival. Her hull is lacquered cobalt over silver underplating, and the dorsal racing stripe still carries the trim-tape of the Spinward Cup three seasons running. Step through the iris and you smell jasmine air-scrub and warm leather; the bridge is a single curved sweep of crystal and brushed nickel, and the acceleration couches retain the body-memory shaping of whoever last sat in them. Under the deck plates in the aft companionway — if you know where to listen — the resonance is wrong by a centimeter and a half.

### History
She was built at the Lyrian Yards in 1087 as a vanity commission for **Sami Al-Hassan**, a textile heir who wanted to win the Trojan Reach circuit and did, twice, before losing interest and selling her on at thirty percent under list. Her second owner was **Budi Suphan**, a broker out of Pax Rulin who never raced her at all — and who used her J-3 legs and her sealed luxury holds to move a series of items the Imperial Ministry of Justice would very much have liked to inspect. The case against Suphan was opened in 1098, quietly closed in 1101 for reasons that involved a senior magistrate's sudden retirement, and the file was archived rather than purged. The bespoke smuggling compartments — three of them, totaling about six tons, accessed through the wine cellar, the engineering crawlway, and a false bulkhead behind the master suite's wardrobe — were never found, because the inspectors were looking for a freighter's tricks, not a yacht's.

Her third owner was **Sakura Suriyawong**, a retired Scout commander who bought her at auction and spent four years using her as a private courier between the Marches and Deneb before her health failed. Sakura kept the compartments. She used two of them for unregistered antiquities and the third, by all accounts, for nothing at all — she liked knowing it was empty. The Pilgrim came onto the market again eight months ago at a price suspiciously close to her scrap value, and the broker did not make eye contact during the walkthrough.

### Quirks
- **The Port Blind Spot:** The portside sensor array has a 14-degree arc, roughly four o'clock low, where returns dissolve into static no matter how the array is recalibrated. The previous owners learned to roll her three degrees to starboard on final approach to any inspection vessel. Nothing has ever quite filled that gap, and a careful pilot has used it more than once.
- **The Wine Cellar That Isn't:** The forward luxury suite includes a climate-controlled cellar rated for 240 bottles. Behind the third rack, a magnetic seal releases the largest of the three hidden compartments — 3.5 tons, lead-lined, sensor-baffled. The wine, incidentally, is real and quite good.
- **The Lyrian Whine:** At jump initiation her grid emits a high harmonic for about eleven seconds that sets every loose piece of metal on the ship singing. Racers called it the *pilgrim's hymn*. It cannot be silenced; it can only be expected.
- **Couch Memory:** Every acceleration couch retains the shaping of its most-used occupant for weeks. The captain's couch on the bridge still holds the shape of a small, narrow-shouldered woman — Sakura, presumably — and refuses to take a new impression for the first dozen hours of any new pilot's tenure.
- **Overtuned Drives:** The M-drive can be pushed to M-4 for short bursts, a holdover from her racing days. The power plant complains bitterly and requires a full diagnostic afterward, but in a chase it has saved her three times that the logs admit to.

### Crew Roles
- **Captain/Pilot** — She handles like a racer, not a freighter; reflex training and a light hand are mandatory, and she will punish a Routine pilot the way a thoroughbred punishes a farm boy.
- **Astrogator** — A J-3 hull lives or dies by its plots; her owners have historically taken her on routes other ships can't, which means she rarely follows beaten lanes and her astrogator is expected to find the quiet vector.
- **Engineer** — Must know Lyrian Yards' proprietary jump-grid tuning *and* be the kind of person who doesn't ask why the wine cellar has a magnetic seal on the racks.
- **Steward** — Six luxury staterooms mean she pays her bills carrying high-passage charters, and the steward is the public face of the ship to people who tip in platinum.
- **Gunner (optional)** — One turret fitted, one capped; in the Marches even a yacht keeps the turret crewed when transiting Five Sisters.

### Current Situation
She is two days out from Aramis on a vector toward Pysadi, carrying a sealed charter cargo (manifest: *philosophical instruments, fragile*) and a single passenger who boarded at Aramis Highport under the name *Veren Coll*, asked three questions in the first hour — about the ship's encryption suite, about the manifest seals, and about whether the captain had ever met Sakura Suriyawong — and has not spoken or left their stateroom since. Meals are accepted through the door slot. The charter pays well, in cash, on delivery. The compartment behind the wardrobe is, the current crew has every reason to believe, empty. Whether that is still true after the passenger boarded is a question no one has yet had the nerve to ask.

### GM Notes
*The Suphan file was archived, not purged — and the Ministry of Justice has just appointed a new senior investigator who is reading old cases for fun. Somewhere in the next two or three jumps, an Imperial cutter will request a courtesy inspection that is not actually a courtesy, and they will be looking for the compartments specifically because someone finally read the footnote. The passenger may be running from the same investigation, running it, or carrying the one item in the empty compartment that ties the whole twelve-year-old conspiracy back to a sitting Count. The Pilgrim has been faster than her past for eighteen years. She is about to find out whether she still is.*
