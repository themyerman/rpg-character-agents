## **The Salt-Lamp Oysterhouse**
*A reopened harborside eating-house in a fishing village where the tide has started bringing in things that shouldn't float.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Coastal village & newly-reopened oyster hall |
| **Condition** | Freshly whitewashed, smelling of lye and old smoke; three nights open, three nights of trouble |

### Atmosphere
Mist rolls off the bay in slow vertical sheets, the way smoke does — not the way mist should. The Oysterhouse glows yellow through fogged glass, its salt-crusted lamps hung low over long communal tables, and the smell of buttered crab and woodsmoke is so warm and right it almost covers the faint iron-and-brine note underneath. Outside, the village of Coldreef is quiet at this hour, except for the dogs, which have not stopped facing the water since sundown.

### Notable NPCs
- **Ixchell Tenoch** (new proprietor of the Oysterhouse) — Bought the shuttered building for almost nothing six weeks ago and reopened it with surprising capital; claims to be a widowed cook from down the coast, but her knives are military-issue and she flinches at bells.
- **Winona Littlebear** (harbormaster, lifelong resident, "everyone's aunt") — Has lived in Coldreef for forty years that no one can quite verify; she remembers the village's drowned grandparents by name but cannot name her own. The strange tides started the week Ixchell arrived. Winona is not worried about Ixchell. Winona is worried about what Ixchell might *find*.
- **Zolin Chalco** (a fisher, drunk and loud tonight) — Pulled something up in his nets two nights ago that he buried behind his shed instead of reporting. Will start the fight. Does not know why he's so angry; he only knows he hasn't slept.

### The Situation
Coldreef sits above something. Winona Littlebear knows this because Winona Littlebear *is* something — a sea-thing in a borrowed shape who took the place of a drowned woman two generations ago and has kept the village quietly safe ever since, in exchange for a thin tithe of memory and the occasional unexplained disappearance. Ixchell Tenoch is a former templar-investigator hunting exactly this kind of arrangement; the Oysterhouse is a stakeout. Neither has confronted the other yet — they're circling, both pretending. Meanwhile the *real* problem is that something deeper than Winona has woken up beneath the reef, and it's pushing strange catches into the nets. Zolin's buried "fish" is a finger-bone the size of a longsword, and it's been whispering to him.

### Hooks
1. The party orders food. Zolin lurches up from a neighboring table, accuses a quiet stranger of "stinking like the thing in my shed," and swings — the stranger ducks, the punch lands on a party member, and within six seconds half the room is fighting and Ixchell is moving toward a hidden crossbow behind the bar with a look that says *finally*.
2. Winona pulls a party member aside after the brawl, presses a carved bone token into their hand, and says quietly: "The woman who runs the Oysterhouse is going to ask you to help her kill someone tonight. Please don't. I'll pay better."
3. A child runs in from the docks, soaked, calm, and dry-eyed, asking if anyone has seen her mother — who, according to three different patrons, died eleven years ago.

### GM Notes
Play the fight as chaos with stakes: Zolin isn't a villain, he's a man who hasn't slept in 48 hours and is being whispered to. Let the party pick a side between Ixchell and Winona slowly — both are lying, both are sympathetic, and the *real* threat under the reef is bigger than either of them and benefits from their feud. Keep what Winona actually is ambiguous as long as possible; she should feel like a beloved aunt right up until she doesn't, and even then the question of whether she's a monster or a guardian should never quite resolve.
