## **The Grove at Whispermoss Hollow**
*A circle of moss-bearded oaks the locals refuse to name aloud, now functioning — barely — as an unsanctioned waystation.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Druid grove turned reluctant trading post |
| **Condition** | Leadership vacuum; the deputy is improvising badly |

### Atmosphere
The standing stones at the grove's edge are warm to the touch, even in the rain, and someone has draped them in oilskin tarps to keep travelers' goods dry — a small desecration nobody mentions. Woodsmoke drifts from a low timber inn built against the roots of a hollow oak, and the air smells of wet bracken, mutton stew, and a faint copper tang that doesn't belong. Three ravens watch from the same branch, and have not moved or blinked since the party crested the ridge.

### Notable NPCs
- **Roimata Ngāpō** (acting warden of the grove) — A junior druid in over her head; she's allowing trade and lodging here to keep the grove "useful" so the Circle won't dissolve it, but every coin spent under these branches makes the old wards weaker, and she knows it.
- **Yonas Gebre** (innkeeper, half-elf, ran the place "informally" for twenty years) — Pours generous drinks and asks gentle questions; keeps a locked cedar box under the bar containing a signed letter that proves a regional magistrate ordered a caravan ambush six winters ago. He will trade it, but only for something specific he hasn't named yet.
- **Kwabena Quispe** (traveling cartwright, or so he says) — Approaches the party at the gate before they speak, addresses one of them by a name they may or may not have given, and asks if they're "here about the bell." There is no bell. Yet.

### The Situation
Archdruid Vethka Mourn vanished forty-one days ago into the deeper wood, and Roimata — her apprentice — has been making compromises to keep the grove from being declared lapsed: letting Yonas's inn expand, accepting "donations" from passing merchants, looking away when hunters use the old stone road. The wards are thinning. The ravens are a symptom. Meanwhile Yonas has spent two decades quietly collecting incriminating documents from drunk travelers, and one of them concerns Magistrate Halric Vond — the same official the party has business with in the lowlands. Kwabena is not a cartwright; he's a scout for something that has been waiting for the wards to weaken, and he recognized the party from a description given to him three nights ago by someone who shouldn't have known they were coming.

### Hooks
1. Kwabena walks up smiling, says "You're earlier than I was told — come, come, the bell's almost ready," and turns to lead them into the grove without waiting for an answer.
2. Yonas refills a cup the party didn't order and sets down beside it a folded parchment bearing Magistrate Vond's seal, says nothing, and walks away.
3. Roimata corners the party near the standing stones at dusk and offers them coin — grove coin, old and unfamiliar — to go find her teacher in the deep wood, because the ravens have started turning their heads now and she doesn't know what comes after that.

### GM Notes
Let the three threads tangle: Yonas's letter is the party's leverage over Vond, but Roimata needs them gone *into* the woods, and Kwabena wants them gone in a very different direction. Play Yonas as warm and unhurried — his power is patience, not menace. Keep what Kwabena actually serves ambiguous; the answer should arrive through play, not exposition, and the ravens turning their heads should happen on-screen at the worst possible moment.
