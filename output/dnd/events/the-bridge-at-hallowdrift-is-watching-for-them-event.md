## **The Bridge at Hallowdrift Is Watching For Them**
*Fires when the party is traveling on the road in heavy rain and reaches the only crossing for miles — a covered wooden bridge with lantern-light flickering inside it.*

### When It Fires
The storm has been worsening for hours. Visibility is down to maybe forty feet, the road has turned to clay, and the party is cold, soaked, and moving slowly along a route they've been on before. Ahead, the covered bridge at Hallowdrift Ford appears through the rain — the only dry shelter for hours, and the only way across the swollen river. Lantern light glows inside it. There are figures on the near end, hooded against the weather, not moving.

### What Happens
As the party approaches, a man steps out from under the eaves with a crossbow held casually but ready. He's flanked by three others in oilskin cloaks. He calls out a name — not anyone in the party, but a description: "the ones who came through with the merchant Sebastián Castro, three weeks back. Stop where you are." This is **Björn Egilsson**, a hired tracker working for the family of someone who died in a job the party took weeks ago — a guard, a bandit, a bystander, whoever the GM can tie back to a previous fight. He's been waiting at this bridge for six days because he knew they'd have to cross it eventually. He wants them alive for questioning. His people have already cut the rope ferry downstream.

### The Party's Position
The party knows they're cold, exposed, and standing in the open on a muddy road with their backs to a rising river. They don't yet know which job this is about, who died, or that Björn isn't here to kill them — he's here to drag them back to a grieving house that paid him in advance and expects a reckoning.

### Possible Responses
1. **Talk through the rain.** Björn will name the dead person if pressed, and his demands are specific: come unarmed to a hearing at the Castro estate two days' ride south. If the party can prove the death was clean — or pay weregild — he may take coin instead of bodies. He is professional, not vengeful.
2. **Fight on the bridge.** The covered span is a chokepoint. Crossbows have advantage from cover; the planks are slick; anyone going over the rail is in the flood. Björn will retreat and pick them off rather than brawl in close quarters.
3. **Go around.** The river is fordable upstream, maybe, in this weather — a Survival or Athletics gauntlet against the current, with gear and mounts at risk. Björn will pursue once he realizes, and now he's angry.

### Complications
- One of Björn's three companions is the **dead person's younger sibling**, who is not a professional and is not here to negotiate. If a fight starts, or if the party tries to buy Björn off, this one acts on their own terms.
- The Castro name on Björn's lips is a problem on its own. **Sebastián Castro is still alive** and has been telling people the party betrayed him on that job — which is the first the party will hear of it. He's the one who pointed Björn at this bridge.

### GM Notes
Time this for when the party is tired and wants the session to wind down — the bridge should feel like relief before it feels like a trap. If they refuse to engage and somehow slip past, Björn finds them again in town, in front of witnesses, and makes it public. Leave the question of what Sebastián Castro is actually doing — and why he sold them out — unresolved for now.
