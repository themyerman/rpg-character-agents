## **The Apothecary Knows Your Name**
*Fires when the party drags a wounded or unconscious member into a settlement seeking aid — and the healer recognises them from a story they haven't told yet.*

### When It Fires
The party has limped into a town or waystation they've never visited, carrying someone who's down — broken ribs, a poisoned wound, a failed save still ticking. They've asked around and been pointed to the only competent healer for a day's ride: a quiet apothecary named Haile Tadesse, working out of a shopfront that smells of vinegar and crushed mint. He's already opening the door before they knock.

### What Happens
Haile waves them inside without asking questions, clears a workbench, and starts treatment with calm efficiency. Halfway through stitching or decanting, without looking up, he says: *"The bridge at Korlen's Ford — was that you, then? The one that came down on the tax-riders?"* He says it the way you'd ask about the weather. On the wall above his workbench is a tally-slate with three names chalked on it — and one of those names belongs to a party member. Underneath, in a different hand: **"two hundred, alive preferred."** Haile keeps working. He has not reached for a weapon. He has not called out. But he knew they were coming.

### The Party's Position
The party knows what they did at Korlen's Ford (or wherever their last memorable job was) — but they're three days' hard ride from there and told no one along the way. They do **not** yet know who chalked that bounty, who fed Haile the description, or whether the apothecary is reporting them in real time, stalling them, or warning them.

### Possible Responses
1. **Press him while the needle's still in.** Demand to know who's paying, who came through asking. Haile will talk — he's not loyal to whoever posted the bounty — but he wants something in exchange: a name of his own, a woman called Daniela Salinas who passed through six weeks ago heading north. He thinks the party might cross her trail.
2. **Finish the treatment and leave fast.** Possible, but the injured PC needs another day of rest to be useful, and Haile is the only person in town who can provide the follow-up draught. Leaving early means the wound reopens at the worst moment — GM's discretion when.
3. **Threaten or silence him.** He's unarmed, alone, and visibly unsurprised by the option. If they go this way, he says only: *"The other name on the slate is the one you should be worried about. He came in person."* Then waits to see what they do.

### Complications
- The bounty wasn't posted by anyone connected to Korlen's Ford. It was posted by **Ren Mixtli**, a fixer the party has never met, who is collecting names of people who've crossed a specific noble house — and the Ford job got one PC added to a list they didn't know existed. The bounty is a *recruitment tool*, not a death warrant. Ren wants to hire them.
- Haile's apprentice left out the back door ten minutes ago. The party will see her crossing the square as they leave — and she's heading for the coaching inn, where a rider is already saddling up.

### GM Notes
Time this for the session after a job the party felt clever about — the point is that cleverness has a half-life and word travels. If they ignore the bounty thread entirely, let Ren Mixtli's agents find them three sessions later in a much worse position. Leave the second chalked name unspoken for now; that's the one that matters.
