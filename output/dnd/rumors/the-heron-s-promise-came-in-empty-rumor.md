## **The *Heron's Promise* Came in Empty**
*A piece of dockside gossip about a riverboat that made an unusual stop two nights ago, now circulating among rival shippers and warehouse hands.*

### As Heard
> "Listen — the *Heron's Promise* tied up at the long pier Tuesday past midnight, and not one crate came off her. Not one. But she sat low when she left, lower than she came in, and Kwame Amponsah's boy was on the night watch and saw three covered wagons roll *onto* her gangplank from the customs house. Now you tell me what kind of cargo gets loaded *out* of a customs house at that hour, eh?"

*Source: Oisín MacLeod, a freight broker nursing his second ale at the Bent Anchor, telling this to anyone who'll listen because his own contract for the season went to the consortium that owns the* Heron's Promise *— and he wants people asking questions.*

### What's Actually True
The ship did arrive empty and leave loaded — that part is real, and Kwame's son did see the wagons. But the cargo wasn't contraband; it was bonded silver bullion being quietly relocated from the customs vault to a counting house downriver, on the orders of the harbor magistrate, who feared a run on local banks after a recent insolvency. The secrecy is legitimate — public knowledge of a bullion move would cause exactly the panic it's meant to prevent. Oisín doesn't *know* it's bullion. He just knows the consortium beat him on price and he wants them investigated for *anything*.

### The Danger
A party that bites on this and starts shadowing the *Heron's Promise* or breaking into the consortium's offices will find no smugglers — only a furious harbor magistrate named Vasily Petrov, who will have them detained for interfering with a sealed civic matter. Worse, if they make enough noise publicly, the bullion transfer becomes known, depositors panic, and two small lending houses collapse. People lose their savings. The party becomes the reason, even though they uncovered "the truth."

### Hooks
1. Oisín, drunker now, offers the party 50 gp to "just follow the ship one stop downriver and tell me what they unload." He has no authority to make this offer stick if things go wrong.
2. Kwame Amponsah approaches the party privately the next day — his son is now being "asked questions" by serious men in good coats, and he wants the boy out of the city until it blows over.
3. A nervous junior clerk from the customs house tries to hire the party as discreet escorts for "a routine ledger delivery" downriver — it's the same operation, second leg, and someone has noticed the gossip and wants muscle.

### GM Notes
Play Oisín as sympathetic but not honest — he believes his own framing, but he's a man with a grudge dressing it up as civic concern. Escalate by having Magistrate Petrov approach the party directly if they poke around: he can become an ally if handled well, an enemy if not. Keep ambiguous whether the consortium has *other* dealings worth investigating — this particular run is clean, but Oisín's instinct that they're not saints needn't be entirely wrong.
