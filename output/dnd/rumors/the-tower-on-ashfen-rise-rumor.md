## **The Tower on Ashfen Rise**
*A whispered accusation against a reclusive scholar, already past the point where reason can pull it back.*

### As Heard
> "Listen — three goats from my brother's pen, gone in a night, and not a wolf-print on the ground. Haruto's already been up to the tower twice with the Bjornsson boys, and the wizard wouldn't even open the door. *Wouldn't open the door,* you understand? Innocent men open doors. We're going back Thirdnight with torches, and I'd thank you not to repeat that."

*Source: A farmhand named Yonas Bjornsson, two cups deep at the Brindled Sow in Ashfen, who didn't realize the party was sitting close enough to hear. He's telling his cousin because he wants someone to know where he'll be if things go wrong.*

### What's Actually True
The wizard — Rayén Watanabe, a semi-retired transmuter studying soil chemistry, not flesh — has nothing to do with the missing livestock. A young chimera that crawled down out of the Ashfen crags six weeks ago has been picking off goats and sheep, dragging them whole up the scree where there are no prints to find. Rayén didn't open the door because she's deaf in one ear, was elbow-deep in a delicate alembic reaction, and assumed the pounding was the wind. The village has already held two meetings. A torch-and-pitchfork party is leaving in two nights. Haruto Park, the unofficial ringleader, has lost four goats himself and needs this to be the wizard, because the alternative is admitting he doesn't know what to do.

### The Danger
If the party rides up to "warn the wizard" or "investigate the tower," they arrive to find a confused scholar who will be genuinely grateful — and then the mob arrives the next night anyway, because Haruto isn't going to be talked out of this by outsiders. If the party joins the mob, they help murder an innocent woman whose patron (a minor noble in the next barony over) will absolutely notice. And while everyone is busy at the tower, the chimera takes another flock — possibly a child this time, since the herding rotation has been thrown off by the unrest.

### Hooks
1. Rayén, after being warned, asks the party to *prove* the real culprit exists before Thirdnight — she'll pay in potion components and a scroll, but she will not flee her own home and won't lower herself to address the village directly.
2. Haruto's younger sister approaches the party privately. She doesn't think the wizard did it either, but her brother won't listen to her, and she's terrified of what he'll do when the door finally breaks.
3. Tracking the missing livestock backward leads not to the tower but to a blood-streaked goat trail going *up* a cliff face no wolf could climb — and a cave that smells of three different animals at once.

### GM Notes
The clock is the point: two nights, a mob already committed, and a real monster still hunting. Let the party feel the pull of the obvious answer before the cliff-trail complicates it. Keep Rayén prickly rather than sympathetic — she shouldn't be easy to save, and whether Haruto can be redeemed (or only stopped) is the question to leave open.
