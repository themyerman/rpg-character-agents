### Description
Her figurehead is a kraken coiled around a sun, and somebody — three somebodies, in fact — has tried to gild it; the leaf is peeling in long curls that catch the wind and chime softly against the bowsprit. Below decks she smells of three different woods: original Lyrissan oak in the keel, cheaper pine in the patched midships, and a sharp resinous southern hardwood in the replaced stern that nobody can quite name. The gun deck is overbuilt for her tonnage — fourteen mounts on a hull that should carry ten — and the ports have been re-cut twice, leaving faint scars where older windows were planked over. Step into the captain's cabin and you notice immediately that the door has six different latches installed at six different heights, none of them currently engaged, all of them gouged at the strike plates.

### History
She was laid down fifteen years ago in a naval yard whose flag no longer flies, commissioned as a fast escort under **Captain Magnus Larsen**, a careful man who kept her for four uneventful years before a privateer squadron under **Vikram Patel** boarded her off the Tin Coast and took her as a prize. Patel renamed her *Sun's Knuckle* and used her for two seasons of raiding before he was found dead in his cabin — drowned, somehow, in his own washbasin with the door bolted from the inside. His quartermaster **Dasha Sokolov** assumed command and was herself relieved of it eight months later when a coalition cutter ran the ship down and pressed her into auxiliary service; Sokolov went over the side during the boarding and was never recovered.

The third taking was the strangest. **Captain Thyra Bergström** of the coalition crewed her for nearly six years and was, by all accounts, beloved aboard — and yet one morning off the Greyshoal she was simply gone from her cabin, the door open, her boots still under the bunk. The ship was sold at auction with her original name restored, the gilding redone on the figurehead, and a note in the bill of sale that the new owner was advised "not to sleep aboard alone." She was built to escort merchant convoys. She has become something else: a hull that outlasts the people who try to own her, and which the navies of three nations have quietly declined to repurchase.

### Quirks
- **The Cabin Door:** No latch, bar, wedge, or spell yet tried has kept the captain's cabin door shut through a full night. By the morning watch it is always standing open, just wide enough for a shoulder. The crew has stopped sleeping in there.
- **Three Woods, Three Voices:** She creaks in three distinct registers depending on which section of hull is flexing — a deep oak groan forward, a thin pine whine amidships, and a wet southern-hardwood pop in the stern that sounds, at certain angles of swell, exactly like a knock.
- **Peeling Gold:** The kraken figurehead sheds gold leaf in any strong wind, and the flakes blow back along the deck. Old hands collect them in a tin nailed by the mainmast; nobody can remember who started the tin, or why nobody throws the flakes overboard.
- **Overgunned:** With fourteen mounts on a hull her size, every broadside shakes loose something — a belaying pin, a cup, a tooth, once a man's wedding ring. She can hit harder than ships twice her displacement, and she will rattle herself apart doing it.
- **The Manifest Drawer:** The bottom-left drawer of the captain's desk contains a ship's manifest in a handwriting nobody aboard recognizes, dated next month, listing cargo the ship is not carrying. Replace the page and a new one appears within a week.

### Crew Roles
- **Sailing Master** — Runs the deck in practice, because no captain stays long enough to learn her. The current Master is expected to brief any new captain on "the door" within the first hour aboard.
- **Master Gunner** — Not optional on a hull this overgunned. Must know which mounts to fire in sequence to keep the frame from racking, and which three to never fire together.
- **Carpenter** — Required to be fluent in all three hull woods and to keep a running inventory of which sections are due for what. The current carpenter keeps a private ledger of every plank's origin.
- **Night Watch (doubled)** — She runs two hands on the night watch instead of one, always within sight of each other, never near the captain's cabin between the middle and morning bells.
- **Ship's Recorder** — An unusual position: someone whose job is specifically to copy out the strange manifest each week and file the copies ashore. The last three Recorders quit.

### Current Situation
She lies at anchor in a working harbor with half her berths empty, her current owner trying to make her last voyage's books balance with a crew of nine when she needs at least twelve to handle weather. Six hands walked off at the last port without collecting their pay — a thing sailors do not do — and the owner is asking no questions about why, only advertising for replacements at a wage twenty percent above scale. She is for sale, quietly, to anyone who'll take her at a discount and ask nothing; she is for hire, loudly, to anyone who needs fourteen guns under one keel and won't read the small print. In her hold, alongside legitimate cargo of salt and milled cloth, is a sealed crate the owner does not remember accepting and cannot find a bill of lading for.

### GM Notes
*Every captain who has tried to own her outright — Patel, Sokolov, Bergström — has been removed by something that comes through the cabin door at night. It is not a ghost in the sentimental sense; it is whatever the ship became when her original commission was broken and she was taken as a prize the first time, and it does not tolerate the word "mine." A captain who treats her as a charge, a command, a temporary trust — Larsen, Bergström for nearly six years — is left alone, sometimes for years. The moment a captain signs papers of personal ownership, or speaks the possessive aloud in her cabin, the door begins to open at night, and the manifest in the drawer starts listing them as cargo. The PCs will figure this out the first time one of them buys her.*
