# The Sealed Letter: D&D 5e — Strangers with a Shared Secret
*Three forgers circling the same crew, each convinced they're the only one with a sibling held hostage and a boss to betray — and only one of them is right.*

---

## **Chanya Srisawat**
*Half-Elf Forger & Document Thief — laughs too loud at her own jokes, eyes never leaving the door.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **DEX** | 16 (+3) |
| **CHA** | 15 (+2) |
| **WIS** | 11 (+0) |

**Demeanor:** Disarmingly cheerful — buys the first round, remembers everyone's drink, drops self-deprecating jokes about her crooked left pinky (broken in a job gone wrong, never set right). Her hands are always doing something: shuffling cards, folding napkin cranes, palming small objects you didn't notice her take.
**Wants:** A clean look at the boss's private ledger — she thinks her name is in it, and not in a good way.
**Secret:** She's *not* the double agent — but she's been quietly forging exit papers for herself under three different aliases. She plans to vanish the moment things turn sideways, and she'll leave the others holding the bag without losing a wink of sleep.
**Hook:** Approaches the party in a tavern with a "harmless favor" — deliver a sealed letter to a specific table across the room. The letter is bait; she wants to see who reads it and who reports it. Whoever flinches is the rat.
**Connection:** Her younger brother **Veasna**, a temple scribe back home who thinks she's a traveling merchant. She sends him money every month and has never once told him the truth. If he ever learned what she does, she thinks it would kill him — and that fear is the only leash she still wears.

## **Yasmin Nouri**
*Half-Elf forger and document-thief — wears too much perfume, watches everyone's hands, never the first to speak.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **CHA** | 16 (+3) |
| **DEX** | 15 (+2) |
| **INT** | 12 (+1) |
| 

**Demeanor:** Polite to the point of opacity. Smiles with her mouth, not her eyes. She'll pour your drink before you ask, then sit with her back to a wall and count exits while you talk about the weather.
**Wants:** A clean copy of the boss's personal seal — she's been trying to lift an impression for three weeks and hasn't gotten close enough.
**Secret:** She *is* the double agent — but only barely. A magistrate in another city is holding her younger brother on a fabricated debt-charge, and feeding the crown's investigators just enough scraps to keep him eating. She hasn't given up anything truly damaging. Yet.
**Hook:** Approaches the party offering to forge papers, travel writs, or a noble pedigree — cheap, fast, no questions. She's fishing: she wants to know if any of them are informants too, so she can finally talk to someone.
**Connection:** Her brother **Faraj**, seventeen, locked in a cell two hundred miles away. Every letter he sends is a knife she carries in her ribs. She'd burn the whole crew to get him out — and she knows it, and it's eating her alive.

## **Sumak Ñanculef**
*Half-Orc Locksmith-turned-Thief — Talks too much when nervous, which is always, and somehow still nobody knows anything real about them.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **DEX** | 14 (+2) |
| **INT** | 14 (+2) |
| **WIS** | 7 (−2) |

**Demeanor:** Wiry, ink-stained fingers that never stop moving — picking at cuticles, tapping table edges, dismantling a pen mid-conversation. Laughs a beat too early at jokes and fills every silence with rambling stories about a cousin who may not exist.
**Wants:** A clean look at the boss's private ledger — they're convinced the numbers don't add up and someone in the crew is skimming for an outside buyer.
**Secret:** They actually *are* the double agent the others suspect exists — but they didn't volunteer. A city magistrate has their younger sibling on a forgery charge and feeds them just enough scraps to keep the leash tight. They genuinely like the crew now, which is making everything worse.
**Hook:** Corners one of the PCs alone and, in a torrent of nervous over-explanation, asks them to "hypothetically" deliver a sealed letter to an address in the merchant quarter — no questions, double pay, please don't read it.
**Connection:** Their sibling **Tepin**, seventeen, rotting in a holding cell across town. Every report Sumak files buys Tepin another two weeks before sentencing. Sumak hasn't been allowed to visit in four months and isn't sure they're still alive.

## Web of Connections
- **Chanya / Yasmin:** They've passed each other twice in the same safehouse stairwell and each clocked the other as "competition" — neither realizes they're forging escape papers in the exact same hand because they learned from the same Vientesi master, two years apart.
- **Chanya / Sumak:** Chanya once bought Sumak a drink to pump them for information about the boss's ledger; Sumak rambled for forty minutes about a fictional cousin and Chanya left certain they were harmless, which is the most dangerous mistake she's currently making.
- **Yasmin / Sumak:** They've never spoken, but both have filed the same magistrate's name in coded reports — Yasmin would recognize Sumak's handwriting instantly if she ever saw it, because they were both trained to forge it.

## GM Hooks
1. The party is hired (by Chanya) to deliver a sealed letter across a tavern; the recipient (Yasmin) opens it, goes pale, and slips out the back door — and a third patron (Sumak) bolts after her, dropping a half-disassembled pen on the floor with a wax-pressed seal hidden inside the barrel.
2. A magistrate's courier is ambushed on the road outside town carrying three separate sealed dispatches — each one a report to the crown about the same crew, each in a different hand, each mentioning a younger sibling by name. The party finds the bodies before the crew does.
3. The boss announces at the next crew meeting that she knows there's a rat, she's identified them, and tomorrow at dawn she'll name them in front of everyone — the party has one night, and three nervous strangers all try to hire them privately before the sun comes up.
