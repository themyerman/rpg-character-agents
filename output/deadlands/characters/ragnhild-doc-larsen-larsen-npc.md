## **Ragnhild "Doc Larsen" Larsen**
*Traveling physician working the Dakota mining camps — a Norwegian-born surgeon who left a Chicago hospital under circumstances she won't discuss, and now squints her way through bullet extractions by lamplight.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Alignment** | Conflicted |
| **Location** | Wherever the camps need her — currently a tent practice outside Deadwood, moves every six to eight weeks |

**Appearance:** Tall and broad-shouldered, with pale hair cut shorter than fashion permits and tied back with surgical thread. Her left sleeve is always rolled higher than her right — underneath, a long, careful scar runs from wrist to elbow, the stitches done by her own hand, in the dark, in a hurry.

**Demeanor:** Direct to the point of rudeness, no bedside manner to speak of, asks the same question three times because she didn't quite catch your face the first two. Patients mistake her bluntness for cruelty until they realize she's the only person in three counties who will work on a Chinese laborer or a Lakota child without raising her price.

**Wants:** A pair of properly ground spectacles from a Denver optician — she has the money set aside, but every time she's ready to travel for them, someone else needs cutting open first.

**Secret:** In Chicago she watched a colleague — a man she trusted — perform a "procedure" on a patient who got back up off the table changed. Wrong. She killed him with a scalpel and ran. She suspects what she saw wasn't unique, and every Harrowed she's quietly identified in the camps is another notch in a list she keeps in code in the margins of her medical text. She doesn't know yet what she'll do when the list gets long enough.

**Hook:** A posse member needs patching up after a bad job. While she works, she squints at one of their faces a beat too long, asks an oddly specific question about whether they've ever "died and felt better afterward," and goes very quiet. Later, she'll find them privately — either to warn them, recruit them, or, depending on what they are, something worse.

**Equipment:** A scalpel in a wrist sheath she draws faster than most men draw pistols; a locked laudanum tin whose combination she changes weekly (she has never taken a drop herself — it's for patients, and she counts every bottle); a heavily annotated copy of *Gray's Anatomy* whose margin notes contradict the text in three languages; and a small, lopsided carved wooden bear that a Lakota boy gave her after she saved his sister — she keeps it in her breast pocket and won't say why.

**Connection:** She trades letters — slowly, by way of a freight clerk in Cheyenne — with **Dr. Halvor Brandt**, an old mentor in St. Paul who taught her surgery and who she believes is one of the few people who would believe what she saw in Chicago. Every letter she sends is a risk: if the wrong eyes read it, the man who came back off the table has friends, and they will know exactly where she's working.
