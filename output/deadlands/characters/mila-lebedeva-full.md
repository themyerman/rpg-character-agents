### Edges
- **Arcane Background (Blessed):** Mila prays in Old Church Slavonic under her breath; the answers come in Russian, in her grandmother's voice, in a language older than either. She does not tell anyone this.
- **Healer:** She has pulled babies out of half-frozen sod houses and dug bullets out of men who cursed her the whole time. Her hands do not shake.
- **Holy Warrior:** When she lays her hand on the iron of her staff and speaks the Name, the thing that crawls back into the dark is not bothered by laudanum or lead. It is bothered by *her*.

### Hindrances
- **Vengeful** (Major): There is a circuit preacher named Obadiah Sloan who came through her settlement in '73 with a wagon full of "healing tonic" and a smile like a split log. People drank it. People died — including her husband Pavel, who drank it to please a guest. She has been three years on Sloan's trail. She does not pray for him.
- **Habit** (Minor): A short black cheroot, smoked down to nothing before she can hear God clearly. She rolls them herself, badly. When the tin is empty, her sermons get sharper and her hands less so.

### Equipment
- **Walking staff, heavy and iron-shod** — the Apostles did not wait for firearms. The shaft is birch, scorched dark at the grip where her hands have lived for three years. The iron cap is dented in a shape that, if you look closely, is roughly the curve of a jaw.
- **Simple clothes of good quality if examined closely** — a black wool dress mended at the elbows in stitches so fine they look like prayer. A man's coat over it, too big for her, that smells faintly of pipe tobacco she does not smoke.
- **Medic's kit:** clean linen bandages rolled tight, a brown bottle of carbolic, a brass needle in a wooden case, gut thread, and laudanum in a small locked compartment she has never once opened for herself.
- **A small carved figure** — a man, or meant to be one. The proportions are wrong and one arm is shorter than the other. Pavel was learning to whittle the winter before he died. He never finished it. She did not finish it for him.

### Personality
- **Trait:** She listens longer than is comfortable before she speaks. When she finally does, people stop talking.
- **Ideal:** That mercy is a discipline, not a feeling — and that she can practice it on everyone in the world except one man.
- **Bond:** Pavel's grave outside the burned settlement at Three Pines, marked with a wooden cross she carved herself. She has not been back. She does not trust what she would do if she stood there again.
- **Flaw:** She knows the hunt for Sloan is poisoning her gift. She feels the Miracles come slower now, harder, like calling through deeper water. She does not stop.

### Connections
- **Ally — Sister Agnes Two Crows:** A Lakota convert and former mission-school teacher who runs a way-station for refugees north of Cheyenne. She taught Mila that the Lord's English is not the only English, and that some prayers work better in the language of the land they're spoken on. Agnes knows about Sloan. Agnes worries.
- **Enemy — Reverend Obadiah Sloan:** The tonic-peddler who killed her husband and a dozen others. He's still moving west, still preaching, and the tonic has gotten *worse* — Mila has begun to suspect that whatever's in those bottles isn't merely poison anymore. Something is feeding him the recipe.

### Backstory

Mila Andreevna Lebedeva was born in 1841 in a village of Old Believers in the Altai foothills, the kind of community that had been hiding from one Tsar or another for two hundred years. She learned midwifery from her grandmother and prayer from her mother, and the two were not separate things. When the conscription notices came again in '67, she and Pavel — already married five years, already grieving two children who hadn't taken root — sold what they had and went east, then south, then across the ocean. They landed in San Francisco speaking no English and reached the Wyoming Territory two years later because someone told Pavel the land was good. The land was not good. It was beautiful, and it was hard, and they loved it.

The Reverend Obadiah Sloan rode into Three Pines in the autumn of 1873 with a painted wagon and a bottle that he swore on his mother's grave would cure consumption, gout, melancholy, and the bite of any serpent. Pavel bought one to be polite — they had guests, and Sloan had been invited to supper. Within a week eleven people were dead, including Pavel, who died in their bed begging her in Russian not to follow him. She buried him in the morning. By noon she had walked out to the meadow where her grandmother had taught her to pray, and she had asked, not for the first time but for the first time *like that*, for an answer. The answer came. It was not comforting. It was a heat in her palms and a clarity in her eyes and a knowing that the tonic in Sloan's bottles was not, had never been, simply poison — something had been *in* it, watching, the way a man watches a trap. Something had been pleased.

She has been moving since. She delivers babies in line shacks and pulls fever out of children in mining camps and reads scripture over graves no one else will stand near. People remember her — the small Russian woman with the iron staff who would not take payment but would take a name, a direction, a description of a tall preacher with a gold tooth. She is in Deadwood now, in the spring of 1876, because three weeks ago a Chinese laundress in Cheyenne told her a man matching Sloan's description had come through buying bulk laudanum and asking after a place called Gomorra. Mila does not know what Gomorra is. She has a feeling she will.
