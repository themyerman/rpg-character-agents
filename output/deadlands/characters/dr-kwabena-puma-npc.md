## **Dr. Kwabena Puma**
*Circuit doctor of the high desert — a physician who rides between mining camps no one else will treat, and who has stopped pretending all his patients are still strictly alive.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Alignment** | Conflicted |
| **Location** | The road between Cerrillos, Madrid, and three smaller camps in the Ortiz Mountains — wherever the wagon breaks down that week |

**Appearance:** Mid-forties, lean from the work, wears a once-fine Boston frock coat that's been patched at the elbows with sailcloth. His left hand is missing the last two fingers, and the stumps are perfectly, surgically clean — he did the work himself, and he had time to do it right.

**Demeanor:** Patient in the way exhausted people are patient. He listens longer than he needs to, asks the same question two different ways, and writes nothing down in front of you. When he disagrees with you he does not argue — he simply proceeds as if you hadn't spoken, because he is right and you will see that shortly.

**Wants:** To finish the circuit before the first snow. To get a shipment of carbolic acid and clean linen out of Santa Fe that hasn't been picked over by the Agency. To sleep eight hours in a bed that isn't moving.

**Secret:** Three months ago, in a played-out shaft outside Madrid, he treated a miner named Elias who'd been crushed by a fall — pelvis pulped, no breath, no pulse, eyes already gone gray. Elias sat up an hour later and asked for water. Kwabena has been visiting him every two weeks since, taking notes he hides inside the lining of his bag, because Elias is *getting better* in ways a body should not. Kwabena is no longer sure whether he is studying a miracle, a monster, or his own complicity.

**Hook:** One of the PCs (or someone they care about) takes a wound that should kill them and doesn't — or takes one that *will* kill them unless Kwabena agrees to try the thing he swore he'd never try twice. He will want something in return: an escort to Madrid, and someone who will not flinch at what they see in that shaft.

**Equipment:** A scalpel in a wrist sheath — surprisingly effective and deeply unsettling; surgical tools in a padded case, kept clean regardless of conditions; a medical bag stocked to professional standards, with field additions the medical college would not have approved (a vial of something dark, labeled only with a date); a small carved wooden figure of a bear, rough, clearly made by someone — a child, maybe — who was trying hard.

**Connection:** Sister Inocencia Vargas at the mission hospital in Santa Fe trained alongside him in Philadelphia and is the only person who knows about Elias. She has not reported him to the Bishop. She has also not answered his last two letters, and he is beginning to wonder if the silence is the answer.
