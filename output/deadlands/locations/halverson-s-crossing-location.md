## **Halverson's Crossing**
*A cattle-trail waystation on the Goodnight-Loving spur where the marshal answers to the man who owns the stockyard, and everyone pretends not to notice.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Cattle trail waystation — corral, scales, saloon, jail, one boarding house |
| **Condition** | A new wanted poster on the saloon wall describes a local exactly |

### Atmosphere
The crossing smells of cattle sweat, creosote, and the iron tang of the brand-fires that never seem to go cold, even at midnight. Drovers drink quietly — too quietly — at the long bar of the Bull & Bell, and the piano player keeps his eyes on the keys like the keys might bolt. The wanted poster is tacked dead-center on the saloon's notice board, fresh enough that the paste is still tacky, and three different men have walked past it tonight without once turning their heads.

### Notable NPCs
- **Marshal Gunnar Björnsson** (town law, badge legitimately deputized out of Tascosa) — Wants to keep his job, his pension, and his teeth; takes a monthly envelope from the stockyard and tells himself it's a "retainer" because the alternative is admitting what he is.
- **Maryam Al-Hassan** (boarding house proprietor, widow, formerly something else entirely) — The poster describes her down to the small scar above her left eyebrow and the false name she's used for eleven years; she's been kind to the party already, fed them stew, asked no questions.
- **Aynuk Lican** (trail cook for the Bar-Slash-Bar outfit, knows everyone's business) — Will sell information for coin or coffee, saw who tacked the poster up, and is deciding right now whether telling the party makes him richer or deader.

### The Situation
Halverson's Crossing runs on a quiet arrangement: Old Man Halverson owns the stockyard, the scales, and effectively the marshal, and in return he keeps the trail drives moving and the killings down to a manageable number. Into this equilibrium has come a wanted poster — $1,200, dead or alive, for a woman who fled a Pinkerton-protected railroad payroll job in Missouri a decade ago. The description is Maryam's, exact, and the poster wasn't posted by a bounty hunter. It was posted by Halverson himself, because Maryam refused to sell him her boarding house, and he's giving her one quiet night to reconsider before the marshal "notices" the poster in the morning. The party knows Maryam — from somewhere, somewhen — and she has perhaps eight hours before legitimate law, doing an illegitimate favor, comes through her door.

### Hooks
1. The party walks into the Bull & Bell to wet the trail dust and the bartender slides their drinks over with a folded paper underneath one glass: *"Maryam says come to the back porch after the piano stops. She knows you saw it."*
2. Aynuk corners a party member at the chuckwagon, offers to name who pinned the poster up for ten dollars — and mentions, free of charge, that the marshal's been drinking more than usual tonight.
3. Halverson himself invites the party to breakfast at his ranch house, polite as Sunday, and offers them $400 to leave the crossing by sundown and forget any faces they've seen. He does not say whose.

### GM Notes
Run Björnsson as a tired man, not a villain — he wants the party to make this easy by leaving, and he'll telegraph that mercy if they let him. Escalate by having Halverson's hired guns (three drovers on his personal payroll, plus a Texas Ranger he's been cultivating) arrive on the morning stage if the party stalls past dawn. Keep ambiguous what Maryam actually did in Missouri — the poster says "murder and robbery," she'll say "robbery, and the murder was theirs," and the truth should stay just out of reach until the party has already chosen a side.
