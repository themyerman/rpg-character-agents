## **The Better Offer Walks In Mid-Heist**
*Fires when the crew is already committed to executing a hand-me-down job and a rival broker arrives with a job that would pay better — if they betray the first.*

### When It Fires
The crew took this job secondhand from Galya Bogdanov, a fixer who couldn't run it herself and passed it down with a finder's fee already skimmed. They're in the middle of executing — say, mid-extraction of a bonded courier's data slate from a freight transfer station in the Procyon belt. Galya's name is on the line with whoever originally hired her; the crew's name is on the line with Galya. Then a private channel chimes on the crew's comms.

### What Happens
Citlali Uxmal — a Hegemonic intelligence cutout running as an independent broker — opens a tight-beam to the crew's ship. She knows exactly where they are and exactly what they're doing. She offers triple the payout plus a clean exit codepath through the next two checkpoints if they deliver that same data slate to her instead of Galya's principal. She gives them six minutes to decide. The kicker: she names the courier by their true name (not the cover identity Galya provided) and reveals the slate contains evidence of a Hegemonic officer skimming from a Stegos slush fund. Galya either doesn't know this, or didn't tell them.

### The Party's Position
The crew knows the original job's parameters and Galya's fee structure. They don't know whether Citlali's intel is real, whether this is a Hegemonic loyalty test, or what Galya's principal will do to people who burn them — and they don't know how Citlali found them mid-job.

### Possible Responses
1. **Take Citlali's offer.** Finish the grab, change destination, collect the better payday — and inherit a permanent enemy in Galya and whoever she answers to. Start a 6-clock: "Galya Finds Out Who Crossed Her."
2. **Hold the line with Galya.** Refuse Citlali, deliver as agreed, and now an active Hegemonic asset knows their faces, their ship, and their exact operational pattern. Roll to see if Citlali burns them anyway out of spite — Resolve or Insight to read her, Sway to talk her down.
3. **Play both sides.** Stall Citlali, finish the job for Galya, then sell Citlali a *copy* of the slate or fabricated intel. High-stakes Finesse or Consort work; reward is huge, failure means two factions hunting them simultaneously.

### Complications
- **Galya is already compromised.** Mid-decision, the crew's contact channel to Galya goes dark. She's either been picked up, killed, or has run — meaning the original payout may evaporate regardless of what the crew chooses.
- **The courier is awake.** Agus Suriyawong, the bonded courier, isn't an NPC asset — he's a person, and he's listened to the entire offer through his earpiece. He has his own opinion about who should get the slate, and he has a panic transponder he hasn't triggered yet. He wants to negotiate.

### GM Notes
Run the six-minute timer in real conversation time at the table if you can — let the pressure breathe. If the crew tries to ignore the event and just complete the original job, Citlali sells their location to a third party on the way out. Leave unresolved: *who* told Citlali the crew would be here, and at what cost.
