## **The Wrong Hand on the Other End**
*Fires the moment the crew is laying low post-job, when adding more heat would tip them into a faction's active sights — and forces them to choose between cutting losses, doubling down, or burning a contact.*

### When It Fires
The crew is hours out from completing the job — payment received, the package handed off, the ship hidden in a sublevel hangar or a kelp-choked tidal dock. Heat is already at 5+; one more incident and a Hegemonic patrol, an Ulixes enforcer, or a local syndicate gets a real lead. The pilot is scrubbing transponder records, the muscle is sleeping it off, and someone is finally counting the take when the encrypted comm chirps.

### What Happens
**Yuri Desta**, the fixer who lined up the job, comes through on a burner channel — voice tight, no preamble. The buyer the crew met, the one who took the package and paid in clean Hegemonic scrip, was not the client. The real client, **Arnar Stefánsson**, just arrived at the rendezvous two hours late with a legitimate counter-sign and is, in Yuri's words, "asking polite questions that won't stay polite." The package — whatever it was, a data-spike, a person in cryo, a piece of pre-Hegemonic tech — is now in the hands of someone who specifically arranged to intercept it. Yuri thinks it was **Aditya Joshi**, an Ulixes cell-runner working the same sector, but he isn't sure. He needs an answer in the next ten minutes: does the crew try to recover it, or does he tell Stefánsson the job went sideways and the crew is willing to refund?

### The Party's Position
They know they completed the handoff cleanly and were paid in full — which now reads as suspicious, because the impostor *wanted* them gone fast. They don't know who the impostor really worked for, where the package is now, or whether Stefánsson is the kind of client who accepts a refund or the kind who puts a bounty on the crew's transponder by morning.

### Possible Responses
1. **Hunt the impostor before the trail cools.** Burn favours to track Joshi (or whoever it was) through the district — every lead generates heat, and Ulixes does not like being chased. Leads to a fast, dirty recovery op with the wrong faction watching.
2. **Negotiate directly with Stefánsson.** Cut Yuri out, meet the real client face-to-face, offer to recover the package *for free* in exchange for a clean slate. Risks: Stefánsson may already suspect the crew was complicit, and the meet itself draws eyes.
3. **Refund and vanish.** Give back the scrip, eat the loss, let Yuri smooth it over. Cheapest in coin, expensive in reputation — and word that the crew can be intercepted will travel.

### Complications
- The "payment" the crew received is marked. Not counterfeit — *traceable*. Whoever set this up wanted the crew to spend it, get caught spending it, and take the fall for the theft from Stefánsson. The longer they hold it, the worse it gets; the moment they try to launder it, a new clock starts.
- Stefánsson isn't a private buyer. He's a broker for a Hegemonic Office of Antiquities black-bag program, and the package contained something the Hegemony officially does not admit exists. He cannot afford a public investigation — which makes him simultaneously the crew's best protector and most dangerous loose end.

### GM Notes
Run the ten-minute window in real time at the table if you can — let the players feel Yuri waiting on the line. If they ignore it or stall, Yuri makes the call for them and tells Stefánsson the crew will recover the package; the clock starts whether they agreed or not. Leave the identity of the impostor's true employer unresolved — Joshi is the obvious suspect, but the marked scrip suggests someone with deeper pockets and a longer game, and that's a thread for a later session.
