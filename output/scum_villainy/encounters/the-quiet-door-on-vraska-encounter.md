## The Quiet Door on Vraska

*A child in a grey smock opens the door before you knock, and behind her the room smells like cardamom, old paper, and ozone — the smell of something that prays and something that calculates, sharing a kitchen.*

### Setting
The outer sanctum of the Threadkeepers occupies three converted shop-floors above a noodle bar in Vraska's Sixth Quarter — a Hegemony industrial moon where the rain falls warm and tastes of refinery sulphur. The rooms are scrubbed clean but everything is second-hand: mismatched cushions, a teapot with a hairline crack, devotional charts pinned over older devotional charts. What's wrong is the silence under the silence — no one here speaks above a murmur, and every door has been very recently oiled.

### Scene
You are seated on cushions and given tea you did not ask for. A wiry, sun-dark woman with white braids introduces herself as Kāhu Parata and tells you, without preamble, that she needs passage off Vraska aboard your ship within the next thirty-six hours. She will not say where. She will not say why. She says only: *it matters, and I would not be asking strangers if there were any other way.* A clerkish man in a damp Hegemony Customs uniform sits two cushions over, sipping his tea and pretending he isn't listening.

### Situation
Kāhu is a Mystic of the Threadkeepers — a small, quietist sect that believes certain "knots" in causality must be physically witnessed at the right place and time or they fray and cause cascading misfortune. She has calculated (the sect would say *received*) that something is going to happen on a rimward moon called Hesh in roughly nine days, and she is the only Threadkeeper in this hemisphere with the training to be there for it. The other two who could have gone are dead in the last month — one by accident, one not. The clerk, Auditor Sigrid Bergström, is here because the Threadkeepers' charitable license is up for review and she is doing a "voluntary compliance interview"; she is also, quietly, sympathetic — her late mother kept a Threadkeeper charm — but she has a quota and a supervisor and an uncle in Ministry who has been asking pointed questions about her loyalties. If Kāhu vanishes off-world while under interview, Bergström will be held personally responsible. If the crew refuses, Kāhu will try to stow away on the next bulk freighter and almost certainly be caught.

### Key NPCs
- **Kāhu Parata** — Threadkeeper Mystic, late fifties. She presents as exhausted, courteous, and entirely unwilling to negotiate on the one thing she came to ask. What she's hiding: she does not actually know *what* will happen on Hesh, only that the absence of a witness will be catastrophic in ways she cannot articulate to a non-initiate, and she suspects — but will not say — that whatever killed her two colleagues is also trying to prevent her from arriving.
- **Auditor Sigrid Bergström** — Hegemony Customs & Charitable Compliance, mid-thirties. She presents as bored, professional, faintly apologetic about the intrusion, the kind of bureaucrat who actually reads the forms. What she wants: to file a clean report and go home; what she's hiding is that she has already noticed the discrepancies in the Threadkeepers' membership rolls (two names quietly removed) and is choosing, for now, not to write them down.
- **Amara Putra** — the child who opened the door, eleven years old, sect-raised. She presents as preternaturally calm and serves tea like a small adult. What she wants: to go with Kāhu, because Kāhu is the closest thing she has to family, and she has already packed a bag hidden behind the rice sacks.
- **Rangi Te Aho** — the sanctum's lay caretaker, a former dock-loader with bad knees. He presents as gruff and unhelpful, vetting strangers. What he's hiding: he's been approached twice this week by someone offering money for Kāhu's travel plans, and he hasn't told her because he's still deciding whether the money would save the sanctum.

### Complication
Auditor Bergström is in the room. Any deal the crew strikes with Kāhu happens under the eyes of a Hegemony official who has the authority to flag *The Arrangement* for inspection on departure — and who will be ruined if her interview subject disappears. She is not corrupt, exactly; she is decent and trapped, which is worse, because corrupt officials can be bought clean. Whatever the crew does, Bergström either has to be brought into the lie (compromising a woman who has done nothing wrong and putting a Hegemony asset in their debt), worked around (leaving her to take the fall), or somehow convinced that what she saw was something else. And Rangi, watching all of this from the doorway, is doing arithmetic in his head about how much the informant's money was, and whether anyone would blame him.

### Possible Outcomes
1. **The Third Cup** — The crew brings Bergström into it, in some partial or full way. She gets aboard *The Arrangement* as "escorting a compliance subject to a regional office," which is technically not a lie for about eleven hours. The crew now has a Hegemony auditor who owes them and knows their faces, which is an asset and a noose in the same package.
2. **The Quiet Departure** — Kāhu is smuggled out clean, Bergström files an honest report, and the auditor's career ends in a windowless office on a colder moon. The Threadkeepers survive; one decent Hegemony servant does not; the crew will hear Bergström's name again, probably from someone wearing her old uniform and looking for them.
3. **The Frayed Knot** — Something — Rangi's buyer, a rival sect, a Hegemony interdiction the crew didn't see coming — intervenes before departure. Kāhu does not reach Hesh in time, and over the following weeks the crew begins to notice small, ugly coincidences clustering around them: a contact's heart attack, a job gone sideways for no reason, a dream they all share on the same night.

### Hooks
Who paid Rangi for Kāhu's travel plans, and why is someone systematically removing Threadkeepers from the rimward hemisphere? And if Kāhu does reach Hesh — what is the crew owed by a woman who claims to have prevented a disaster no one else can see?

### GM Notes
*The truth held back: the "knot" on Hesh is the scheduled, unannounced testing of a Hegemony resonance-array prototype that interacts badly with Ur-substrate buried under the moon's crust. The Threadkeepers don't know the technical details — they only know that being present, witnessing, and performing a specific small ritual collapses a probability cascade that would otherwise wake something. The party who wants Kāhu dead is not the Hegemony; it is a faction within the Cult of the Forgotten Gods who very much wants that thing to wake up. Mechanical tripwire: if the crew offers Kāhu a hard bargain (coin up front, no questions), she will agree to anything — she is desperate — and the table should feel that desperation as pressure, not as leverage. Charging her market rate is a Devil's Bargain the crew is making with themselves.*
