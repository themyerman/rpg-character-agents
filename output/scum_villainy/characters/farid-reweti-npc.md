## **Farid Reweti**
*Dockside arbiter for the Stevedores' Hand — settles disputes with a quiet word, a fixed match, or a broken wrist, depending on the asker.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Playbook** | Speaker (with Muscle tendencies) |
| **Heritage** | Sah'iyan |
| **Background** | Labor |
| **Vice** | Gambling / the back-room *bone-court* fights beneath Pier 14, Wharfside |

**Key Actions:** Sway (d8) — he reads a room before he opens his mouth. Consort (d8) — every crew foreman on three stations owes him a favor or a debt. Skirmish (d8) — twenty years of cargo-hauling and longer than that practicing the *thekha*, his grandmother's fighting forms.

**Demeanor:** Soft-spoken, deliberate, with cargo-hook scars laddering both forearms he doesn't bother to hide. Listens longer than feels comfortable, then says exactly one sentence that ends the argument.
**Wants:** Forty thousand creds, fast and quiet, to cover a bone-court marker before the holder sends collectors who'll start with his sister.
**Secret:** He threw the last sanctioned dockworkers' strike vote — took Hegemony money to do it. Three of his friends died in the lockout that followed and he tells himself he didn't know.
**Hook:** He'll offer the crew a "simple" job: intimidate a shipping clerk into losing a manifest. The clerk is actually the bagman for the loan he can't pay, and Farid wants the books to disappear with him.
**Connection:** His younger sister **Pita Reweti**, who runs a legitimate medical clinic on the lower decks and still thinks he's the honest one in the family. He would burn the station down before he'd let her find out otherwise.
