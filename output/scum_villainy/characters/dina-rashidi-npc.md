## **Dina Rashidi**
*Disgraced xenolinguist turned forger of antiquities — she can read languages the Hegemony pretends never existed, and she'll sell you the wrong translation if the price is right.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Playbook** | Speaker (with Scoundrel lean) |
| **Heritage** | Sah'iyan |
| **Background** | Academic |
| **Vice** | Obligation / monthly tribute carried by courier to her grandmother's clan-keeper on Sah'iya |

**Key Actions:** Consort (d8) — she still has friends in three university faculties who owe her favors. Study (d8) — give her an hour with a fragment and she'll tell you who made it, when, and what they were lying about. Sway (d8) — academics, fences, and lonely bureaucrats all answer her letters.

**Demeanor:** Quiet, precise, slightly stooped from years over manuscripts; speaks five languages but pretends to struggle with two of them so people underestimate her. Wears a single brass earring shaped like a Sah'iyan oath-knot — never takes it off.
**Wants:** Three hundred credits within the week to make this month's tribute, or her grandmother loses standing and Dina loses the only family that still speaks her name.
**Secret:** She's the one who authenticated the "Yajan Codex" that sold to a Hegemony collector for half a million — and she wrote it herself, six months ago, in a rented room above a noodle shop.
**Hook:** She approaches the crew with a job: a rival forger is about to expose her work to the buyer. She needs the rival's archive stolen and burned — or the rival convinced, by any means, to stay quiet.
**Connection:** Her grandmother **Yelet Rashidi**, clan-keeper of a dwindling Sah'iyan house. Dina was exiled from the clan for taking Hegemony schooling; the tribute is the only thread left, and Yelet has never once written back.
