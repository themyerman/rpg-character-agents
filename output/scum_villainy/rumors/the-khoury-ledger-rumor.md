## **The Khoury Ledger**
*A rumor about a Procyon-system fixer who stopped accepting credits and started accepting favors — and how those favors keep escalating.*

### As Heard
> "You know Sana Khoury? Used to run loans out of the Lower Spindle. Won't take a single bit anymore — only favors. Started small, a delivery here, a name there. Now she's having people walk Cobalt customs officers off their shifts. *Whole shifts.* And nobody who owes her has refused yet, because — listen — nobody knows what she's collecting *toward*."

*Source: A drive-tech named Vanya Ivanov, drinking alone in a portside bar on Cantos Reach, voice dropped to nothing. He owes Khoury for a kidney trace his sister needed, and he's telling you because he wants someone — anyone — to ask questions he can't.*

### What's Actually True
Sana Khoury is real, and the favor-economy is real, but she isn't the one orchestrating it. Aoi Suzuki, a mid-tier Cartel intelligence broker, **planted this rumor** three weeks ago through six separate mouths. Suzuki has been quietly buying Khoury's debt-book from Khoury herself for months — Khoury is dying, slowly, of a neural prion she can't afford to treat. Suzuki's plan: let the rumor build Khoury into a figure of menace, then "inherit" the network when Khoury dies, stepping in as the only person who knows what the favors were *for*. The favors aren't building toward anything yet. Suzuki will decide what they were building toward *after* she takes ownership of the story.

### The Danger
If the party tries to confront Khoury, they'll find a frail woman who genuinely doesn't know why the rumor is spreading and will assume the party works for whoever's behind it — which means she'll burn one of her favors to have them killed, defensively. If they try to "free" Vanya from his debt by paying it off, they'll discover Khoury no longer holds it; Suzuki does, and Suzuki will mark the party as people who interfere with her acquisitions. The trap is that there's no villain to punch yet — the conspiracy is still *under construction*, and disturbing the scaffolding alerts the architect.

### Hooks
1. **The Favor Comes Due.** Vanya is called in to repay his debt — he's told to deliver a sealed case to a Church bursar on a transit station. He begs the party to come with him. The case contains nothing illegal. It's a *test* of whether Vanya can be trusted with bigger things.
2. **The Buyer's Audit.** A Cartel functionary approaches the party offering good money to compile a list of everyone in the Spindle who's been seen entering Khoury's office in the last six months. The functionary works for Suzuki. The party is being asked to do the inventory.
3. **The Dying Woman's Offer.** Khoury, having heard the party is asking around, summons them. She knows she's being maneuvered but not by whom. She offers them her entire ledger — names, debts, leverage — if they'll find out who's building a story around her before she dies.

### GM Notes
Play this as a slow-burn information game, not a combat scenario; the tension is in *who learns what when*, and Suzuki's identity should be the reveal the party works toward over two or three sessions. Keep ambiguous whether Khoury's illness is natural or whether Suzuki accelerated it — let the party decide if that question is worth answering.
