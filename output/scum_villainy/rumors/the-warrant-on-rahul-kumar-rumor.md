## **The Warrant on Rahul Kumar**
*A Hegemony arrest warrant that the dockside whisper-network insists is a clerical mistake — and that everyone is loudly advising each other to ignore.*

### As Heard
> "You hear they put paper on Rahul Kumar? The shipwright at Berth Nine, the quiet one with the bad leg. Hegemony warrant, capital-tier, signed by a Procurator two systems over — for a man who hasn't left this rock in nine years. Listen: it's a clerk's slip, wrong file, wrong Kumar. But if you go waving that around trying to *help* him, you'll just put yourself on a list. Stay out of it. They'll sort it or they won't, and either way it isn't ours."

*Source: Natalia Flores, who runs the bonded-cargo ledger at the port office and saw the warrant cross her desk this morning; she's telling everyone in the canteen because she's frightened and wants the rumor to settle into "nothing to do with us" before someone gets curious.*

### What's Actually True
The warrant is not a clerical error. Every fact in it — name, birth-world, biometric hash, the nine-year residency — is correct. What's wrong is the framing the whisper-network has built around it: people assume "Rahul Kumar, harmless shipwright" can't be the same person as "Rahul Kumar, capital-tier fugitive," so they've decided it must be the wrong file. It isn't. Nine years ago, before the leg and the limp and the quiet, Kumar walked away from a Cinder-class weapons program with something the Procuracy is only now able to prove he took. The warrant is precise. The Procurator waited until the asset became actionable. Kumar himself doesn't yet know the warrant exists; Natalia is the only person on-station who has seen it, and her instinct to bury it under "obviously a mistake" is the kindest reading she can give a man who fixed her brother's skiff for free.

### The Danger
If the party "helps" by quietly tipping Kumar off, they're aiding a genuine fugitive on a capital warrant — and the Procuracy's slow-walk means there are almost certainly observers already in place, watching to see who breaks cover to warn him. If they instead take the rumor at face value and stay out of it, they miss the chance to get to Kumar (and whatever he took from the Cinder program) before the snatch team arrives, which by the port logs is two days out on a sealed Hegemony courier. The trap isn't the warrant. The trap is the comforting interpretation everyone is repeating.

### Hooks
1. Natalia, three drinks in at the canteen, corners one of the PCs and asks them — specifically them, because they don't owe anyone here anything — to "just check on Rahul, don't say why, just see he's alright." She is testing whether her own advice is one she can live with.
2. A second outsider arrives at Berth Nine asking polite, wrong-shaped questions about Kumar's work schedule: Natnael Tadesse, traveling as a marine-insurance assessor, carrying credentials that scan clean and a sidearm that doesn't match the cover.
3. Kumar's apprentice, a teenager who idolizes him, overhears two dockhands repeating Natalia's "stay out of it" and correctly intuits that something is wrong. She comes to the PCs because they're the only crew on-station who aren't from here, offering to pay in stolen ship-parts for an honest answer.

### GM Notes
Let the players inhabit the comfortable interpretation for as long as possible — every NPC reinforces "obviously a mistake," and the truth should land only when they're already committed. Escalate by tightening the courier's ETA each scene. Keep ambiguous what Kumar actually took from the Cinder program: he should be willing to die rather than say, and the answer matters more as leverage than as fact.
