## The Oversight
*Stardancer-class — a smuggler's yacht that listens to frequencies it was never built to hear*

### Description
She sits low on her landing struts like a coursing hound at rest, hull plates lacquered the matte non-color that Stardancer captains call "customs grey" — somewhere between cargo-hauler beige and patrol-cutter slate, deliberately unmemorable. The forward sensor blister has been re-skinned three times, and you can see the seams; someone keeps opening it up. Inside, the air smells faintly of ozone and cardamom, the latter from a previous owner's tea habit that has soaked permanently into the galley bulkhead. The bridge crew chairs are mismatched — one of them is a Hegemony pilot's seat with the insignia ground off, and the grinding wasn't done carefully.

### History
She was launched as a private courier for a minor Guild factor's family on Aklatan — a wedding gift, supposedly, though the dowry-yacht story has the suspicious neatness of a cover. For two decades she carried discreet cargoes between Guild stations under increasingly creative manifests, passing through three legitimate owners and at least one shell company before **Amit Nair** bought her at auction and went pirate-adjacent with her. Nair was the kind of captain who collected debts in favors and favors in silence; he flew her hard for six years, installed the signal dampener through a contact who didn't ask questions, and was reportedly very pleased with himself about something the week before he vanished.

The disappearance happened mid-run between Lockstep and a drop point that has since been scrubbed from every chart the crew can access. Nair went out the airlock in a vac-suit to "check something" the sensors had flagged — the Ur-band receiver, though no one on the current crew installed it and no one knows when it was installed — and never came back. The cargo (sealed crates, no manifest) was delivered on time. The pay cleared. Sitting around the galley table afterward, **Kofi Mukherjee** (mechanic, sharp-tongued, the one who'd been with Nair longest) put it to a vote: split and scatter, or keep her flying. **Þorgerður Ólafsdóttir** — the pilot, twenty-three and recently aboard — broke the tie by pointing out that none of them had anywhere better to go. They've been crewing her ever since, and they do not talk about Amit Nair.

### Quirks
- **The Whisper Band:** The Ur-band receiver picks up signal traffic on frequencies the Hegemony swears don't carry anything. Sometimes it's static. Sometimes it's rhythmic. Once, near a derelict in the Procyon Reach, it was a voice counting backward in a language no one aboard recognized. The crew has learned not to listen alone.
- **Cardamom Bulkhead:** The galley wall behind the heating unit smells permanently of spiced tea, no matter what's actually cooking. New crew find it comforting. Sniffer-dogs at customs find it confusing, which has proven useful twice.
- **The Empty Chair:** Nair's captain's chair on the bridge has never been re-assigned. The crew rotates command depending on the job. No one sits in his seat. They don't discuss this rule; they just enforce it by not sitting there.
- **Customs-Grey Camouflage:** Her hull color reads as boring on every visual scan in the sector — which is great when you need to be ignored, and terrible when you need a pickup pilot to find you in a crowded yard. She has, twice, been towed by mistake.
- **The Loud Dampener:** The signal dampener works beautifully at range but produces a faint electromagnetic "shadow" up close that a careful tech can spot. They've learned to land hot, shut it down fast, and walk away from the ship for the first hour after touchdown.

### Crew Roles
- **Pilot** — Þorgerður flies her now, and the ship rewards a light touch on the throttle; she's fast but the Light hull means a Pilot who treats her like a gunship will get the crew killed. The Empty Chair rule means the Pilot often runs the bridge during a job.
- **Mechanic** — Kofi's seat, and the ship needs one badly: the Ur-band receiver wasn't installed by anyone living, and keeping it functional means working on hardware no manual covers. A Mechanic aboard The Oversight will eventually have to decide whether to learn what it's saying.
- **Scoundrel** — Someone has to forge the manifests, work the customs officers, and keep the cardamom-bulkhead trick paying dividends. The ship's history of discreet cargoes means contacts in three port cities still remember her silhouette.
- **Mystic** *(optional but increasingly necessary)* — The Whisper Band is starting to ask for one. The crew has been resisting this.
- **One open berth.** They keep it open. They tell new hires it's for cargo overflow. It is not for cargo overflow.

### Current Situation
She's running hot off a score that went better than planned and worse than safe — a data extraction out of a Hegemony auxiliary station that left a heat signature the crew can feel between their shoulder blades. They're looking for a low-profile layover, somewhere with a tolerant dockmaster and no Guild auditors, and they're willing to take a milk run or a quiet courier job to justify the parking. In her cargo hold right now: two crates of legitimate medical supplies (the cover), a sealed datacore (the score), and — in a compartment Kofi welded shut last month and won't discuss — something the Ur-band receiver started reacting to three days ago. She represents, to the right buyer, a fast and forgettable ship with installed surveillance gear worth more than her hull; to the wrong buyer, a loose end with a missing captain and a haunted antenna.

### GM Notes
*Amit Nair did not die in the airlock. The Ur-band receiver was installed by something that contacted him, and what went out that lock met what was waiting outside; whatever came back wearing his vac-suit didn't come back aboard. The receiver still listens because it is still being spoken to, and the sealed compartment Kofi welded shut contains a fragment of Ur-tech that Nair brought aboard on his last legitimate run — the thing the crew delivered for him, in those unmarked crates, was the rest of the same artifact. Someone, somewhere, has now reassembled it. When the Whisper Band stops being static and starts being instructions, the crew will have to decide whether they have a passenger, a haunting, or a contract they never knew they signed — and whoever assembled the rest of the artifact will come looking for the piece Kofi has hidden.*
