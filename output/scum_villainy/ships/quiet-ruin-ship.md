### Description
She sits low on her gear like a cat about to bolt — Cerberus airframe stretched longer than spec, with the hull armor patched in matte black over the original gunmetal so she reads as two ships welded into one shadow. The cargo vault dominates the lower deck, a Hegemony-spec pressure door with the original inspection seal still intact and a fresh weld-bead around the frame where someone added a second locking mechanism the manufacturer never authorized. The bridge smells like coriander and ozone — someone has been growing herbs in the auxiliary console housing for years, and the long-range comm gives off a faint, persistent harmonic that the crew has stopped hearing. Captain Jaber's coat still hangs on the back of the pilot's chair. No one has moved it.

### History
She was commissioned six years ago by Siti Jaber, a former Hegemony customs inspector who took early retirement under circumstances she never explained and used the severance to buy a stripped Cerberus hull and a vault rated for the exact contraband classifications she used to interdict. For four years Jaber ran a tight, profitable circuit through Aklan and the outer Hegemony stations, moving the kind of cargo that needed paperwork her old colleagues would recognize. She hired a four-person crew, paid them well, and told them almost nothing — which suited everyone fine.

Then, on a run to a station she'd visited a dozen times before, Jaber walked off the ship to make a delivery and did not come back. The crew waited eighteen hours, completed the rest of the manifest themselves, split her share four ways, and held a vote in the galley about whether to scuttle the ship, sell her, or keep flying. They kept flying. That was two years ago. They have never reported Jaber missing, never changed the registration, and never moved her coat.

### Quirks
- **The Captain's Chair:** Jaber's coat still hangs there and the crew rotates pilot duty rather than sit in it. New hires are not told why until they ask, and then they're told only once.
- **The Coriander Console:** Someone — no one will say who — has been growing herbs in the warm exhaust housing of the auxiliary nav console for years. It's surprisingly productive. It also means the auxiliary nav console cannot be used without killing the garden.
- **Survivor's Reputation:** In certain ports — Hutaka Spire, the back bays of Lumin, anywhere ex-Hegemony customs drink — she is known not for any job she's done but for the things she's come back from. People buy the crew drinks and ask questions they don't answer.
- **The Second Lock:** The cargo vault has two locking mechanisms. The original Hegemony seal opens with standard codes. The second one, welded on after Jaber vanished, requires all four current crew to authorize together. No exceptions, no overrides, no captain.
- **The Harmonic:** The long-range comm emits a faint tone at exactly 47Hz whenever it's powered. The crew doesn't hear it anymore. Passengers always do, and it always bothers them.

### Crew Roles
- **Pilot** — Rotates. No one sits the captain's chair as a permanent posting. The Pilot on shift is the Pilot for that shift only, and decisions that would normally fall to a captain go to vote.
- **Mechanic** — Inherits the coriander garden, the harmonic, and a maintenance log written in four different hands with no clear chain of authority. Must be comfortable with the words "I don't know why she does that, she just does."
- **Scoundrel / Speaker** — The crew runs without a captain, which means every port negotiation requires someone who can sell that as a feature rather than a vulnerability. The face of a ship that has no face.
- **Muscle or Stitch** — The fourth seat is open and has been since Jaber. The current three have not agreed on what they need, which is itself a statement about how they're running.

### Current Situation
Quiet Ruin is currently docked at a mid-tier station on the Aklan rim, taking on a circuit job that three other independent crews have recently turned down — and none of the three have explained why, even to friends, even drunk. The pay is good. The cargo manifest is clean on paper. The route passes within sensor range of the station where Siti Jaber walked off and disappeared, which the crew has not discussed out loud but which all four of them have noticed. They need a fourth hand before they leave dock. They are being unusually particular about who.

### GM Notes
*Siti Jaber is alive. She walked off the ship because she recognized someone on that station — an old Hegemony colleague who had finally tracked down what her early retirement actually cost the service — and she ran without warning her crew to keep them clean. She's been living quiet in the Procyon underlayer for two years, and the circuit job that three crews refused is being offered by someone who has finally figured out that Quiet Ruin is the thread that leads to her. When the crew realizes their job is bait, they'll have to decide whether the captain who abandoned them is worth dying for — and whether the coat on the chair was a promise or just a coat.*
