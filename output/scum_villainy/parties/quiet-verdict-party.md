### Crew
- **Wren Adeyemi-Vasque** — Pilot — The captain by default; flies like she's looking for an exit and won't say from what.
- **Halix Morr** — Stitch — Ex-Hegemonic combat medic, keeps the crew breathing and keeps a private ledger of every life she's saved and lost.
- **Petrel "Pet" Sukhanov** — Scoundrel — A drone-wrangler and signals-thief who can talk a Hegemonic patrol AI into a slow nap; nervous about hands-on work.
- **Brokk Vael-Tenn** — Muscle — A former arena fighter from the Procyon free-pits, calm in a firefight and unsettlingly polite about it.

### How They Came Together
Halix found Wren first — not as crew, but as a patient, in a backroom clinic on Aragas where Wren had walked herself in with a hand she couldn't stop shaking and no intention of explaining why. Halix didn't ask. She did, however, recognize the name from the *Halberd Lyssa* manifest she'd treated survivors from — and there shouldn't have been a Adeyemi-Vasque on that list at all. She kept that to herself and offered Wren work flying a medical-grade cargo nobody was supposed to know existed.

That first run needed muscle, and Halix knew Brokk from a pit-circuit she'd patched fighters for on Procyon. Brokk came aboard for the money and stayed for the quiet. Pet they picked up sideways — caught trying to spoof the *Verdict's* transponder in a Lokra dock to hitch a ride off-world ahead of a Hegemonic warrant. Wren liked the audacity. Halix liked that Pet could make their drones invisible to customs. The score that bound them was a milk-run that turned: a Cerberus patrol vectored in on intel they shouldn't have had, and only Wren's flying and Pet's jamming got them out. They've been a crew since the burn cooled.

### What Holds Them Together
Heat, mostly. The Hegemony has partial scans of the *Verdict* from that botched run, and none of them can show their faces in three sectors without the ship and the people in it. Issa Vey's loan is the leash; the Cerberus patrol report is the gun. They work together because separately, they get caught.

### The Fault Line
Wren (Pilot) and Halix (Stitch) circle each other carefully. Halix knows what's in the unedited *Halberd Lyssa* record — or enough of it — and Wren knows Halix knows. Neither has spoken it aloud, and the longer that silence holds, the more it shapes every conversation. Brokk reads the tension and assumes it's romantic. Pet reads it correctly and is terrified of being in the room when it breaks.

### Shared Secret
They've all noticed Wren's left-hand tremor gets worse in the dark, and worse still when she's been drinking at the Cygnet. None of them have asked. Halix has quietly adjusted the ship's stims to compensate without telling her. Pet keeps a private bet with themself on how long until Wren mentions it. Brokk simply makes sure he's in the cockpit doorway on hard burns, in case.

### First Score Hook
A fixer on Procyon named **Caro Lleyne** — a mid-tier diplomatic aide, and incidentally a former classmate of Wren's brother Téo — has reached out through a deniable channel. She wants a shipment of pacification-grade combat drones intercepted before they reach a Hegemonic garrison on Iri Xochi. She's offering a route, a window, and a docking code into Iri Xochi's lower archives district. She says Téo recommended the *Verdict*. Wren has not heard from Téo in two years. Pet, meanwhile, recognizes the drone model from the warrant that put them on the run — they were the one who originally cracked its command protocol, and someone's been improving on their work.
