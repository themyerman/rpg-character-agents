### Crew
- **Vex Tarrow** — Muscle — The crew's blunt instrument and reluctant moral compass; she does the killing but she keeps count.
- **Sennik "Six" Olarra** — Pilot — Flies the Vow like she's trying to outrun something specific, which she is.
- **Brother Calish Mire** — Speaker — Defrocked from a temple no one will name, he negotiates contracts with the unsettling calm of someone who has already forgiven himself for what comes next.
- **Dr. Imen Vrast** — Stitch — Patches the crew up and harvests what the marks leave behind; keeps a locked case under her bunk that the others have learned not to ask about.

### How They Came Together
Vex and Six met first, in a holding cell on Procyron after a job went sideways for both of them — Vex had been working solo wetwork, Six had been running courier flights for a syndicate that decided she knew too much. They broke out together and stole the Stardancer from a Hegemony impound yard, which is most of why the Vow's transponder still reads as a ghost ship in three databases.

Brother Calish found them, not the other way around. He walked onto their ship at a fueling station on Aldermish with a sealed contract from the Reconciled and the calm certainty that they would take it. The first score was a Hegemony tax assessor on Lochran — Vex made the kill, Six flew the extract, Calish handled the cover story. It paid enough to bring Dr. Vrast aboard when they needed someone to keep Vex alive after a knife in the kidney on the second contract.

Vrast was working a back-alley clinic on the Lochran undercity when Calish recruited her. She didn't ask for much money. She asked for passage off-world and for nobody to ask about the case.

### What Holds Them Together
The blood debt to the Reconciled. Three names — they've taken one. Two left. If they don't finish the contract, the Reconciled stop being a patron and start being a problem, and the Reconciled don't have a problem with anyone for very long. Until those names are crossed off, this crew is welded together whether they like each other or not.

### The Fault Line
Vex (Muscle) is keeping count of the kills, and Calish (Speaker) is the one who keeps finding reasons the count needs to go up. She took the contract thinking it was three. Calish has hinted twice now that the Reconciled may "amend" the list, and Vex has started watching him during briefings the way she watches marks. Six sides with Vex when pressed but won't say so out loud, because Calish is the one who pays the docking fees.

### Shared Secret
The second name on the list — the one they took a month ago — wasn't the right person. They knew it before Vex pulled the trigger. The intel was wrong, or Calish lied, or the Reconciled didn't care. The mark was a clerk with two children. Vrast was the one who confirmed the ID afterward, from the body. None of them have spoken about it since, and Vrast's locked case now contains something she took off that body that she has not explained to anyone.

### First Score Hook
A fixer named Halgren on the orbital at Kray's Anchor has a line on the third name: a Hegemony logistics officer named Theron Vail, currently aboard a slow freighter bound for the inner systems. Halgren wants a finder's fee and one favor owed. The complication Calish hasn't mentioned: Vail used to be stationed at the same outpost where Six ran her last syndicate route, and he's the reason she had to run. The complication Vex hasn't mentioned: she recognized Vail's name the moment Calish said it, because he was on a list of her own from years ago — a list she thought she'd finished.
