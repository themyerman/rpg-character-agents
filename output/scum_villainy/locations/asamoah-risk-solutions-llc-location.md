## **Asamoah Risk Solutions, LLC**
*A House of Knives front pretending to be a security consultancy on a station that just lost the person holding it together.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | House of Knives front — boutique security consulting firm |
| **Condition** | Peacekeeper gone, knives loose under the table |

### Atmosphere
The office occupies the third tier of Concourse Halcyon — clean glass, recessed lighting, a receptionist who keeps her hands beneath the desk. Real plants in real soil, which costs more than most ships. The waiting room smells of citrus polish and, underneath it, the faint copper tang of a recent cleaning that wasn't about dust; one of the chairs has been replaced and doesn't quite match the others.

### Notable NPCs
- **Nana Asamoah** (Senior Partner, nominal House lieutenant) — Inherited the firm three weeks ago when his sister Adwoa was "reassigned coreward." He's competent at consulting and terrified of the Knives; he wants to sell the whole operation to someone, anyone, before his people realize he can't hold them.
- **Esi Agyeman** (Lead "Threat Analyst," wetwork specialist) — Loyal to the memory of Adwoa, not to Nana. Believes Nana arranged his sister's removal. Is taking small private contracts on the side and feeding them through the firm's accounting; one of these contracts may already involve the party's patrons.
- **Paola Rodríguez** (Hegemony Customs Inspector, Checkpoint 7-Ardent) — Just had her checkpoint upgraded with new biometric scanners and a Warden-class cutter, the *Pale Justice*, parked outside. She is bored, ambitious, and looking for a career-making bust. Will accept bribes, but only large ones, and remembers every face.

### The Situation
Adwoa Asamoah ran this office like a clinic — clean books, clean kills, clean tribute upwell to the House of Knives. Three weeks ago she vanished, and the official story (promotion) fools nobody. Her brother Nana now sits in her chair pretending nothing has changed while Esi quietly builds her own client list and her own faction inside the firm. The whole place will fracture within a month; Nana knows it and is preparing to run. His escape route is already booked — a chartered hauler departing through Checkpoint 7-Ardent — but the checkpoint upgrade has rendered his forged credentials useless, and he doesn't know it yet. Meanwhile the firm's public job board carries a posting nobody can quite believe: *"Discreet specialists wanted, courier work, 40k credits, ask for Mr. Asamoah."* That's four times the going rate. It is either Nana's desperate bid to hire his own getaway crew without the Knives noticing, or it is Esi's bait, posted to flush out who Nana might trust.

### Hooks
1. The party walks in answering the 40k posting. Nana takes one look at them, sweats visibly, and asks them into the back office — where Esi is already sitting, smiling, having "just stopped by to say hello."
2. A previous client of the party gets a polite call from Asamoah Risk Solutions offering a "complimentary security audit" of their ship while it's docked. The audit team is real. So is the tracking suite they're installing.
3. Inspector Rodríguez approaches the party at a bar, having flagged them on arrival. She knows the firm is dirty, she knows something is about to break, and she will trade looking the other way at the checkpoint for whatever they can give her on Asamoah Risk Solutions.

### GM Notes
Play Nana as a man drowning in a suit that fits him — let the party smell the desperation and decide whether to exploit it, save him, or sell him. Esi is the real danger; she should be charming in person and ruthless on paper, and the party should suspect her too late. Keep it ambiguous whether Adwoa is actually dead or whether she's coming back — that uncertainty is what makes the whole board interesting, and the answer should depend on which way the party tilts the firm.
