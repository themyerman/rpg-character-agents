## **Carrowmore Deepwell Research Annex**
*An off-books xenobiology lab drilled into a frozen moon, where two armed parties are about to pretend to negotiate.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Unlicensed research station, half-buried in methane ice |
| **Condition** | Hosting a "diplomatic" meeting in 14 hours. Everyone is lying about their headcount. |

### Atmosphere
The lift down from the surface pad smells of antifreeze and something organic underneath it — a sweetness like fruit two days past ripe. Sodium lamps buzz in the long observation corridor, and behind the inner glass, suspended in a column of pale brine, something coils slowly that does not have the right number of segments to be from any catalogued world. A junior tech wipes the glass with a cloth, smiles at the party, and keeps wiping the same spot.

### Notable NPCs
- **Dr. Paola Rojas** (Station Director, ex-Hegemonic Council xenobiologist) — Wants the meeting to go cleanly so her buyers walk away with samples and her debts get paid; hiding that the specimen has been *responding* to proximity for six days and she hasn't told either party.
- **Fionuala Campbell** (Ulixian syndicate broker, here to "purchase research access") — Arriving aboard the *Best Intentions*, a Jackal-class with four extra bodies in the cargo hold; one of your crew burned her on a job in the Procyon Veil and she has not forgotten the face.
- **Lan Jung** (Cinder Shroud envoy, austere and polite) — Came in on the *Quiet Ruin*, a Warden-class running dark on the L2 shadow; another of your crew owes him a debt of honor from a prison break, and he intends to collect by name during the meeting.

### The Situation
Rojas is selling access to a specimen she has no license to possess — recovered from a wreck the Hegemony officially never logged — to two rival buyers simultaneously, because she needed both deposits to keep the station's life-support contracts current. Campbell's syndicate plans to walk out with the specimen and leave the Cinder Shroud delegation as a corpse pile and a story. The Cinder Shroud knows this and intends to do exactly the same thing first. Neither side knows the specimen has begun influencing the dreams of everyone within forty meters of its tank, and Rojas is the only person who's noticed because she's the only person sleeping on-station. The party walked into a powder keg with two of their faces already on someone's list.

### Hooks
1. As the party docks, a robed figure waiting at the airlock — a Mystic the manifest doesn't account for — addresses one PC by a name they haven't used in years and says, *"It's already awake. You came because it called you. The other two are just here to die."*
2. Rojas pulls the party aside within the first hour and offers double the standard rate to act as "neutral security" for the meeting — she does not mention either rival faction by name, only that "the guests have personalities."
3. Campbell spots the PC she has history with across the commissary, smiles, and sends over a bottle of something expensive with a handwritten note: *"After the meeting. We have things to discuss. Bring the others."*

### GM Notes
Run the first few hours as social pressure — corridors are tight, everyone is armed under their coats, and any PC who sleeps on-station has a vision involving the specimen that contains one true piece of information they shouldn't know. Escalate by having the two factions' extra bodies start "accidentally" running into each other in the service tunnels; the shooting starts whether the PCs are ready or not. Keep what the specimen actually *is* ambiguous — let the Mystic's claim that it summoned the party hang unresolved, and never confirm whether the dreams are precognition, manipulation, or both.
