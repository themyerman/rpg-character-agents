## **Nadezhda "Nadya" Vosk**
*Independent freight broker working out of a back office on Albion — the kind that doesn't have a sign on the door.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Appears to be** | A mid-tier cargo broker with a legitimate debt claim and a time-sensitive retrieval problem |
| **Actually is** | *(revealed only in Truth 2, 3, or 4 below)* |

**Appearance:** Mid-forties, broad-shouldered, hair pulled back so tight it pulls at her temples. She's wearing a perfectly respectable rust-colored coat over a clean blouse, and she's tucked a hand-cannon — a Callahan revolver, the long-barreled kind people carry when they want to be seen carrying it — into the front of her waistband like she forgot it was there. When she leans in to talk numbers, her breath hits you like a dead animal in a hot crawlspace. She doesn't seem to know. Nobody's told her in a while.

**The Pitch:** *"Here's the situation, plain and clean: a merchant named Fen Chou owes me sixty-two thousand platinum, has defaulted on the note, and is currently hiding on Constance while his goods sit in a bonded warehouse on Dyton waiting for a bankruptcy judge to sort things out in five months. Some of what's in that warehouse is mine. I got the purchase contracts right here. The problem is Chou's cousins are sitting on the facility, and the bankruptcy trustee won't release disputed cargo until after the hearing. I don't have five months. I need a crew to go in, pull eight crates marked with a blue diamond, bring them to a transfer point on Paquin. Legal, mostly. The paperwork's solid. It just won't hold up to a very long look."*

**The Job:** Get into a bonded commercial warehouse on Dyton, locate and extract eight specific crates marked with a blue diamond, and transport them to a handoff contact named **Susa Rhee** at an entertainment venue on Paquin. No docking records. Nine days.

**The Payment:** 2,400 platinum up front, 3,600 on delivery to Rhee. She'll cover fuel for the Dyton leg.

**The Truth (GM rolls 1d4 in secret — only one is real):**

1. **Straightforward** — Nadya's claim is legitimate and the crates are hers. The complication: Chou's extended family has been living in the warehouse since the bankruptcy filing, turned it into a semi-permanent camp. They're not dangerous — just territorial and numerous, and they've seen enough debt collectors to know how to make a retrieval miserable without throwing a single punch.

2. **One Layer Down** — The eight blue-diamond crates are hers. There are also three others in the same storage unit, different consignee markings, that she intends for the crew to grab as well. If pressed, she'll claim those are hers too. They're not; they belong to the Chou estate, and she's hoping nobody looks closely until the ship has left atmo.

3. **The Real Story** — Fen Chou defaulted deliberately, on advice from a trade syndicate that wants to absorb Nadya's client contracts while she's bogged down in legal proceedings. Susa Rhee on Paquin is the syndicate's own broker. Nadya doesn't know any of this — she thinks she's recovering her goods; she's walking them straight to the people who engineered her situation. The crew figures this out before or after the drop, depending on which question they think to ask.

4. **The Reversal** — Chou never owed Nadya anything. She forged the purchase contracts after a deal went sideways three years back and has been steadily claiming his inventory as hers ever since. The bankruptcy is his last legal option to recover something. Susa Rhee is a fence. The "transfer point" is a chop operation. The crew is helping strip a legitimate businessman of everything he owns, and the paperwork Nadya is so proud of will fall apart under thirty seconds of actual scrutiny.

**Why They'd Take It:** Six thousand platinum for a warehouse job is serious money, the paperwork looks solid at first pass, and Dyton-to-Paquin is a route with light traffic and lighter inspection. If someone aboard has ever been on the wrong end of a creditor or a bankruptcy court, Nadya's framing of Chou as a deadbeat lands surprisingly well.

**Connection:** *Yolanda Petrescu*, longshoreman foreman who runs the bonded warehouse Nadya uses on Albion. Yolanda owes one of the crew a favor from a bar fight two years back. Asked over a drink, she'll say: *"Nadya pays on time and doesn't argue the weight. But she's been jumpy these last two months — comes down to the docks herself now, won't send her clerks. And she started carrying that ridiculous gorram pistol in March. Whatever she's into, she's into it alone, and she's scared."*
