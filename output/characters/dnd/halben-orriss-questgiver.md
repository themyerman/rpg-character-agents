## **Halben Orriss**
*Quest Giver — a master glassblower with ink-stained hands and a problem he can't take to the guild*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Appears to be** | A respected glassblower of the Cinderhall Guild, mid-fifties, prosperous but visibly unslept |
| **Actually is** | *(revealed only in Truth 2, 3, or 4 below)* |

**Appearance:** A barrel-chested man in a fine but rumpled green coat, the cuffs scorched at the edges from a lifetime near furnaces. He carries a small lacquered case under one arm and keeps glancing at the tavern door; his right hand is bandaged, and there's fresh ink — not soot — staining the fingers of his left.

**The Ask:** *"I'll be plain with you, because I haven't got the time to be clever. Six nights ago my apprentice, Wenna, didn't come home. The watch says she ran off with a lover, and the guild — the guild has decided that's the end of it. It is not the end of it. She left her mother's ring on her bench, and that girl would sooner cut off her hand than leave that ring behind. I need someone who isn't on the guild's payroll and isn't on the watch's payroll to go to the old kiln-yard on Marrow Street and find out what happened there. That's where she was last seen, and that's where nobody wants to look."*

**What They Want:** Investigate the abandoned kiln-yard on Marrow Street in the artisan quarter. Find evidence of what happened to apprentice Wenna Carrow six nights ago. Return with the truth — or with her, if she's alive.

**What They're Offering:** 75 gold up front, another 150 on completion, and a set of three "truthglass" lenses of his own making — flawless crystal lenses that grant advantage on Investigation checks involving forgery, hidden writing, or fine craftsmanship. Also: a written letter of recommendation to the Cinderhall Guild, which opens doors in five trade cities.

**The Truth (DM rolls 1d4):**

1. **Clean** — Halben is exactly the worried master he appears to be. Wenna was abducted by a small ring of body-snatchers selling fresh corpses to a necromancer operating out of a cellar beneath the kiln-yard. She's still alive, barely, being kept for "later." The complication: the snatchers have a watch sergeant on their payroll, which is why the official investigation died so fast.

2. **Complicated** — Halben isn't lying about Wenna, but he's left out that she was *his* spy. He suspected another guild master, Orlen Vesh, of selling counterfeit guild-stamped glasswork to a foreign buyer, and he sent Wenna to dig for proof. She found something — and now she's missing. The ink on his fingers is from drafting an accusation he doesn't yet have evidence for. The party isn't just rescuing a girl; they're walking into a guild war Halben isn't ready to admit he started.

3. **The Real Story** — Halben isn't a glassblower at all anymore — he hasn't touched a pontil in two years. He's a quiet informant for a crown agency that watches the trade guilds for smuggling and worse. Wenna was a junior agent, not an apprentice. The kiln-yard isn't an abandoned workshop; it's a transfer point for something being moved through Cinderhall glass shipments — and Halben suspects it isn't goods at all but people, or something stranger. His superiors told him to stand down. He's hiring adventurers because he has no one else, and because if he's right, Wenna is the second agent to vanish this month. The bandaged hand is from someone who came to warn him to stop asking.

4. **The Reversal** — Wenna isn't missing. She's hiding — from Halben. She found his private ledger six nights ago: records of bribes paid, shipments falsified, names of guild members who died inconveniently after crossing him. Halben runs the counterfeit operation he claimed to be investigating. Orlen Vesh was his fall guy, already positioned to take the blame when the crown's auditors arrive next month. Wenna is being sheltered by people who believe her, and the kiln-yard on Marrow Street is where she handed off a copy of the ledger to someone she trusts. The party has been hired not to rescue her but to find her — and bring back whatever she took. The truthglass lenses are not a gift. They are Halben's eyes. Every forgery, hidden mark, or concealed document the party examines through them, he sees too. He'll know exactly what they've found before they know what it means.

**Why They'd Say Yes:** The reward is real, the lenses alone are worth the trip, and a man this composed doesn't beg strangers for help unless the official channels have already failed someone who deserved better.

**Connection:** **Sergeant Mira Doht** of the city watch — she filed the dismissive report on Wenna's disappearance. Halben mentions her name with carefully flat disgust. Whether she's lazy, bought, or being leaned on from above is the first question worth asking, and her answer points toward whichever truth the dice have chosen.
