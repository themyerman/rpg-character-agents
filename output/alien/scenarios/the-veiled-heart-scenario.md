## **The Veiled Heart**
*Cinematic one-shot — Outbreak Response — estimated session length: 4 hours*

### The Setup
Forty-one hours ago, the Weyland-Yutani research station **Theron-IV** ceased routine telemetry burst and broadcast a Class-3 Biohazard flag on its emergency band. The crew has been contracted as a rapid response team out of Anchorpoint Station: assess the breach, render medical aid to survivors, and uplink a full status report to W-Y Atmospheric Sciences Division. Pay is generous. The brief is twelve pages long and says nothing about what is being researched at Theron-IV.

### The Location
**Theron-IV Atmospheric Research Station:** A spindle-shaped orbital lab in geostationary tether above LV-447, ostensibly studying stratospheric ammonia chemistry. Built by W-Y Subcontractor Geholgod & Hess in 2174, it has three ring decks (Habitat, Lab, Containment) connected by a single central spine elevator. The spine elevator is the *only* way between rings — there is no secondary route, and the maintenance crawlspaces around it were welded shut six months ago "for thermal efficiency."

**Condition on arrival:** Docking clamps engage clean. Internal atmosphere reads nominal. Two details: every interior light on Habitat Ring is set to emergency amber rather than standard white, and the station's resident cat — a fact mentioned offhand in the personnel file — is sitting in the airlock when they cycle through, perfectly calm, watching them.

### The Situation (GM only)
Theron-IV's actual research project, **Codename VEILED HEART**, was the cultivation of a xenomorph "royal" specimen recovered from a derelict on LV-447's surface eight months ago. Lead xenobiologist Dr. Idowu Castellan was attempting a controlled molt to study the queen-caste transition. Thirty-six hours ago the specimen — still in its larval-warrior phase — broke restraint during a feeding cycle, killed two technicians, and impregnated four more before retreating into the Containment Ring's coolant manifolds. The bursts have already happened. There are now five active xenomorphs on the station plus the original specimen, which is undergoing rapid growth toward queen morphology in the warmth of the reactor shielding.

Station director Dr. Castellan is alive, barricaded in the Habitat Ring's medbay with three uninfected survivors. She triggered the biohazard flag and then immediately triggered the **Q-7 Quarantine Lockdown** — sealing the station from external command override. She did this *before* calling for help, because she intends to make sure no one leaves. If the crew does nothing, the queen will complete morphology within eighteen hours, lay the first egg clutch within thirty, and the station will become a permanent hive that W-Y can quietly "lose" and recover later.

### The Threat
**What it is:** Five juvenile xenomorphs (warrior caste, 48 hours post-emergence — fast, hungry, still establishing hunting patterns) plus one pre-queen specimen roughly two and a half meters tall, currently sessile and gorging in the reactor coolant bay. The juveniles hunt in pairs through the ventilation network. The pre-queen cannot yet pursue but can produce facehuggers within twelve hours if disturbed.

**What it wants:** The juveniles want to feed and to drag living prey back to the pre-queen for cocooning. The pre-queen wants warmth, biomass, and to be left alone until morphology completes. Disturbing her early triggers a defensive surge — all juveniles converge on her location.

**What the crew doesn't know:** Dr. Castellan's personal terminal in the Habitat Ring's director's office contains the full VEILED HEART project file, including the location of a **scuttling charge** wired into the reactor shielding. The charge can be remote-detonated from any terminal *if* the operator has Castellan's biometric override. Castellan herself will not give it up — she still believes the specimen can be saved.

### Escalation Structure
**Act One (arrival):** The crew finds the Habitat Ring eerily intact — half-eaten meals, a still-running shower, the cat. They locate the medbay survivors. Castellan is composed, professional, and lying about almost everything. She claims the breach was a chemical containment failure and that the missing personnel evacuated to the surface in lifeboats. (The lifeboats are still docked. A player should notice this.) She urges the crew to sweep the Lab Ring for "contamination samples."

**Act Two (revelation):** First contact with a juvenile in the Lab Ring spine corridor. One NPC casualty — probably a survivor Castellan sent along as a "guide." The crew now knows what they're dealing with. They also discover the Q-7 Lockdown when they try to dust off: docking clamps will not release. The override panel is in **Containment Ring, Section C-4** — directly adjacent to the reactor coolant bay where the pre-queen is nesting. Castellan, cornered, reveals partial truth and tries to convince the crew the specimen is "stable" and "recoverable."

**Act Three (finale):** Someone has to go into Containment to hit the manual override. Doing so will almost certainly wake the pre-queen and bring the juveniles down on them. The crew must decide: blow the station (requires Castellan's biometrics — taken willingly, taken by force, or taken from a corpse), fight their way to the override and run, or attempt the W-Y extraction the Company Agent has been quietly briefed on. Whatever they choose, someone is not getting on the shuttle.

### The W-Y Factor
Weyland-Yutani knows exactly what VEILED HEART is — Castellan reports directly to Bioweapons Division, not Atmospheric Sciences. The Company wants the pre-queen specimen recovered intact, or, failing that, viable tissue samples and at least one ovomorph. The Company Agent received a sealed addendum to the mission brief: **PRIORITY ALPHA — Recover Specimen VH-1 and all associated genetic material. Crew is expendable. Do not permit station destruction.** The addendum includes a tracker the Agent is supposed to attach to the specimen's containment unit, and a sedative cocktail "for use on uncooperative personnel."

### Complication
**The Q-7 Quarantine Lockdown** is the spine of the scenario. The crew is locked in within ninety minutes of arrival — earlier if they try to leave too fast. The override panel sits four meters from the pre-queen's nest, accessed through a coolant maintenance hatch that runs warm and damp and smells like meat. Reaching it requires a Mobility roll under stress with the pre-queen *right there*, behavior currently sessile but liable to wake on noise. Whoever pulls the override is alone in the dark with something the size of a horse breathing nearby.

### Survival Hooks
- **The Pilot** is the only one who can manually disengage the docking clamps from the cockpit the instant the lockdown drops — there's a thirty-second window before the station's secondary security cycle re-engages. Anyone still aboard when that window closes stays aboard.
- **The Medic** has to make the call on Castellan: she is the key to the scuttle charge, but she's also actively sabotaging the crew. Sedate her, kill her, or trust her? The medic is the one who can take the biometric scan from her hand whether she's conscious or not.
- **The Roughneck or Marine** is the one going into the coolant manifold for the override. Everyone else physically cannot fit through the hatch in a suit, or doesn't have the nerve. They are alone down there. Their squadmates are a voice on the comm.
