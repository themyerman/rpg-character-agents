## The Manifest Discrepancy

*You wake choking on the taste of cryo-fluid, three months early, and the first thing you see through the fogged glass is Diego Morales already out of his pod, sitting in his underwear on the deck with a fire axe across his knees.*

### Setting
The hypersleep vault of the W-Y survey vessel *Persephone* — sixteen pods arranged in a ring under sodium-yellow emergency lighting, the main grid dark. The air is colder than it should be and tastes faintly of burned plastic from a ventilation scrubber someone has manually disabled. Two of the pods are open and empty, the gel inside dried to a brown crust; one of them has been open for a long time.

### Scene
Diego is calm in the way of someone who has practiced being calm. He tells the party the *Persephone* is forty-one days out from Thedus, that the nav computer locked them out of FTL nineteen days ago, and that he and Isabela have been awake the whole time waiting for the ship to drift into beacon range. He says he woke the party because he needs help with the cargo bay, and he says it in the voice of a man who is asking and not asking.

### Situation
The manifest lists 2,400 kg of agricultural seed stock, sealed, climate-controlled, do-not-open. What is actually in the cargo bay is one military-spec stasis cylinder bolted to the deck plates, its readouts in a language the ship's systems flag as "RESTRICTED — CONTRACTOR PROPRIETARY." Nineteen days ago the cylinder started cycling its internal temperature in a pattern Isabela recognised as a thaw sequence; she and Diego cut the power feed, then cut it again when it found an auxiliary, then welded the maintenance hatch shut. The FTL lockout is not a malfunction — Diego triggered it deliberately, because something in the ship's command tree was trying to bring them to a rendezvous coordinate that isn't on any chart they can access. They woke the party because Diego's arm is broken (he doesn't want to say how), Isabela hasn't slept in four days, and the cylinder's outer casing is now warm to the touch.

### Key NPCs
- **Diego Morales** — Acting captain, former USCM corpsman, W-Y contract pilot. Presents as steady, deliberate, the kind of man who makes coffee during a crisis. He is hiding that he opened the cylinder's external diagnostic panel eleven days ago and read enough of the contractor codes to know what's inside, and he has already decided that if it comes to it he will vent the entire cargo bay with everyone in it, including himself.

- **Isabela Vega** — Cargo officer and ship's medic, mid-thirties, a W-Y lifer on her seventh long-haul. Presents as exhausted, professional, faintly hostile to questions. She is hiding that the second empty cryopod belonged to a junior officer named **Hana Māhoe**, who woke up on day three of the emergency, panicked, tried to override the FTL lockout, and was killed by Diego with a wrench — Isabela helped him put the body in the airlock and she has not stopped seeing it.

- **Maame Asante** *(voice only, recorded)* — W-Y Special Cargo liaison whose pre-recorded "in case of variance" message plays automatically when the cargo bay door is opened. Presents as warm, reassuring, scripted. She instructs the listener to maintain the cylinder's integrity and await retrieval by an unnamed vessel; she does not mention that the retrieval window expired six days ago.

### Complication
Diego is not wrong. The cylinder almost certainly contains a specimen — bioform, ovomorph, something the contractor wing of W-Y has paid a great deal to move quietly between two points it doesn't want on a flight plan. Diego's plan (sit dark, wait for a civilian beacon, abandon ship to the first non-W-Y rescuer) is the only plan that keeps everyone alive *and* gets the truth into the open. But the moment the party arrived, the *Persephone's* transponder — which Diego could not disable without admitting to W-Y that the ship was compromised — pinged their arrival. Someone, somewhere, now knows the *Persephone* is no longer alone. The retrieval ship Maame's recording promised may still be coming. It may already be close. And whoever is on it will not want witnesses to a manifest discrepancy of this size, whether the cylinder is intact or not. Diego knows this. He is buying time for a rescue that may not exist, against a retrieval that may already be inbound, and he needs the party to choose a side before he has to choose for them.

### Possible Outcomes
1. **Cold Drift** — The party helps Diego maintain the silence: cylinder stays welded, transponder spoofed, ship drifts toward the nearest civilian lane. They survive, but they are now four people who know about a cargo W-Y will spend the next several years quietly erasing, and the *Persephone's* black box is still recording.
2. **The Retrieval** — A W-Y contractor vessel arrives before any civilian ship does. The contractors are polite, efficient, and willing to offer hazard bonuses and NDAs; the cylinder leaves with them, Hana Māhoe's death gets reclassified as a cryo-failure, and the party walks away paid. Diego does not walk away. Isabela might, if she signs.
3. **Containment Breach** — Someone (party, Diego in a moment of resolve, the cylinder itself) cracks the seal. Whatever wakes up has been in transit for a long time and is very hungry. The party now has the rest of the session to decide whether the *Persephone* reaches anywhere at all, and Diego's airlock plan stops being theoretical.

### Hooks
Where was the cylinder going, and who was supposed to receive it? The rendezvous coordinate Diego locked out of nav is still in the system — a point in deep space with no station, no colony, no reason to exist. Someone built something out there, and they're still waiting for a delivery.

### GM Notes
*The truth the party won't learn this session unless they really dig: the cylinder is not a specimen. It's a person — a contractor scientist in deep stasis, carrying the specimen inside her, gestating under medically managed conditions because the company has decided live-host transit is more reliable than mechanical containment. Maame Asante's recording is warm because she briefed the host personally. If the party plays the audio log buried in the cylinder's diagnostic panel, they hear a woman's voice saying "I consent" in the flat tone of someone who was paid a great deal of money to say it.*

*Mechanical tripwire: every hour the party spends in the cargo bay near the cylinder, roll one STRESS for any character with EMPATHY 4+ — something in there is dreaming, and the dreams are leaking. If anyone uses a motion tracker in the bay, it reads* two *contacts inside the cylinder, not one. Don't explain why.*
