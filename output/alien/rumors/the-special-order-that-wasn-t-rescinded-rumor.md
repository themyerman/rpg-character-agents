## **The Special Order That Wasn't Rescinded**
*A bar-deck rumor about a standing contractor directive that W-Y claims no longer exists — and a ship whose crew keeps following it anyway.*

### As Heard
> "You know the *Acheron's Margin* — Type-C hauler, out of Gateway three months back? I ran a cargo check on her at Thedus waystation last week. Manifest checks out, crew checks out, everything checks out. Except the route she filed puts her twenty-two days off the optimal corridor for no reason in the manifest, and she's running dark on her transponder for the middle nine days. I know that ship's comp officer from a training posting. She didn't look at me. Not once. You know what that means."

*Source: A W-Y contract logistics analyst named Femi Adeyemi who has been at the bar since 1800 and is not drunk — is very deliberately not drunk — and is telling this to anyone who sits down because he put it in an internal report six days ago and nothing happened.*

### What's Actually True
The *Acheron's Margin* is operating under a Special Order — a direct contractor directive from W-Y Special Acquisitions that supersedes her captain's routing authority and her crew's standard operating protocols. The Special Order is routed through a command layer that bypasses the standard W-Y operations chain, which is why Adeyemi's report went nowhere: it reached someone who has been managing the *Margin's* silence for the last three months. The nine-day dark-transponder window covers a survey waypoint in the outer belt that W-Y Survey Division officially decommissioned two years ago. The *Margin* has made this transit four times in the past eighteen months. On her second transit she returned with three sealed containers she off-loaded directly to a W-Y Special Research facility at Waypoint Kovacs. On her third transit she returned with two containers. On her fourth transit — the current one — she has not returned yet. She is nine days overdue.

### The Danger
Anyone who starts pulling on the *Acheron's Margin*'s route history is pulling on a W-Y Special Acquisitions operation that has been active for at least two years and involves a survey site the company officially closed. The logistics analyst Adeyemi is a loose thread; he filed a report, which means there is now an internal record of his concern, which means he is a liability, which W-Y Special Acquisitions will handle efficiently and quietly. If the party makes contact with Adeyemi, they become associated with his inquiry, and associated inquiries get managed. The more interesting thread is the comp officer who didn't look at Adeyemi — she is the *Margin's* only member of crew who has been on all four transits, and she is the only one who knows what the sealed containers held on the return trips. She is currently nine days overdue on a ship that is not transmitting.

### Hooks
1. Adeyemi approaches one of the party directly — not at the bar, but at the dock, before they lift — with a portable drive and a story about his report. He wants them to carry the drive off-station and transmit it from outside W-Y communications management range. He will pay. He is also, he admits, afraid of what happens to him when the party leaves.
2. The *Acheron's Margin* comes in. She docks quietly, three days after the party's arrival. Her crew manifest is correct. Her captain files a standard arrival report with no anomalies. The comp officer requests a private berth-to-berth transmission — to a W-Y Special Research address — that the dockmaster processes without looking at it. The sealed containers, this transit, number four. Adeyemi, watching from the bar, starts shaking.
3. The comp officer, whose name is Yolanda Ferreira, contacts the party through an intermediary with a single question: "Do you have a route out of this sector that doesn't go through any W-Y waystation?" She doesn't say why. She doesn't say what she saw on the fourth transit. She looks like someone who has decided to stop waiting for someone else to pull the thread.

### GM Notes
Leave the contents of the sealed containers ambiguous as long as possible — the rumor is more useful as a pressure system than as a reveal. Escalate by having W-Y security "check in" with the party shortly after any contact with Adeyemi — polite, professional, no direct accusation, just noting that they've been in contact with a person of interest in an ongoing internal review. Whether the *Acheron's Margin* ever explains what the survey site holds is the lever you pull when the party is ready — until then, let the silence do the work. Ferreira knows. Play her as someone who has decided the truth is worth more than her W-Y career, but not more than her life, and the math on that calculation is currently very close.
