## The *USCSS Meridian Gate* Crew

### Members
- **Captain Fatou Camara** — Pilot, sixteen years on commercial runs — The vessel's master in every formal sense and in no practical one; W-Y has final say on cargo, routing, and "mission parameters," and Fatou has been deferring to that since the mortgage on this hull forced her hand. She knows exactly what she's traded away. She keeps flying because stopping would mean admitting how far the trade has gone.
- **Chief Engineer Piotr Maciejewski** — Roughneck — The ship runs because Piotr keeps it running through a kind of mechanical stubbornness that borders on personal argument. He has strong opinions about the right way to do things and a documented inability to stay quiet when the wrong way is being done. This makes him invaluable and periodically unbearable.
- **Dr. Sera Nakamura** — Scientist, xenobiology secondment — The W-Y contractor who joined at Waypoint Kovacs, who has two locked cases she handles herself and a communications window to W-Y Special Research that the rest of the crew cannot access. She is polite, thorough, and carries the particular stillness of someone who is not surprised by anything that is happening.
- **Corporal Devon Hatch** — Colonial Marine, "security attachment" — Technically assigned for "hazardous cargo escort" on a freight run that isn't supposed to have hazardous cargo. He doesn't know what he's guarding. He's starting to narrow it down.
- **MU/TH/UR 7.3 (called "Mutter" by the crew)** — Synthetic — Medical, emergency protocols, ship systems redundancy. Completely reliable. Replies to every question with a degree of precision that feels, slightly, like a door closing. The crew is professionally comfortable with Mutter and personally unsettled by her in roughly equal proportion.
- **Ship's Medic / Cargo Officer Remi Asante** — The one who manages the manifest, the one who knows the numbers don't add up, and the one who has decided to keep that knowledge private until he understands why. He is the friendliest person on the ship and the one who sleeps least.

### How They Came Together
The *Meridian Gate* is a W-Y long-haul commercial vessel on a transit run from Gateway Station to the outer survey corridor, with a documented cargo of processed minerals and agricultural supplies. Captain Camara has run the ship for four years under a W-Y operating lease; the crew is a mix of long-term contract staff (Piotr, Remi) and assignment personnel joined at Gateway (Nakamura, Hatch). MU/TH/UR 7.3 shipped with the vessel from the yards.

Nobody told Camara about the second cargo manifest until after they broke orbit. W-Y Special Acquisitions produced it — a secondary consignment attached to a contractor code she didn't have clearance to review — and informed her that Dr. Nakamura would be overseeing it. Camara reviewed what she was allowed to review, which was almost nothing. She filed no objection, because the lease agreement's breach clauses would eat the ship. She has told herself, three times, that she made the right call.

### What Holds Them Together
The *Meridian Gate* is three months from the nearest waystation. Nobody is going anywhere. Beyond necessity: Piotr and Remi have run this ship together for four years and operate with the efficiency of a long marriage, including the arguments. Hatch is career military and follows chain-of-command structures even ambiguous ones. Nakamura is polite, professional, and produces no friction. Camara is a good captain in all the ways that matter until the ways that matter change.

### The Fault Line
Camara and Nakamura. Camara is the vessel's legal master and has been functionally overruled on her own ship, which she has been swallowing for six weeks. Nakamura is following instructions from a principal she reports to that Camara cannot contact. The arrangement is legal. It is also the precise mechanism by which every story like this one becomes what it becomes, and Camara knows it — has known it — and has not yet decided whether she is the kind of person who acts on what she knows before the situation forces the question, or after.

### Shared Secret
Three weeks out of Gateway, Piotr ran a mass-balance check on the cargo hold and found 240 kilograms unaccounted for in the sealed secondary consignment. He told Remi. Remi told nobody, but pulled the W-Y secondary manifest and found a line item with a contractor code that resolved, after two hours of cross-referencing, to a biohazard classification he has never seen applied to agricultural transit. They have not told Camara. They are waiting to understand what they're looking at before they hand her a problem she can't un-see — because once she knows, she'll have to act, and Piotr and Remi have both read the operating lease, and they know what acting costs.

### First Session Hook
The *Meridian Gate* is twelve days from its scheduled course correction when the proximity alarm fires — not a ship, but a beacon, automated, standard W-Y survey format, broadcasting from a planetoid that isn't on any commercial chart. Nakamura receives a simultaneous communication from W-Y Special Research that she does not share. She tells Camara the beacon is a navigational artifact and recommends they hold course. Mutter, without being asked, notes that the beacon frequency matches a restricted-access W-Y survey protocol associated with Category-4 biological containment operations, and that this information has been in the public navigation database for eleven months. Nakamura looks at Mutter for one second longer than is comfortable. Mutter looks back, neutral, precise, a door that is very nearly closed. Camara's hand is on the course-correction interface. In the cargo hold, 240 kilograms of something in the secondary consignment has shifted — Remi can tell from the hold-balance readout — to the port side. It wasn't on the port side before.
