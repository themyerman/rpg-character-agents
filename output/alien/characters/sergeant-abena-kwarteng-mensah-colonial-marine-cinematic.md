*She has run seventeen drops and been officially debriefed after four of them; she knows the difference between the kind of debrief where somebody wants your report and the kind where somebody wants your silence.*

### Career Talents

- **Toughness:** She took a round through the thigh at Fiorina and walked the last two hundred meters to the dropship on her own terms. She does not go down from things that put other people down, and the squad stopped asking how after the second deployment. They just keep her between themselves and the corridor.
- **Banter:** She doesn't rally the unit with speeches. She makes them laugh — at the wrong moment, the wrong target, and perfectly. Platoon-level black humor delivered at the exact instant it converts terror into functioning aggression. Civilians find it disturbing. Marines find it essential. Fourteen of the people she has served with are still alive specifically because they were laughing when they should have been freezing.

### Equipment
- **M41A Pulse Rifle (serial number filed off the receiver)** — Third weapon in four tours. She has never named one, which the others find ominous. The tactical light is held on with electrical tape. The underbarrel grenade launcher has one round in it she has not fired in two years, and she will not explain why she's holding it.
- **USCM combat armor (chestplate from one unit, arms and legs jury-rigged from two others)** — The chestplate has a dent above the sternum from a round she stopped at close range on her second drop. The round itself is in her breast pocket, deformed, where it has been for six years.
- **Debit card with Cr 14,000 unspent** — Hazard pay, mostly. She doesn't spend it. Not because she's saving for something; she just hasn't, and when she thinks about what for, nothing comes.
- **A sealed document wallet — USCM legal transcript, hearing file 7-7-4-ALPHA** — A copy she was not supposed to keep. Her testimony is five pages; three pages are redacted in the printed copy she was handed, but she read the document before the redaction was applied, and she has not forgotten what was on them.

### ⚠ Personal Agenda *(GM only — do not show to other players)*

The Fiorina inquiry Kwarteng-Mensah copied the transcript from was a cover-up. Garza is not USCM Intelligence; she is a Weyland-Yutani Special Acquisitions liaison embedded in the debrief system. What was covered up: a viable specimen was recovered from the Fiorina site and transferred off-world under sealed W-Y contractor orders. The three witnesses listed in the transcript as "transferred" have subsequently been hospitalized — stress responses, psychiatric holds, conditions consistent with everything Kwarteng-Mensah has read in the restricted bioform-exposure literature. She requested this specific deployment because the transfer manifest she managed to cross-reference lists this vessel's cargo bay as a waypoint. She doesn't know if the specimen is still aboard, was transshipped, or is somewhere in this hull right now. She came to find evidence. Garza approved this deployment because Garza wants to know how much Kwarteng-Mensah actually knows — and whether she needs to be handled. **If Kwarteng-Mensah finds what she came looking for, she has already decided: not up the chain (an organization that buried this once), but directly to whichever civilian survivor is still standing — burn it into the open, career and life be damned, because fourteen people she made laugh are still breathing and she intends to keep it that way.**
