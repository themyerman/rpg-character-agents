### Career Talents

- **Field Surgeon:** He is not a medical doctor, but he has pulled shrapnel from a thigh, sutured a scalp laceration in zero-g, and kept a man stable for eleven hours on the wrong side of a bulkhead with nothing but a trauma kit and a very clear head. Trauma, it turns out, is just biology under bad conditions. He understands biology.
- **Researcher:** He sees patterns faster than the situation deserves — in data, in behavior, in the things people say when they think they're saying something else. In the field this manifests as a kind of uncomfortable accuracy: he tells the group what is actually happening before the group is ready to hear it, which makes him useful and difficult in roughly equal measure.

### Equipment
- **A W-Y-issued SpecLab sampling kit (field model, heavily modified)** — Cultures, slides, a portable DNA analyser with the factory firmware replaced by his own builds, a spectroscopic probe he rewired to work on biological tissue because the original purpose was mineral surveys and he needed something faster. The kit weighs 4.3 kg. He can tell you its current weight within 50 grams.
- **A personal data tablet with 847 locked research files** — Twelve published papers, thirty-one drafts, and a directory marked LANDER-7 that he opens once every six months and cannot bring himself to delete. The tablet has a photograph as its lock screen: six people in yellow survey suits standing in front of a biodome airlock, grinning. He is on the right. He is the only one still alive.
- **A W-Y Standard Personnel Sidearm (still in factory packaging)** — He was issued it when he signed the contractor rider for this mission. He has not opened the package. He is aware this is irrational. He is also aware that opening it means accepting a version of this job he is not prepared to accept yet.
- **A notebook, paper, mechanical pencil** — He writes observation notes by hand because typing feels like filing reports and filing reports feels like telling someone else what he's found before he understands it himself. The pages at the back are in a shorthand only he reads.

### Rival
**Dr. Constance Weller, W-Y Principal Investigator (remote oversight)** — She was third author on four of his published papers, principal investigator on Lander-7, and signed the mission report that attributed the incident to equipment failure and personnel error. He was one of the personnel. He has read the report. He knows what she chose to include and what she chose to omit.

### Signature Item
A sealed culture vial on a lanyard around his neck — sample ID L7-CONTROL-0, the negative control from Lander-7, the one thing that came back clean. He keeps it as a reference point. Also as a reminder that clean is not the same as safe, and reference points are not the same as answers.

### ⚠ Personal Agenda *(GM only — do not show to other players)*

The Lander-7 incident was not equipment failure. It was contact — controlled contact, brokered by Constance Weller and a W-Y Special Research Division team that had been on-site for two weeks before Osei-Bonsu's survey team landed. The cover story required two things: a plausible mechanical failure narrative and no surviving witnesses who had seen the wrong airlock door open. Osei-Bonsu survived because he was in the negative-pressure anteroom taking a routine control sample when the door closed. He heard everything. He has spent six years writing careful papers about harmless extremophile bacteria because anything that draws attention to his area of expertise draws attention to Lander-7, and he is not ready for that attention until he has evidence that cannot be dismissed. He is on this mission because a signal in the public survey data matches a subsurface resonance signature from Lander-7 that Weller's report described as "instrument artifact." It is not an instrument artifact. He knows what it is. He needs a sample. **If he finds what he came for, he will not report it to W-Y — he will transmit it to the Interstellar Commerce Commission's sealed evidence repository, the one legal channel Weller cannot reach, and accept whatever comes after, because six people smiled at him in front of an airlock and he is the only one who can tell anyone what really happened to them.**
