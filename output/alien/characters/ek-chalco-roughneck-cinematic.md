### Career Talents

- **Mechanic:** She can keep a dying compressor breathing through a shift it has no business surviving. Her fixes are ugly — bracket clamps, jumper wire, the wrong gasket trimmed down with a knife — and they hold because she refuses to walk away from a thing that's still trying.
- **Grit:** She has worked sixteen-hour shifts inside a pressure hull with a known crack. The cold doesn't reach her the way it reaches others, and neither does panic. It catches up later, in the bunk, when there's nothing left to fix.

### Equipment
- **Industrial cutting torch (hull-rated, safety guard removed)** — the guard was in the way during the last job and she never put it back. The trigger has a hair pull now. She knows.
- **Hardhat with a cracked brim and three stickers** — one sticker is a union local that no longer exists, one is a radiation warning from a deck she shouldn't have been working on, one is hand-drawn and she will not explain it.
- **Work suit with the company logo half-peeled off — underneath is a different company's logo** — Geistwerke Extraction. She didn't peel it cleanly. She wanted you to see.
- **A physical photograph of a crew she's no longer part of** — six people in orange coveralls, arms around each other, grinning at a camera in a refinery mess hall. She is in the photograph. Three of the others are dead. She has not said which three.

### Buddy
**Yusra, the galley cook** — Yusra noticed Ek wasn't eating during the first week aboard and started leaving a covered plate by her bunk without a word. Ek has decided, quietly, that Yusra gets off this ship alive.

### Rival
**Mr. Halvard Reese, W-Y Safety Compliance** — He boarded at the last waypoint with a clipboard and a commendation pin and a smile he uses on people whose names he doesn't bother to learn. He doesn't recognize her. She has not slept properly since.

### Signature Item
A folded sheet of carbon paper in her interior suit pocket — an incident report, page three of seven, with three names underlined in pencil and a fourth name circled at the bottom. The circled name is not one of the dead.

### ⚠ Personal Agenda *(GM only — do not show to other players)*

Your Rival is Halvard Reese, the W-Y safety inspector who buried your report after the blowout at Tharsis-9. Three people died — Marisol, Kweku, and a kid named Theo who had been on the job six weeks. Reese filed it as operator error and walked away with a commendation. You memorized his face from the hearing transcripts. He is on this ship and he does not recognize you yet, because to him you were a serial number on a deposition he never read. You want him to know who you are before he dies, and you want him to die somewhere the company cannot recover the body or the truth. The trouble is that Reese knows the evacuation protocols, knows the override codes, and is exactly the kind of man who will survive this by stepping over the rest of you. If the crew decides to trust him — to follow his lead, to put him in a lifeboat — you will have to choose between letting him live and exposing what you know in a way that turns the crew against you in the worst possible moment. **If it comes to it, you will sabotage his escape — quietly, mechanically, in a way only another roughneck would recognize — even if it means someone else doesn't make it out either.**
