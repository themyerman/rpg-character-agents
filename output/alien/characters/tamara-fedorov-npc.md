## **Tamara Fedorov**
*Senior pressure-systems tech — twenty-two years on rigs and refineries, and the kind of operator who can hear a bearing failing through six inches of plate steel.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Role** | Roughneck (Pressure & Fluids Technician) |
| **Affiliation** | Independent contractor, currently leased to Geholgod Chemical Refining |
| **Appearance:** | Coverall sleeves rolled to the elbow, forearms latticed with old chemical-burn scars in a pattern too neat to be accidental. Wears a faded enamel union pin on her collar — the union it represents was dissolved by court order in 2174. |

**Demeanor:** Speaks in short, level sentences, mostly to the machinery. Doesn't make eye contact when she's working — does when she's lying. Has a habit of cleaning her wrench on her thigh whenever a supervisor's name comes up.

**Wants:** To finish her rotation, sign off the maintenance log, and catch the next supply hauler off-station. Says she's done with deep-rotation work. Says it like she's said it before.

**Secret:** Foreman Vukotić has been bleeding her overtime pay into a shell account for three years — she's tracked every credit. She's not leaving until she has it, even if it means letting a "minor" coolant fault escalate enough that company auditors come asking the right questions. She has the leak already staged.

**Hook:** The crew notices her in the mess hall reviewing a private datapad she immediately blanks when approached. Later, when something on the installation goes wrong — and something *will* — she's the only one who knows which junction box still has working bypass relays. The price of her help is silence about what she's doing in Pump Room 4 after shift.

**Equipment:** A torque wrench with a custom-machined grip, worn smooth. A pocket dosimeter she checks compulsively even in clean zones. A sealed envelope addressed to a child she has not named on any personnel file.

**Connection:** Foreman Andrej Vukotić — her direct supervisor, her debtor, and the man who signed off on the safety waiver that took two fingers off her left hand in '79. Every shift she works under him costs her something she can't get back, and they both know it.
