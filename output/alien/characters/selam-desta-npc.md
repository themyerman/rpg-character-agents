## **Selam Desta**
*Installation Medical Officer — the one who reads the exposure timelines before anyone else and decides what gets written in the log.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Role** | Medic |
| **Affiliation** | Weyland-Yutani (contracted) |
| **Appearance:** | Tall, careful-handed, hair pulled back tight enough to look like discipline. Med-bay scrubs immaculate. The W-Y badge on her chest is a clip-on — the original was pried off and the adhesive square underneath is still sticky, gathering lint. |

**Demeanor:** Quiet, attentive, the kind of bedside calm that makes people sit down before they realize they've sat down. She listens more than she speaks, and when she does speak she sounds like someone who has already rehearsed it twice. Her hands never shake. Her jaw does.

**Wants:** To finish her two-year rotation, collect her hazard bonus, and transfer to a civilian hospital on Thedus. She'll tell you that, unprompted, if you ask about home.

**Secret:** A sealed company directive is sitting in her quarters, unopened, on the nightstand. She knows roughly what it says — one paragraph about a specific crew member's tissue samples, one paragraph about what she is *authorized* to do if containment fails. She has been finding reasons not to read it for nine days.

**Hook:** Someone in the crew comes to her with symptoms that don't match anything in the standard exposure index. She'll treat them. She'll be kind. She will also quietly pull a second blood draw she doesn't log, and the PCs may catch her doing it — or she may come to *them*, white-faced, asking who else has been in the lower deck.

**Equipment:** An M4A3 service pistol kept in a drawer under gauze, cleaned monthly with the same professional neutrality she gives a scalpel. A padded case of surgical tools maintained to sterile standard regardless of circumstances. A field medkit stocked well past regulation — three items in it are not on the manifest, and one of them is a sedative dose calibrated for an adult human at roughly the crew engineer's body weight. Folded inside her breast pocket, a child's crayon drawing of two figures holding hands under a yellow sun. She has never explained who drew it, and no one has asked twice.

**Connection:** Dr. **Adaeze Okeke**, her residency supervisor at the Thedus Colonial Health Authority, wrote her the recommendation that got her this contract. Adaeze also signed an NDA Selam wasn't told about. If Selam ever talks about what's in that sealed directive to anyone outside the company, Adaeze loses her license, her pension, and her daughter's tuition — and Selam knows it.
