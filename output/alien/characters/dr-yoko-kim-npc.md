## **Dr. Yoko Kim**
*Installation Medical Officer — the kind of doctor Weyland-Yutani sends when they already know the body count.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Role** | Medic |
| **Affiliation** | Weyland-Yutani (contracted medical staff) |

**Appearance:** Mid-forties, hair cropped short and practical, sleeves rolled to the elbow showing forearms scarred from old chemical burns she never got dermal regen for. Her ID badge is current. The lanyard it hangs from is from a different facility — one that was decommissioned six years ago.

**Demeanor:** Warm, unhurried, the kind of voice that makes you describe symptoms you weren't planning to mention. She listens with her whole face. She never quite answers questions about herself, and you don't notice until later.

**Wants:** To finish her rotation, sign off her reports, and rotate back to Gateway on the next available transport. She mentions a daughter in university on Earth. Twice. Unprompted.

**Secret:** There's a sealed W-Y directive on her personal slate she hasn't opened yet. She knows it concerns a specific crew member's tissue samples, and what she's authorized to do if "containment fails." She's a good doctor. She took an oath. She also took the contract, and the contract is winning so far by inches.

**Hook:** A crew member comes to her with a symptom that shouldn't be possible given their stated medical history — and she doesn't seem surprised. She'll patch the crew up, no questions, no charge. She just wants to keep them close enough to watch.

**Equipment:**
- **M4A3 Service Pistol** — clean, holstered low and behind the hip, handled with the professional neutrality of someone who considers it one more instrument on the tray.
- **Field surgical medkit** — stocked beyond regulation, with three items not on the facility manifest: a cryo-stable sample case, two unmarked ampoules, and a bone saw still in factory wrap.
- **A physical paper patient file** — for a crewmember who, per the official roster, has no prior medical history with the Company.
- **Personal comm** — older model, scratched. The contact list has fourteen names. None of the numbers connect to anything anymore.

**Connection:** Dr. **Aren Petrosyan**, her former mentor at the Hadley's Hope clinic before reassignment. He vanished from the company directory eighteen months ago. She still drafts him messages she never sends. If she ever finds out what happened to him, the directive on her slate will stop mattering very quickly.
