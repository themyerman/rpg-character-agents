## Sundown on Verlayne — The Baroness Wants a Ride

*A small woman in a dust-grey travelling coat steps out of the Tsabo Down concourse crowd, lays a gloved hand on Nasrin's sleeve, and says quietly: "You are the* Nighthawk*. I will pay in Kessar."*

### Setting
Tsabo Down's outer concourse smells of recycled coolant and the cheap incense the local cult burns to mark a change of administration — the third such change in fourteen months. The wall-screens are showing a loyalty oath nobody has memorised yet, cycling between two flag designs while a maintenance crew argues about which to paint over first. Half the customs booths are unstaffed; the other half are staffed by men who look like they were factory workers a week ago.

### Scene
The woman is calm in a way that the concourse is not. Behind her, at a polite distance, three other people are pretending not to watch the conversation: a heavyset Solomani in a medic's coat carrying a sealed cryo-case; a thin man in a Scout-cut jacket with no insignia; and a teenage girl with a Vargr breed-dog on a short leash. All four have luggage that has already been searched, and all four are looking at the *Nighthawk* the way drowning people look at driftwood.

### Situation
Baroness **Fjóla Ingvarsson** holds a minor patent over a moon that no longer recognises her authority — Verlayne's second coup in a year flipped the planet's government rating from feudal to participating democracy, and the new assembly has politely informed her that her titles, her stipend, and her safety are matters for the Moot to sort out, eventually. She needs to be off-world before the assembly's mood hardens, and she has three people she will not leave behind. She also has, in the cryo-case, **something she will not declare and will not let out of her sight** — the real cargo, the reason for the urgency, and the reason her destination keeps changing as she receives updates by tight-beam every six hours. The word "Kessar" is not a payment offer. It is a name she has been told to use with this specific ship by a contact who knew the *Nighthawk*'s registry, knew Aldric was aboard, and knew that word would open the ramp.

### Key NPCs
- **Baroness Fjóla Ingvarsson** — stranded patent-holder, ostensible client. Presents as composed, courteous, and absolutely certain that everyone in the room is beneath her in ways she is too well-bred to mention. What she's hiding: the cryo-case contains tissue samples from a survivor of an Imperial bioweapons program — a living person, sectioned into stasis-stable pieces, still legally a citizen — and she is the courier, not the patron. Someone above her is buying time for a procedure that cannot be rushed.
- **Dr. Emeka Adjei** — her "physician," Solomani, carries the cryo-case. Presents as a tired company doctor doing a job. Hiding: he is the procedure. He knows how to reassemble what is in the case, and he is the only one who does; if he dies, the cargo dies with him, and he knows it, and he is terrified.
- **Haruto Zhao** — the thin man in the unmarked Scout jacket. Presents as hired security, quiet and competent. Hiding: he is Scout Service Intelligence, and he is here because Sector Command also wants this cargo to arrive — but at a *different* destination than the Baroness's tight-beam updates are sending her. He recognises Nasrin's name on the manifest and is not happy about it.
- **Mika Watanabe** — fifteen, the Baroness's ward, with the breed-dog. Presents as sullen teenage baggage. Hiding: nothing, yet. She is the only person in this group who does not know what is in the cryo-case, and she has begun to suspect, and she is the pressure point if anyone wants to crack this open without violence.

### Complication
The Baroness's destination keeps revising because the people running the procedure on the other end keep moving — they are also being hunted, and the cargo is alive only as long as the schedule holds. Zhao's orders are to ensure the case reaches a Scout black site near Kessar, not the Baroness's stated destination; he will not act against the party openly but he will sabotage, lie, and if pushed, kill the doctor to force a diversion. Meanwhile the new Verlayne assembly has just realised the Baroness is at the port and has dispatched a squad of newly-deputised militia — earnest, undertrained, and authorised to shoot — to "escort her to a hearing." The *Nighthawk* has perhaps ninety minutes before the militia arrive. Every passenger has a reason their preferred destination is the only one that keeps the cargo alive, and at least one of them is lying about which destination that is.

### Possible Outcomes
1. **The Baroness's Route** — The party flies her where she asks, the cargo reaches its intended hands, and somewhere in the Marches a person who was supposed to be dead wakes up in a body that mostly works. The Baroness's patron remembers the *Nighthawk* favourably. Zhao files a report that puts Nasrin back on a watch-list she thought she was off.
2. **Zhao's Route** — Aldric, hearing the word *Kessar* and seeing a Scout-cut jacket, sides with the Service. The cargo reaches the black site; Dr. Adjei does not survive the debrief; the Baroness is quietly disappeared. Brigadier Carrowen's recall offer arrives one week later with a significant pay bump and a note that reads *good work.*
3. **The Third Door** — The party works out what is in the case before they lift, and decides for themselves where it goes. This is the hardest and most expensive path; it requires getting Adjei alone, getting Mika to ask the right question, and probably leaving Zhao behind on Verlayne with the militia closing in. It also means the *Nighthawk* now carries a person, in pieces, who can testify to what Sector Command was doing at Kessar.

### Hooks
The tissue in the cryo-case has a name, and that name appears in the same sealed file that has Aldric's name on it. Whoever sent the Baroness to *this* ship, by name, knew that — which means someone, somewhere, is moving pieces on a board the crew did not know they were on, and has decided the *Nighthawk* is one of the pieces.

### GM Notes
*The truth held back: the "procedure" is not a rescue. It is a reconstruction — Sector Command's bioweapons program at Kessar produced a small number of long-lived, regeneration-capable subjects (the Imperial files, in a moment of black humour, designated them* vampires*), and the Baroness's patron is trying to wake one up to testify before the Moot about who gave the orders. Dr. Adjei is the surgeon who put this one to sleep, eleven years ago, on the same unscheduled stop the* Nighthawk *made during her last refit. If Joe runs a radiation comparison on the cryo-case and the drive plating, he will find the match. Do not volunteer this. Let them earn it.*

*Mechanical tripwire: the cryo-case has an active maintenance signature that will show up on any thorough customs scan as "medical, classified, Imperial." Law level 6 on Verlayne under the new government means militia* will *scan. Korven's dockside contacts can delay this by perhaps forty minutes — not longer. The clock is real.*
