## *Ladrona del Río* — Crew Brief
*Tohoku-class freighter — known on three docks as "the Shepherd's boat," for reasons nobody can quite explain*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Ship** | *Ladrona del Río* (Tohoku-class long-hauler, retrofitted for short-range cargo) |
| **Condition** | Compression coil sings off-key above 40% burn — pilot has stopped mentioning it |
| **What It Owes** | Forty-two hundred platinum in docking fees backlogged across three Border worlds, and a favor to a Beaumonde fence named Oduya that nobody wants called in |

### Crew
- **Imka Vorel** — Muscle — The quiet center of gravity; everyone watches where she's standing before they decide where to stand themselves.
- **Wend Saro Calderón** — Shepherd — The ear the others talk into; the only one who knows two of the four real names aboard.
- **Henrik "Wick" Olufsen** — Pilot/Mechanic — Forty-eight, ex-Alliance merchant marine cashiered for selling fuel off the manifest; flies the boat and patches the boat and never quite looks at anyone straight on.
- **Suyin Ahn** — Grifter/Fixer — Twenty-six, walked off a Persephone con three years ago with a partner's money and his last name on a marriage certificate she never got around to filing; books the jobs, talks the customs men, lies before breakfast.

### How They Came Together
Wick bought the *Ladrona* at auction eight months ago with money that wasn't entirely his, and discovered within a week that flying her alone was a good way to die alone. He picked up Suyin in a Persephone bar where she was running a salvage-share scam on a man twice her size; she needed to be off-planet that night and he needed someone who could talk to a harbormaster without flinching, and the arrangement stuck. The two of them ran light cargo for four months before a job on Beaumonde docks went bad and they needed a body between them and a warehouse full of angry longshoremen. Imka took the contract through a cutout, did the work, didn't ask follow-up questions, and was halfway down the ramp when Wick offered her a permanent berth and a cut. She said she'd think about it. She coughed into her sleeve and said yes.

Wend came aboard last, three weeks ago in a cantina on Greenleaf. She'd been working the circuit through the Georgia system, hitching freight where she could, and the *Ladrona* was the third ship she'd asked. The first two had laughed at the offer of blessings-for-passage. Wick said yes before Suyin could say no, partly because he liked the look of her and partly because a Shepherd aboard is good cover at customs. She hasn't mentioned Tomás. She has mentioned Beaumonde, twice, with a quietness Imka clocked immediately.

### What Holds Them Together
The ship. None of them owns more than a duffel and a debt, and the *Ladrona* is the only thing keeping any of them out of a worse situation — Imka out of a wage-job that would kill her faster than the lungs, Suyin out of reach of a man on Persephone who still calls himself her husband, Wick out of the brig his old fleet would put him in if he ever set foot on a Core dock, and Wend off the slow-moving train of a circuit that was carrying her in the wrong direction. They are not friends. They are four people who all need this ship to keep flying for another six weeks, and that turns out to be enough.

### The Fault Line
Imka and Suyin. Suyin books the jobs and Suyin shaves the truth off them on the way to the briefing — a habit that got Wick yelled at twice and got Imka shot at once, on a "simple pickup" on Whitefall that turned out to involve a rival crew and a contested crate. Imka has told Suyin, quietly and only once, that the next time a job has been *trimmed* she will walk off the ship at the next port and take her cut in cash. Suyin believes her and resents her for it, because Suyin is the one bringing in the money and Imka is the one with the moral ledger, and Suyin has noticed that Wend now sits on Imka's side of the galley table.

### Shared Secret
They all know Wick coughs in his bunk too, and not the same cough as Imka's. They found his pill case in the galley two months back — Alliance-issue, the kind given to merchant marines exposed to unfiltered drive plasma — and nobody mentioned it, not even Suyin. Wend prays for him in the private hour after the others sleep. Imka leaves the strong tea on the console before her own shift. None of them have asked him how long. None of them want the answer, because the answer determines who flies the ship after, and that is a fight none of them are ready to have.

### First Session Hook
The *Ladrona* puts down on Persephone for a forty-eight-hour layover — Suyin's idea, a small courier run, in and out, she swears. On the second morning the airlock chimes and Wick opens it to find a woman standing on the gantry in nothing but a thermal blanket someone has thrown over her shoulders, barefoot, composed, beautiful in the specific dangerous way of someone who knows it and has stopped finding it useful. She asks for Suyin Ahn by a name Suyin has not used in three years. When Suyin appears at the top of the ramp the woman smiles the smile of a wife who has been looking for her spouse for a very long time, and says, in front of the whole crew, that she has come to collect what she is owed. She has no clothes, no luggage, no ship visible on the dock manifest, and a fresh surgical scar behind her left ear of exactly the kind Wend recognizes from the Alliance correctional hospital on Beaumonde where her brother is being held. Imka is already counting exits. The clock on Tomás just shortened.
