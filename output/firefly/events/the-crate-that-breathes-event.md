## **The Crate That Breathes**
*Fires the moment the cargo is loaded and the dockmaster's clipboard is still in hand — before the crew can lift off under the eyes of port security.*

### When It Fires
The ship has just set down at a mid-rim port — say Beaumonde or Persephone — and the loading bay doors are open. Two Alliance Federal Marshals are leaning on a crawler at the edge of the pad, not approaching, just watching every ship that lands today. The pickup is a routine three-crate haul of "agricultural samples" for a buyer named Thyra Hansen, paid half up front, clean enough that nobody asked twice. The dockmaster, a tired man named Carlos Reyes, has just thumbed his slate and is waiting on the crew's sign-off so he can go home.

### What Happens
As the third crate rolls up the ramp on the dock-loader, it shudders — not the jolt of shifting cargo, but a single sharp *thump* from inside, followed by a muffled, unmistakably human cough. The crate is keyed to Hansen's biometric only, sealed with a cortex-link tamper plate that will ping its owner if forced. Reyes hears it too. His eyes flick to the marshals across the pad, then back to the crew, and he very deliberately does not look at the crate. He clears his throat and says, "I need that signature, captain. Sometime today would be ideal."

### The Party's Position
The crew knows there is a living person in a crate they just agreed to deliver, that the marshals haven't moved but are definitely watching, and that Reyes is giving them a narrow window to decide what kind of people they are. They don't know who's in the crate, whether Hansen is smuggling them *out* of trouble or *into* it, or why Reyes — a man they've never met — is pretending he heard nothing.

### Possible Responses
1. **Sign and go.** Take off with the cargo intact, sort it in the black. Fast, simple, and commits them to whatever Hansen is actually doing. The cough may become screaming before they reach atmo.
2. **Stall and investigate.** Find a reason to delay liftoff — a "pre-flight check" — and try to crack the crate quietly in the hold while the ramp is still down. Risks the tamper-ping reaching Hansen, and the marshals noticing the delay.
3. **Talk to Reyes.** He clearly knows more than he's saying. A quiet word, maybe a few platinum, might buy context — or might tip him into a panic that gets the marshals walking over.

### Complications
- The person in the crate is a teenage girl, drugged but waking — and she has surgical scars at the base of her skull that any medic on the crew will recognize as Academy work. Hansen isn't a smuggler. Hansen is a *rescuer*, part of a thin network moving Academy survivors off-grid. Or she's something worse pretending to be that. The crew has no way to tell yet.
- One of the marshals is wearing a lapel pin the crew may recognize from a previous job — the same insignia worn by a fixer who burned them six months back. He's not here for routine patrol. He's here for *something*, and the timing is suspicious enough that it might be this crate, or might be the next ship over, or might be the crew themselves.

### GM Notes
Time this so the players have to make the call in the moment — don't let them retreat into hours of planning while Reyes stands there sweating. If they ignore the cough and lift off clean, the girl wakes up screaming in the cargo bay an hour into the burn, and now they're committed without information. Leave Hansen herself unseen for now — she's a voice on the cortex at the dropoff, and what she actually is should stay ambiguous until the crew has already chosen a side.
