## The Widow's Price

*A grease-stained mechanic refuses to charge you for a fuel cell, presses it into your hands, and whispers, "Don't come back here. Not for a month. Maybe two."*

### Setting
Heliotrope Station hangs in a slow tumble at the edge of the Kalidasa system — a converted ore-processing rig wrapped in welded-on habitat rings, smelling of recycled air, machine oil, and the faint sweetness of the hydroponics deck three levels up. The traders here are *too* warm: every shopkeep remembers your face, every barkeep stands you a drink, and the prices on basic goods run thirty percent above what they ought to. There's a fresh weld on the main concourse bulkhead that nobody will explain, and the security cameras are all aimed at the floor.

### Scene
The crew came in for fuel and a quiet night. Instead they're getting nervous hospitality — free drinks, anxious smiles, and a station-master who insists they take their business *anywhere but* the lower maintenance ring. A young woman in a chandler's apron quietly slips a paper note into a crewmember's pocket: *"The Federal at the bar isn't here for smugglers. Get out tonight or help us. Bay 4-C, 0200."*

### Situation
Eighteen months ago, Browncoat Lieutenant **Kiona Whitehorse** was reported killed when her unit was overrun on Du-Khang. She wasn't. She crawled out, took a new name, and has been Heliotrope's beloved deckhand mechanic ever since — the station took her in, hid her, and quietly became a way station for vets passing through who needed papers or a long sleep. Three days ago an Alliance Federal Marshal named **Yusuf Jaber** arrived on "routine inspection." He knows. He hasn't moved because he doesn't have her on cortex yet — and because he intends to frame the station's chandler, **Sigrid Eriksen**, for harboring her, which would let him seize the station's books and roll up the whole network. Sigrid is twenty-three, pregnant, and has no idea she's the bait. The station-master **Qori Nahuelpan** knows all of it and is trying to engineer a quiet evacuation before the Marshal's backup arrives in 36 hours, but she won't sacrifice Sigrid — and Kiona has decided, without telling anyone, to walk into the Marshal's hands so neither woman has to pay.

### Key NPCs

- **Kiona Whitehorse** — the "dead" Browncoat, now station mechanic. Presents as a quiet, competent woman with burn scars on her forearms and a habit of fixing things nobody asked her to fix. She is exhausted in a way that has nothing to do with sleep, and she has already decided to die rather than let the station or Sigrid burn for her — she just wants it done somewhere the Alliance won't get the satisfaction of a trial.

- **Marshal Yusuf Jaber** — Alliance Federal, alone, patient. Presents as polite, bookish, almost apologetic; orders tea instead of liquor and tips well. He genuinely believes the war's ghosts need to be laid to rest for the Core to heal, and he is willing to ruin an innocent pregnant woman to do it because the math, to him, works out — one life of comfort traded for closing a wound across three systems.

- **Sigrid Eriksen** — chandler, the one who slipped the note. Presents as bright, brittle, talking too fast; wears her late husband's coat two sizes too big. She knows *something* is wrong and that the Marshal has been asking about her ledgers, but she does not know she's the one being set up — she thinks she's protecting Kiona by warning the crew.

- **Qori Nahuelpan** — station-master, ex-quartermaster, never wore browncoat colors but lost a brother who did. Presents as a tired bureaucrat with a clipboard and a flask. Will lie to the crew's faces about everything until she decides she can trust them, and is prepared to vent a cargo bay full of her own people's contraband to make Jaber's case fall apart if it comes to that.

### Complication
Marshal Jaber is not a monster, and the crew will figure that out within five minutes of talking to him. He'll buy them a drink. He'll ask after their ship. If pressed, he'll explain — with real sadness — that he lost a sister at Serenity Valley to a Browncoat sniper who was never identified, and that he has spent eight years learning to do this work without hatred. He is going to arrest Sigrid at 0600 on charges that will stick, because the chandler's ledgers genuinely *do* show irregularities (she's been skimming to send money home, nothing to do with Kiona). Killing him solves nothing — his backup arrives in 36 hours with his case file already filed. The only way out is something *worse than murder*: discrediting him, breaking him, or giving him a different ghost to chase. And Kiona, who's worth saving, is actively trying not to be saved.

### Possible Outcomes

1. **The Ghost Walks** — The crew smuggles Kiona off-station in *Valor*'s smuggling hold, and Qori feeds the Marshal a planted trail toward a corpse on a dustball moon two systems over. Kiona lives, hating them for it; Sigrid's ledgers get "lost" in a convenient fire; the Marshal goes home with a closed case he half-suspects is a lie, and Heliotrope's network goes dark for a year.

2. **The Marshal's Mercy** — Someone gets Jaber alone and tells him the whole truth, including Sigrid. Maybe he folds. Maybe he doesn't. If he does, he files a different report and lives with it — and the crew now has an Alliance Marshal who owes them, and who will hate them for making him owe it. If he doesn't fold, somebody on the crew has to decide what to do with a federal officer who knows their faces.

3. **Kiona's Way** — The crew fails to stop her, or chooses not to. Kiona walks into the concourse at dawn in her old Independents jacket — kept folded under her bunk for eight years — and surrenders publicly, loud enough that Jaber can't pretend he was after anyone else. Sigrid is safe. Heliotrope survives. The crew watches a woman they barely knew get walked off her station in cuffs, and Jaber doesn't look triumphant. He looks tired.

### Hooks
Whichever way it breaks, *somebody* on Heliotrope now knows the crew's faces and what they're willing to do — and the network of stations that quietly moved veterans through the Rim has just lost a node, or gained a new one. If Jaber lives and is compromised, he becomes a recurring complication: not an enemy, exactly, but a man who needs the crew never to surface again, and who has the reach to make that happen.

### GM Notes
*The truth nobody learns this session unless they dig: Qori Nahuelpan was the Browncoat sniper at Serenity Valley who killed Marshal Jaber's sister. She recognized his name the moment he docked. Every choice she's made in the last three days — including her willingness to let Kiona walk into his hands — is shaped by the fact that the real ghost he's chasing is standing next to him pouring his tea. If the crew ever brings these two into the same room knowing this, that's a session of its own.*

*Mechanical tripwire: Jaber is a Big Damn Hero–tier opponent in a social fight, not a combat one. Treat his Influence and Knowledge dice as Diamond. He travels with no muscle because he doesn't need any — the Alliance is his muscle, and it arrives in 36 hours whether he's alive or not.*
