### Description
From outside she's the prettiest dancer in a rough crowd — her hull seams are clean, her paint a faded rose-gold that the previous crew couldn't bring themselves to scrub off, and her thrust vectors twitch with the responsiveness of a much newer ship. Step aboard and you'll feel the trade-off in your boot soles: the deck plating gives a half-millimeter under weight, the bulkheads ring hollow when you knock, and the cargo bay door closes with a sigh rather than a thud. The galley still smells faintly of cardamom from a tin someone left open in the spice cupboard six months ago, and nobody's thrown it out because the smell has become part of her. Her wings flex visibly in atmo, and the pilot has learned to love that flex the way a rider loves a nervous horse.

### History
Before she was *Breaking Dawn*, she was *Ragnheiður's Pride* — a legitimate cargo hauler out of Persephone owned and captained by **Ragnheiður Einarsdóttir**, an Icelandic-descent freightwoman who flew Independent munitions during the War under a false transponder and never quite came clean about it afterward. The Alliance impounded the ship in '17 on what the paperwork called "irregularities in declared manifest" and what Ragnheiður called "a grudge with teeth." She died waiting for the appeal hearing. The ship sat in a Boros impound lot for nearly four years, rusting in the salt wind, while the docking fees ate up whatever her estate might have recovered.

Three deckhands who'd served under **Freya Nilsson** on a different boat pooled what they had, took on a debt to a Eavesdown moneylender named **Kwesi Attah**, and bought her at auction for less than the cost of her thrusters. They renamed her *Breaking Dawn* — for the hour they signed the papers, for the war they wouldn't talk about, for the second chance none of them had expected. Freya, who'd lost her own ship to Alliance seizure two years prior, took the captain's chair without ceremony. The crew never repainted the rose-gold. Nobody's asked them to.

### Quirks

- **The Ghost Coordinates:** The backup nav console boots up with a set of coordinates pre-loaded that don't match any registered world, moon, or station — somewhere in the deep black between the Kalidasa and Blue Sun systems. Nobody put them there. The mechanic has tried four times to wipe them and they reappear within a week. The crew calls them "Ragnheiður's place" and has a standing bet on who'll be the one to finally fly out and look.
- **The Cardamom Cupboard:** The starboard galley cabinet has held that smell for half a year and refuses to air out. The doctor thinks it's a hull breach in the vent recycler. The captain thinks it's the ship being stubborn. Either way, every meal aboard tastes faintly of someone else's kitchen.
- **Featherfoot:** Her Agility d12 shows in the way she sidesteps. The pilot can thread her through a debris field on instinct, but she pays for it — every hard burn makes the structural alarms chirp like nervous birds. Strength d4 and Toughness d4 means she dances or she breaks. There is no middle gear.
- **The Sighing Door:** The cargo bay ramp's hydraulics were rebuilt with mismatched parts and now close with an audible exhale. The crew swears she's relieved every time she gets her people back inside. Newcomers find it unsettling for about a week and then start listening for it.
- **Rose Paint:** The hull paint can't be matched — it's a custom color Ragnheiður mixed herself, and the formula died with her. Any repair patch shows as a brighter spot. The ship is becoming a map of her own injuries, and the crew has stopped trying to hide it.

### Crew Roles
- **Captain** — Currently Freya Nilsson. Needs someone who can hold a line with the Alliance without rising to the bait, because the bait is constant. The captain's chair on this ship is half political position, half grief management.
- **Pilot** — The role this ship rewards above all others. Her Agility d12 wants a pilot who flies by feel, not by instrument, and who understands that pulling a hard maneuver on a d4-Toughness hull is a conversation, not a command.
- **Mechanic** — Must accept that the Ghost Coordinates cannot be wiped, the cardamom cannot be aired, and the rose paint cannot be matched. The job is keeping her flying, not making her new. A mechanic who fights her personality won't last a month.
- **First Mate / Quartermaster** — Manages the moneylender debt to Kwesi Attah, which is not yet paid off, and handles the paperwork that the Alliance officer keeps finding "irregularities" in. Half the job is forgery; the other half is bookkeeping.
- **Doctor** — Optional but increasingly necessary given how often the crew comes home bruised from hard burns. The infirmary is a converted passenger berth with a real surgical table bolted to a floor that flexes.
- **Two Passenger Berths** — Currently empty. Currently watched.

### Current Situation
*Breaking Dawn* is docked at Beaumonde's Bellerophon Berth, taking on a legitimate cargo of medical centrifuges bound for Greenleaf, and for the third port in a row an Alliance lieutenant named on his badge as **Tala Ironhorse** has appeared on the dock within six hours of their arrival. He buys coffee from the same stand. He nods politely. He has not asked a single question, filed a single report the crew can find, or made a single move — and that's what's wearing on Freya more than an outright raid would. The ship needs a quiet run, a paying job that doesn't require fast moves, and an answer about whether Ironhorse is hunting her or warning her. She's carrying centrifuges in the hold, two unregistered crates of Ragnheiður's old personal effects in a hidden compartment under the galley, and a navigation system that knows where it wants to go.

### GM Notes
*The Ghost Coordinates are real, and they lead to a small unregistered moonlet where Ragnheiður Einarsdóttir cached the manifest records of every Independent munitions run she ever made — names, dates, drop sites, the works. Some of those names belong to people who are now respectable Alliance citizens, and one of them belongs to Lieutenant Tala Ironhorse's mother. He's not following the ship to bust her; he's following her to find out what she knows, and he hasn't decided yet whether he wants the cache destroyed or delivered. When the crew finally flies to Ragnheiður's place — and they will — Ironhorse will be waiting there already, alone, off-duty, and armed with a choice he hasn't made.*
