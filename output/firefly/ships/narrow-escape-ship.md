### Description
She sits low and broad on her landing struts, all shoulders and no neck — a stub-nosed bruiser with twin oversized thrust cones that glow a deeper orange than they ought to and throw a wash of heat you can feel from across the bay. The hull plating is the giveaway: layered, overlapped, far too thick for a freighter, and patched in three different shades of grey where old turret mounts were cut away and welded over. Inside, the corridors are narrow and the bulkhead doors close with a heavy, hydraulic finality that civilian ships don't bother with — and everywhere along the ceiling you can see the painted-over stencils where Alliance regulation numbers used to be. She smells like hot iron and machine oil and the faint, persistent ghost of gun-cotton that no amount of scrubbing has gotten out of the forward storage hold.

### History
She came off the yards at Osiris twenty years back as a fast-response cutter, the kind of ship the Alliance used to chase smugglers through the Border worlds during the war. She saw three years of that work and was good at it — there are still scorch marks behind the engine baffles from a hit she took at Sturges that should have killed her and didn't. After Unification she was demilitarized in a hurry: turrets pulled, missile racks cut out, hold expanded by knocking through two interior bulkheads. The Alliance auctioned her to a contract freight outfit, where Captain **Mio Sato** ran her on government supply routes for seven years — milk runs to garrison worlds, mostly, hauling boots and rations and the occasional sealed crate nobody was allowed to ask about.

Sato gave her up the day she was asked to carry something she couldn't stomach — she never said what, only that "that wasn't a liveable arrangement" — and sold the ship cheap to her former first mate, **Malinee Jirayu**, who renamed her *Narrow Escape* in the same week. Jirayu ran her independent for nearly a decade, mostly honest freight with the occasional questionable charter, and the name proved prophetic more than once. She passed the ship along two years ago to the current captain after a bad job at Persephone that left Jirayu with a limp and a strong preference for dirtside life. The name stuck. The reputation stuck harder.

### Quirks
- **The Cold Room:** A small compartment off the secondary hold runs a consistent ten degrees colder than the rest of the ship, no matter what the environmental system says. The mechanic blames a thermal bleed from the old missile-coolant lines that were never fully removed. The crew uses it for storing perishables and, occasionally, for sobering people up.
- **The Heavy Doors:** Every interior bulkhead door is milspec — slow to open, impossible to force, and loud enough that you can track who's moving through the ship by sound alone. The crew has learned to time conversations around the hiss-thunk of the galley door.
- **Orange Burn:** Her thrust cones run hotter and brighter than any freighter has business doing, a leftover of the oversized military drive she still carries under all that converted plating. Dock workers always comment. It's the first thing port authority notices, and the captain has a story ready every time.
- **Ghosts in the Forward Hold:** The forward storage hold — which used to be the ordnance bay — has an acoustic dead spot near the starboard wall. Sound just stops there. New crew always notice. Old crew don't talk about it.
- **She Wants to Run:** The agility is unremarkable, but the engines were built to outrun warships, and the pilot can feel it. Push her in a straight line and she comes alive in a way she never does in a turn.

### Crew Roles
- **Captain** — Inherits the ship's name and the debts that come with it. Needs to be someone comfortable making decisions about cargo they may not have inspected.
- **Pilot** — The agility is mediocre but the straight-line speed is exceptional; this pilot has to know when to run and when not to try fancy.
- **First Mate / Quartermaster** — Manages the converted hold, which has odd dimensions thanks to the demilitarization, and handles the manifests that need to look one way to port authority and another way to the crew.
- **Mechanic** — Inherits twenty years of military-grade systems shoved into a civilian configuration. Has to know two trades: Alliance engineering and the dark art of pretending they don't.
- **Gunner (optional, contested)** — The old turret mounts can be reactivated. Whether they should be is an ongoing argument aboard.
- **Doctor or Medic** — The infirmary is small but, oddly, still has its original military trauma-bay fittings. Useful. Also a tell.

### Current Situation
She's docked at Cribbage Station in the Kalidasa system, where station boss **Eibhlín Asante** holds paper on the captain for a job that went sideways eight months back. Asante hasn't said what she wants yet — only that the ship isn't to leave until they've had a conversation, and that conversation keeps getting postponed. In the meantime, *Narrow Escape* sits in Bay Nine burning fees she can't pay, her crew drifting between the cantina and the cargo hold, her engines warm and ready and forbidden from lighting. There's a sealed crate in the Cold Room that the captain hasn't told the rest of the crew about. There's a buyer asking quiet questions about the ship herself. And Asante's people walk past the bay twice a day, counting.

### GM Notes
*The sealed crate in the Cold Room is the unfinished business from Captain Sato's last Alliance contract — the cargo she refused to deliver, that Jirayu hid, that's been quietly riding aboard this ship for over a decade without the current captain knowing what it is. Asante has figured out it's here; that's what the "debt" is really about. When the crate is opened — and it will be, because Asante will eventually send someone to take it — the crew learns that *Narrow Escape* has been carrying evidence of an Alliance war-crime cover-up since before half of them were flying. Suddenly the heavy doors and the milspec hull and the engines built to outrun warships aren't quirks anymore. They're what's going to keep the crew alive.*
