## **Mary Okafor-Vance**
*Salvage broker working the Beaumonde docks — known up and down the strip as "One-Eyed Mary," and she'll tell you the story before you ask.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Appears to be** | An independent salvage broker matching wrecks to crews willing to haul them, taking a finder's cut. |
| **Actually is** | *(revealed only in Truth 2, 3, or 4 below)* |

**Appearance:** A woman in her fifties in a clean but much-mended dock coat, missing the left eye — no patch, no glass, just the scarred socket left open like she wants you to look. She keeps her hands flat on the table when she talks, fingers spread, and there's old engine grease worked so deep into the knuckles it's never coming out. The right eye does all the work and it does plenty.

**The Pitch:** *"Sit down, sit down — and yes, you can look, everybody does, gets it out of the way. Now. There's a Firefly-class transport went silent six months back, the* Mei Hua*, came down hard in the badlands east of New Dunsmuir. Registry says she was hauling agricultural equipment, which means tractors and seed drills and other honest dull things that are worth real coin to the right buyer out on the Rim. I got coordinates, I got the manifest, I got nobody fool enough to fly out there and crack her open. That's where you come in, dong ma?"*

**The Job:** Fly to the crash site of the *Mei Hua* in the Beaumonde badlands (two days out from the city, rough country, no roads). Strip the cargo hold, recover what's salvageable, bring it back to a warehouse Mary holds the lease on. She wants it done inside ten days because the salvage claim window with the local magistrate closes after that.

**The Payment:** Four hundred platinum on delivery, plus the crew keeps any personal effects or non-manifest cargo they find aboard. For a ship burning through fuel cells and behind on a Persephone berth fee, that's three good months of breathing room.

**The Truth (GM rolls 1d4 in secret — only one is real):**

1. **Straightforward** — The *Mei Hua* is exactly where Mary says, and the cargo is exactly what the manifest claims. The complication is the badlands themselves: a clan of scrappers calling themselves the Red Dust Boys has been picking the wreck slow for weeks, hauling pieces back to their camp. They're not organized enough to take the whole ship but they'll absolutely fight a crew trying to take *their* find.

2. **One Layer Down** — There's a body still aboard. The *Mei Hua's* engineer, a man named Tomas Reyes, didn't make it out, and his sister back on Persephone has been pestering Mary for months about recovering remains. Mary didn't mention it because she figures spacers get squeamish, and the body's been in a sealed compartment for half a year. The sister's offering a separate reward — but only if she finds out he's there.

3. **The Real Story** — The "agricultural equipment" includes two crates of unregistered Blue Sun chemical precursors that were being moved off-books. Mary knows. She's not the buyer — she's the middleman for a small-time chem cook on Whittier who lost his last courier and can't afford another. She figured if she told the crew it was tractors they'd just deliver tractors and never ask. The Alliance customs inspector who's been sniffing around her warehouse is closer than she thinks.

4. **The Reversal** — The *Mei Hua* didn't crash. She was forced down by Mary's old partner six months ago in a dispute over a debt, and the crew aboard — six people, including two kids belonging to the captain — were left to walk out of the badlands. They didn't all make it. The survivors have been rebuilding the ship piece by piece, living in the wreck, and Mary wants it cleared out before they can fly her again and come looking for the people who put them there. The "salvage" is a family's last home, and the crew is the eviction.

**Why They'd Take It:** Four hundred platinum is real money, the job sounds clean on paper, and Beaumonde's a long way from anyone who'd care about a wreck in the badlands. If the pilot or the mechanic has been worrying about fuel cells or a slipping compression coil, this pays for the fix with margin left over.

**Connection:** **Hiroshi "Hosh" Bello**, a bartender at the Maidenhead who's known Mary since before she lost the eye. Ask him and he'll say: *"Mary deals straight more often than not, but she's been jumpy these last two months — keeps looking at the door, keeps paying her tab the same night she runs it. Whatever this salvage job is, it ain't just salvage to her. She wants it done fast for a reason, and Mary don't usually want anything fast."*
