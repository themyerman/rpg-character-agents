## **Hollow Tine**
*A tin-roofed sorghum town on the high prairie of a Rim moon, gone quiet since the factory ship stopped its monthly run.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Rim world settlement, pop. ~340, two days from nearest Alliance cruiser |
| **Condition** | Bleeding out slow — one month since the buyer didn't show |

### Atmosphere
The wind comes off the flats carrying the sweet-sour smell of sorghum mash gone too long in the silos, and underneath that, the iron tang of a town not quite ready to admit it's dying. The main street is three blocks of pressed-dirt road between corrugated buildings, and at the far end sits a Dragonfly-class hauler called the *Vagrant Son*, parked on the public pad where no ship has any business being parked for six days running. Nobody's looking at her directly, which is the wrong thing — on a Rim world this hungry, a strange ship should have a half-dozen kids hanging off her landing struts by now.

### Notable NPCs

- **Mika Alemu** (acting town reeve, formerly the schoolmistress) — wants the *Vagrant Son* gone before whatever it's carrying brings the Feds down on a town that can't afford their attention; will trade information, fuel, even silence for the right outcome.
- **Kweku Attah** (captain of the *Vagrant Son*, sweating) — sitting on a hold full of pre-war Independent military medical supplies he lifted off a derelict supply cache, trying to sell them piece by piece to a town that genuinely needs them but can't pay; he's been broadcasting a coded ID transponder he doesn't know is twenty years out of date and very, very flagged.
- **Nashoba Swifthawk** (foreman of the silo cooperative, Browncoat veteran) — recognized the crates the moment Attah cracked one open; wants the medicine for his people, knows the cost, and is quietly deciding whether to warn Attah, rob him, or sell him to the Alliance scout that came through asking questions four days ago.

### The Situation
The Pao-Yu Combine pulled their factory ship's route last month — corporate restructuring, two lines on a memo somewhere on Persephone — and Hollow Tine's entire sorghum harvest is sitting in silos with no buyer and no shelf life left to spare. Into this walks Kweku Attah with a hold full of expired-but-still-good Independent battlefield medicine: antibiotics, plasma, the works. He's selling them honest-cheap because he's scared, and the town's buying because three kids have lung-rot and the nearest licensed clinic is a week's ride. Problem one: those crates are stamped with serial numbers that flag on any Alliance manifest scan, and Attah's old transponder has been singing them across the black for six days. Problem two: an Alliance recon skiff already passed through, asked polite questions, and left. It will be back, and it will not be alone. Problem three: Nashoba Swifthawk knows exactly what Attah has because he helped load crates just like them off a hospital ship at Du-Khang in '11, and the war is not over in his chest.

### Hooks
1. The party puts down for fuel and the fuel-master (Mika) meets them on the ramp with a stack of script and a quiet ask: *"Get that Dragonfly off my pad. I'll pay you in sorghum or favors, your pick, but she leaves tonight."*
2. A boy of maybe nine sprints up to the party in the street, presses a folded paper into the nearest hand — a medical inventory list, with prices crossed out and lower prices written in — and runs off before anyone can ask who sent him. The handwriting matches a sign on Kweku's ship.
3. Nashoba finds the party in the town's one bar, sets down a bottle of something homemade, and asks flat: *"You know what a Pattern-7 field kit looks like? 'Cause there's a man down the street selling them out of a cargo hold, and I want to know if he stole 'em from the dead or just found 'em."*

### GM Notes
Play Hollow Tine slow and tired — every NPC is exhausted and making bad choices because the good choices ran out a month ago. The Alliance scout returning is a clock, not a threat to negotiate with; once it's overhead, the window closes. Keep ambiguous whether Kweku actually killed anyone to get the medicine or just got very lucky in a graveyard of ships — let the party decide what they believe, and let Nashoba's verdict turn on what they say to him in the bar.
