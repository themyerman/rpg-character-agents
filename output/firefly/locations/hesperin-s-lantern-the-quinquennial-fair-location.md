## **Hesperin's Lantern — The Quinquennial Fair**
*A border-moon festival that only wakes once every five years, and this year it woke up hungry.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Border-moon festival town, half the 'Verse passing through |
| **Condition** | A factory ship stopped buying ore a month ago — the rot is just starting to show |

### Atmosphere
Lantern-paper kites in twelve languages strung between the prefab grain silos, and beneath them the smell of fry-oil, ox dung, and the faint metal tang of unrefined platinum dust that gets into everything on Hesperin. A brass band three drinks deep is playing a war song from the wrong side, and nobody's stopping them. The crowd is enormous — too enormous, really, for the number of food carts still serving; half the stalls have hand-painted *CLOSED — NO STOCK* signs, and the merchants who *are* open are smiling a little too wide.

### Notable NPCs
- **Hotah Björnsson** (Festival Reeve and former Independent quartermaster) — Wants the fair to go off clean so the moon doesn't starve this winter; is quietly running a black ledger of who owes whom since the *Sihnon Star* factory ship pulled its contract, and would pay handsomely for someone to find out *why* it stopped buying.
- **Ayasha Greywolf** (Registered Companion, traveling on sabbatical) — Came for the festival, recognized a voice on a dead-channel transmission last night, and now won't leave her room at the Lantern House; she knew someone aboard the *Far From Home*, the courier that was reported lost with all hands eighteen months ago over Hera.
- **Shepherd Rahul Chatterjee** (Itinerant clergyman with a federal marshal's badge in his sock drawer) — Says he's here to bless the harvest-stones; is actually tracking a name on a warrant, and has not yet decided whether the crew is help, hindrance, or quarry.

### The Situation
Hesperin's Lantern exists because of the festival — the moon's platinum tailings aren't worth shipping out except when the *Sihnon Star* swings through every five years to buy in bulk, and the fair grew up around that windfall. Except this cycle, the factory ship signaled a polite refusal from three systems out and changed course. Nobody knows why. The Reeve is bleeding credit to keep stalls open and crowds fed, debts are being called in across six guilds, and a lot of armed travelers are about to discover their festival money is worth half what it was yesterday. Into this powder keg drops a faint, looping distress ping from the *Far From Home* — a ship the Alliance officially logged as destroyed by Reaver action in '17. The signal's coming from somewhere on this moon. Someone aboard her was carrying something the *Sihnon Star* didn't want to be anywhere near, and the Shepherd-who-isn't has a list of names that overlaps with the crew manifest.

### Hooks
1. A skinny boy of maybe twelve, paid in festival scrip, presses a cortex chit into a crewmember's hand and runs — it's a fifteen-second loop of a man's voice saying *"This is* Far From Home *— we are not lost, repeat, we are not lost"* — and Companion Ayasha Greywolf is watching from a second-floor window when they look up.
2. Reeve Björnsson approaches the crew openly in the beer-tent: two thousand platinum, half now, to fly to the last known coordinates of the *Sihnon Star* and find out what spooked her off — he needs an answer before the festival's third night or the moon loses its winter.
3. Shepherd Chatterjee sits down uninvited at the crew's table, orders a round, and says he's been told the captain is a person of *interesting acquaintance* — then asks, mildly, whether anyone aboard ever served on a Tohoku-class courier, and watches their faces.

### GM Notes
Run the festival as a pressure cooker — every scene should have crowd noise, a vendor closing up, or a tense conversation at the next table. The three threads (the dead ship, the factory ship, the lawman) are the same thread; the *Far From Home* was carrying a witness or a cargo the *Sihnon Star*'s owners want buried, and pulling the festival contract was leverage to make Hesperin's people *find* it for them. Keep the Shepherd's true allegiance ambiguous as long as you can — he might be hunting the crew, the witness, or the corporation, and he genuinely hasn't decided which fight is God's work. The signal from the *Providence*-class wreck (which is what the *Far From Home* really is, repainted) should stay just out of reach until the crew picks a side.
