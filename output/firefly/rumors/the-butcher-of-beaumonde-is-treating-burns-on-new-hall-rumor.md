## **The Butcher of Beaumonde Is Treating Burns on New Hall**

*A name-and-face rumor making its way through the Rim moon circuit, the kind that turns a quiet country doctor into something much worse.*

### As Heard

> "Listen — my cousin's wife works the dispatch desk at the Beylix relay, and she heard it straight from the Fed marshal who filed the paper. There's a doctor out on New Hall calls himself Khalid Saleh, real polite fella, delivers babies and everything — but his real name's on an Alliance warrant from the war. They call him the Butcher of Beaumonde. Cut up Alliance prisoners during the war, did experiments. Been hiding in plain sight nine years and nobody knew."

*Source: A freight loader named Posey at the Eavesdown docks, who heard it from a deckhand off the* Mei Lin *who heard it from somebody's cousin. Posey's telling it now because a New Hall trader just paid him in coin stamped with Saleh's clinic mark, and Posey wants to know if it's blood money before he spends it.*

### What's Actually True

Dr. Khalid Saleh is real, the warrant is real, and he was at Beaumonde during the war — the facts check out. But he was a Browncoat field surgeon who triaged Alliance wounded *and* his own under fire, and the warrant exists because he refused to surrender a field hospital full of Independent casualties when ordered. The "experiments" line came from an Alliance press officer trying to justify the shelling of his aid station, which killed forty-six people including most of his staff. He fled to the Rim and has been quietly patching up miners and farmers ever since. The marshal who filed the warrant is dead. Nobody is actively hunting him — until now, when somebody hears this rumor and decides there's bounty in it.

### The Danger

If the crew takes this to the Alliance expecting a payday, they'll find the warrant is technically active but unfunded — no bounty, just paperwork — and they'll have personally outed a beloved community doctor to a bureaucracy that *will* eventually come collect him. New Hall settlers, who owe Saleh their children's lives, will remember the ship that did it. If the crew confronts Saleh as a war criminal, they'll find a fifty-eight-year-old man with arthritic hands who won't deny anything because explaining feels like an insult to his dead. And somewhere out there, a bounty broker named Kweku Adjei is already hearing the same rumor through different ears.

### Hooks

1. The crew puts down at New Hall for a routine resupply and a child runs up asking if they're "here for the doctor" — the settlement is already nervous, already organizing, already armed.
2. Kweku Adjei approaches the crew on Persephone offering a finder's fee for confirming Saleh's location. He has the warrant printout. He does not have the context.
3. Saleh himself, having heard the rumor reach New Hall, hires the crew for what he calls "a quiet passage off-world" — he won't say why, and he's bringing a locked medical case he refuses to let anyone touch.

### GM Notes

Play Saleh as exhausted, not heroic — a man who stopped explaining himself a long time ago and is tired of being grateful he survived. Escalate by having the rumor reach New Hall *before* the crew does, so they walk into a community already deciding what to do about it. Keep ambiguous whether anything in that locked medical case is actually incriminating, or just the last photographs of people he couldn't save.
