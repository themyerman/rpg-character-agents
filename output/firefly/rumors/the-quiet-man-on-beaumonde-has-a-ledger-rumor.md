## **The Quiet Man on Beaumonde Has a Ledger**

*A rumor passing through dockside fixers about an Alliance deserter peddling something he claims could topple a Core admiral — and the price he's asking has people nervous.*

### As Heard

> "Listen — there's a fella holed up above a noodle place in the Hsien-Tien district, calls himself Magnus Yupanqui. Used to be supply corps on the *Brigadier-class*. Says he's got a ledger — actual paper, not a wave — names every officer who skimmed off the Hera relief shipments after Serenity Valley. He's not selling it to the highest bidder, see, he's *waiting* for someone. Someone Browncoat. Got to be a sting, don't it? Has to be."

*Source: A cargo broker named Parvati Rao who heard it from a dockworker who heard it from the noodle shop's dishwasher. She's telling you because she thinks the bounty for flipping the deserter is cleaner money than whatever the ledger's worth.*

### What's Actually True

Magnus Yupanqui is real, he did desert, and the ledger is genuine — every name, every diverted crate, every starving Independent settlement that never saw their grain. But he isn't waiting for a Browncoat buyer and it isn't a sting. He's waiting to *die*. He has a degenerative condition the Alliance medical corps wouldn't treat after his discharge papers came through irregular, and he's been sitting on the ledger for eight months trying to find someone he trusts to *publish* it, not sell it. He doesn't want money. He wants a witness. The "waiting for a Browncoat" detail came from him asking the dishwasher, in a fever, whether anyone who fought at Du-Khang still drank in the neighborhood. The facts traveled. The reason did not.

### The Danger

If the party walks in offering creds or pretending to be Independent veterans looking to buy, Magnus clocks the pitch immediately and clams up — he's been refusing offers for months and every one confirms his fear that the ledger will be bought and buried. Worse, Parvati Rao is already arranging to sell the *location* to an Alliance Internal Affairs contact named Winona Greywolf who's been quietly cleaning up loose ends from the Hera scandal for two years. Greywolf doesn't want the ledger recovered. She wants Magnus dead before he gives it to anyone, and she has a four-day head start on whoever the party thinks they're racing.

### Hooks

1. The party arrives at the noodle place to find Magnus mid-collapse on the stairs — he's worse than the rumor suggested, and any plan that assumed weeks of negotiation now has hours. He won't hand over the ledger until someone convinces him they'll actually *use* it.
2. Parvati Rao approaches the party directly, offering to "split the flip" — turn Magnus in together. Refusing tips her off; accepting means meeting Winona Greywolf, who is sharper and more polite than anyone wants her to be.
3. A second buyer surfaces: a Core journalist with a Cortex following who absolutely would publish the ledger — and absolutely would also get Magnus killed in the process, possibly on purpose, because the martyr story sells better than the survivor.

### GM Notes

The whole rumor pivots on the misread motive — let the party operate on the "he's selling" assumption for as long as possible, and reward the player who actually *talks* to Magnus instead of negotiating with him. Keep ambiguous whether the ledger, once public, would actually accomplish anything; some of those officers are now powerful enough that exposure might just mean a new scandal buried under a louder one.
