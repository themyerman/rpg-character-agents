## **Vroornk-striel — First Contact**
*A Scout Service first-contact team has already come and gone; the party arrives to find a pack of whale-sized leviathans hiding in the deep atmosphere, broadcasting infrasonic prayers no one has yet learned to hear.*

### Species Profile
- **Body plan**: Radial three-fold symmetry, limbless and serpentine, whale-scale (5,000kg+)
- **Primary sense**: Electromagnetic — they read fields and current the way humans read faces
- **Diet**: Grazer — instinctive posture is flight or freeze, never aggression-first
- **Social structure**: Small kin-pack of 3–8 — intimate, defensive, no formal institutions to negotiate with
- **Communication**: Infrasonic, far below human hearing — completely inaudible without equipment; speech attempts so far have produced no result
- **Tech level**: TL 8 — early industrial-spaceflight; they are not peers, but they are close enough that Non-Interference Protocol does **not** shield them
- **Lifespan**: Human-range (60–100 years)
- **Cognition**: Emergent hive — no individual decides; the pack thinks together as a distributed mind

### The Contact Situation
- **How it happened**: Scout Service first-contact team made initial entry, filed preliminary reports, and rotated out. The party is the follow-up — sent to build on whatever foundation was laid (or to inherit whatever mess was left).
- **Initial posture**: **Fearful.** Grazer instinct meeting whale-scale anxiety — they hide, they retreat into deep atmosphere, they freeze when cornered. The pack is not hostile; it is terrified, and a terrified 5,000kg organism is dangerous by accident.
- **What they want**: **Trade.** Despite the fear, something in the Scout team's first contact registered as opportunity. They want exchange — likely materials or knowledge their TL 8 industry cannot produce.
- **Who can speak for them**: No one and everyone. The pack thinks as a unit; there is no chief to bargain with. Proposals must be presented to the whole pack and allowed to settle into consensus — slowly, over infrasonic timescales measured in hours, not minutes.

### Imperial Context
- **Non-interference protocol**: **Does not apply.** The Vroornk-striel are approaching spaceflight; the Imperium treats them as proto-peers. Contact is legal, trade is legal, and — critically — so is exploitation by any party that gets there first.
- **Stake**: **Military / strategic value.** Naval Intelligence has flagged either the system's position or something the species *knows* as tactically significant. The moment they finalize the assessment, Scout Service jurisdiction ends and Naval Intelligence takes over. The party is operating on borrowed time.

### Complications
- **The pack thinks the party are gods, demons, ancestors, or figures from prophecy.** Something the Scout team did — their arrival pattern, their EM signature, the configuration of their ship — matches a Vroornk-striel myth. The pack is offering trade the way one offers tribute. Every request will be interpreted as a divine test. Refusing gifts is blasphemy; accepting them is fraud.
- **Infrasonic comms gear is not standard issue.** The Scout team took theirs with them. The party has hours of fumbling with field-rigged sensor arrays before they can hear, let alone speak.
- **Naval Intelligence is in transit.** A courier or cruiser is inbound. When it arrives, the party's mandate evaporates and any promises they've made become someone else's problem — or someone else's leverage.
- **The pack's EM-sense reads the party's ship, weapons, and reactors clearly.** Lies about capability are impossible. Concealed sidearms are not concealed.

### Running This Encounter
The core tension is **time and authenticity**: the party has a short window to establish honest trade with a frightened hive-mind that mistakes them for gods, before Naval Intelligence arrives and converts the whole relationship into a strategic asset. What the players don't know yet is *what* Naval Intelligence wants — the system, or something the Vroornk-striel know — and whether the "prophecy" the pack believes is coincidence or something the Scout team deliberately seeded. The decision this encounter forces: **do you correct the theology, or do you use it?** Either choice has a cost, and the pack — thinking as one — will remember whichever answer you give.
