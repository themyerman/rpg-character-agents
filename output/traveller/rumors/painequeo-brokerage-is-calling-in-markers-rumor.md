## **Painequeo Brokerage Is Calling In Markers**

*A whispered claim that a mid-tier merchant house is desperately collecting old favours after backing the losing side in a recent subsector political quarrel.*

### As Heard

> "Listen — keep this between us. Hana Painequeo's people are on every concourse from here to Rhylanor cashing in markers. *Old* markers. She bet the house on the Tukera-aligned bloc in the Imbrium customs vote and it cratered. I've seen three small carriers reroute cargo on her say-so this week. When Painequeo Brokerage starts spending goodwill like that, the receivers are about to come for what's left."

*Source: A factor named Mila Morozov, two drinks deep at a startown freight bar, telling a colleague — loudly enough that the table behind her, which she did not check, heard every word.*

### What's Actually True

Painequeo Brokerage is fine. Hana Painequeo did *not* back the losing bloc — she stayed scrupulously neutral on the Imbrium customs vote and is quietly profiting from the fallout. The rumor was seeded six weeks ago by Kostya Tula, a rival factor who lost a contract to her and wanted to spook her counterparties. The problem: enough small operators heard it, *believed* it, and have already pulled lines of credit, refused her cargo, or sold off her paper at a discount. Painequeo doesn't yet know why her partners are flinching. When she finds out, she will be furious — and she has the resources to make it expensive for whoever is still repeating it.

### The Danger

Anyone who acts on this — refusing Painequeo cargo, dumping her promissory notes cheap, or trying to "buy in" while she's supposedly distressed — is committing to a position based on a lie. The notes are good. The cargo is profitable. And Hana Painequeo is methodically building a list of every counterparty who treated her as damaged goods. That list will become a blacklist within the season. The party may also find themselves on the receiving end of a defamation inquiry from a house lawyer who is *very* interested in tracing the rumor's chain of custody back to its source.

### Hooks

1. A nervous shipper offers the party a Painequeo-paper cargo contract at sixty percent of face value, "because nobody else will touch it." It's a perfectly good contract. Taking it puts the party on Painequeo's grateful-creditor list — and on Tula's enemies list.
2. A woman in an unmarked suit approaches the party in port and offers a flat 5,000 Cr per name: who told them about Painequeo's troubles, and who they told in turn. She works for Painequeo's house counsel.
3. Kostya Tula, realizing his rumor has overshot, hires the party for an unrelated milk run — actually a pretext to get them off-world before Painequeo's investigators interview them.

### GM Notes

Play this as a social/economic trap, not a combat one — the danger is reputational and financial, and the most interesting outcome is the party realizing partway through that *they are the rumor's next link in the chain.* Keep ambiguous whether Tula knows his lie has spiraled this far; he may be panicking or he may be doubling down with a second seed rumor.
