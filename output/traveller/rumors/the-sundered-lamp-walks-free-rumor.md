## **The *Sundered Lamp* Walks Free**
*A dock-bar tip-off about a free trader operating openly under a name that — supposedly — has an unserved Imperial warrant attached to it.*

### As Heard
> "You know the *Sundered Lamp*? Type-A out of berth nine, runs the spinward circuit? Mahlet at the harbourmaster's office pulled its filing for me — there's a sealed Ministry of Justice flag on the hull registry, six years old, never executed. Whoever brings that warrant home gets the recovery bond on a 400-ton hull, and the crew's been sleeping in port for three nights. Someone moves quick, that's a ship and a finder's fee both."

*Source: Astrid Catrileo, a freelance recovery agent nursing the same drink for an hour at the Concourse Tap. She's telling everyone who'll listen because she can't act on it herself — and she wants someone else to spring the trap so she can read the room.*

### What's Actually True
The warrant is real, unserved, and exactly as described. The *Sundered Lamp* is wanted for the murder of an Imperial Ministry courier six years ago in a neighbouring subsector. The ship's captain, Rimac Catrileo (Astrid's estranged half-brother, a detail she is not sharing), bought his way into a quiet arrangement with a SuSAG factor who needs a deniable hull for sensitive cargo runs. SuSAG corporate security has been quietly suppressing the warrant at every port the *Lamp* visits — bribed harbourmasters, "lost" filings, the works. Mahlet Mehari at the harbourmaster's office is not bribed; she's new, and she pulled the record because Astrid asked nicely. That filing is now flagged in a system SuSAG monitors.

### The Danger
Anyone who moves to execute the warrant is moving against SuSAG, not against a lone free trader. The recovery bond is real but will never be paid — the paperwork will vanish into Ministry channels and emerge as a counter-charge of interference with a sealed investigation. Meanwhile, Mahlet's terminal access has already pinged a SuSAG watch-list; she has roughly seventy-two hours before something unpleasant happens to her career, her apartment, or her. Astrid knows enough to suspect this and is using the party as a tripwire to see who comes out of the woodwork.

### Hooks
1. The party approaches the *Sundered Lamp* at berth and finds Rimac Catrileo polite, unhurried, and quietly confident — he offers them a charter at twice the going rate to leave the system tonight, no questions, and his crew are armed under their jackets.
2. Mahlet Mehari contacts the party in a panic the next morning: two men in unmarked coveralls have been "auditing" her office, her access has been revoked, and she wants out of the system on the first hull leaving.
3. Astrid corners one of the PCs alone and admits a partial version of the truth — she'll split the bond if they bring her brother in alive, because she thinks SuSAG will kill him the moment he stops being useful, and she's running out of time to extract him.

### GM Notes
Play this as a rumor that gets *more* dangerous the more the party verifies it — every records pull is another flag on a corporate watch-list. Escalate by having SuSAG security arrive in-system within a day or two of party interest, polite and well-dressed, asking the party to "clarify their intentions." Leave ambiguous whether Rimac actually killed the courier or was framed into the warrant by SuSAG itself to ensure his cooperation — both versions should remain plausible until the party forces an answer.
