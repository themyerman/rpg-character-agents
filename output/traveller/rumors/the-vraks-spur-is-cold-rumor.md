## **The Vraks Spur Is Cold**
*A whispered warning circulating among free traders and small-ship crews about a Scout-maintained jump route that "isn't there anymore" — but is still on every astrogation cache in the subsector.*

### As Heard
> "Listen — Tsion told me himself before he stopped flying. The Vraks Spur, that little three-jump dogleg the Scouts cleared off the main lane? It's *cold*. No tender, no beacon refresh in eight months. He saw a Type S sitting derelict at the midpoint and the IISS won't say a word. Don't ask, don't file a flight plan through it, and for the Emperor's sake don't take a cargo contract that routes you that way. It's not your problem. Walk away."

*Source: Coya Quilaqueo, a bonded cargo broker at the startown bar, who heard it from a former Scout courier pilot named Tsion Kiros. She's repeating it now because a shipping agent just offered her an unusually generous commission to route a client through the Spur, and it spooked her.*

### What's Actually True
The IISS quietly decommissioned the Vraks Spur seven months ago after a survey team failed to return and a follow-up found anomalous jump-mass readings at the midpoint system — the kind that suggest something is interdicting traffic. Rather than post a formal Amber Zone (which requires Moot notification and an investigation budget), the Scout Service simply stopped maintaining the route and let it fall off updated charts. Old astrogation data still shows it as clear. A small circle of senior Scouts, one Naval Intelligence liaison, and at least one shipping cartel know — and the cartel is now selling "discount routing" through the Spur to brokers, collecting insurance payouts on ships that don't come back. Tsion Kiros figured it out and went to ground.

### The Danger
A party that treats this as "stay away" advice misses that someone is *actively funneling ships into the Spur for profit*. A party that investigates the Spur directly will jump into whatever killed the survey team — the readings suggest a derelict-era automated picket or something stranger, and a 100-ton free trader is not equipped for first contact with it. The middle path — tracing the broker network — puts them across from a cartel that has already paid to make one Scout disappear and has the manifests of every ship that's taken the bait.

### Hooks
1. The shipping agent who spooked Coya approaches the party directly with a "time-sensitive" charter through the Spur, offering double rate and a 5,000 Cr signing bonus in hard credits. The cargo manifest lists agricultural machinery; the actual crates mass wrong.
2. Tsion Kiros surfaces in the same startown, drunk and broke, looking for a berth off-world *tonight*. He'll trade what he knows for passage anywhere coreward — but he's being watched, and whoever picks him up inherits the watchers.
3. A distress beacon on a salvage frequency pings from the Spur's midpoint system. It's two months old, automated, and the registry belongs to a ship whose insurance was paid out six weeks ago by an underwriter linked to the cartel.

### GM Notes
Run this as a choice between three scales of trouble: the broker scam (criminal/investigative), the rescue of Tsion (chase/extraction), or the Spur itself (deep-space horror). Let the party think they've picked the safe one and discover the threads connect. Keep what's actually killing ships at the midpoint ambiguous — a Zhodani black globe picket, an Ancients artifact, a rogue defense AI from the Long Night — pick when the party gets close enough to matter, and not before.
