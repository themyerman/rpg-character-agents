## **The Ghosthounds Are Buying**
*A whispered rumor circulating in startown bars about a Vargr mercenary company aggressively recruiting for a job they refuse to describe.*

### As Heard
> "The Ghosthounds — Aekhuusakhug's outfit, you know the ones — they're hiring out of three ports now. Triple normal rate, half up front in hard credits, but nobody's saying what the contract is or where it ships from. I talked to a gunner who took the bond last week. He said the recruiter, some Vargr called Roegz, didn't even ask about his combat record. Just wanted to know if he had family who'd come looking. That's not a mercenary contract, friend. That's something else."

*Source: Kofi Sarpong, a between-berths free trader engineer who heard it from a cousin in the Ghosthounds' medical screening pool, and is repeating it because he's been told — not asked — to repeat it, by someone who paid his bar tab for the week.*

### What's Actually True
The Ghosthounds *are* hiring, but at normal rates for a perfectly legal anti-piracy sweep contracted by a subsector trade combine. The aggressive, sinister-sounding recruitment story is a deliberate plant by a rival broker, Karim Farouk, who lost the bid and is salting the rumor pool to scare off quality applicants and embarrass the Ghosthounds' command. He's paying small-timers like Sarpong in drinks and favors to keep the whisper moving. The "no family" question Sarpong's cousin reported was a routine next-of-kin liability waiver, retold with menace. The rumor is doing real damage: two veteran applicants have already withdrawn.

### The Danger
If the party investigates the Ghosthounds expecting a black operation, they'll find a boring legitimate outfit and waste a week of port fees. Worse, if they start asking pointed questions in the wrong bars, Farouk's people will assume they're working *for* the Ghosthounds' counter-intelligence and respond accordingly — at minimum a beating, at maximum a frame-up involving planted contraband. Meanwhile, the actual Ghosthounds command, captain Aekhuusakhug Roegz, is becoming aware she's being slandered and is hunting for the source herself. Anyone caught repeating the rumor near her people had better have a name to give up.

### Hooks
1. The party is offered the recruitment bond themselves — straight up, no menace — and must decide whether the rumors are reason to refuse a legitimate, well-paid gig.
2. A nervous fixer approaches the party offering 2,000 Cr to "verify" the rumor by getting close to the Ghosthounds. The fixer works for Farouk and wants deniable corroboration to feed the next round of whispers.
3. Aekhuusakhug Roegz herself finds the party (she's read the bar gossip logs the port authority sells) and offers payment for the name of whoever started the rumor — politely, but with two armed kin standing behind her.

### GM Notes
Play the first scene exactly as the rumor describes — let the players lean in to the conspiracy — then let the truth surface through contradictions: the Ghosthounds' books are public, their contract is filed with the subsector trade office, and Roegz is more wounded than dangerous. Keep Farouk's *actual* motive ambiguous: spite over the lost bid is the surface answer, but he may also be working off a debt to someone who wants the Ghosthounds specifically discredited — leave that thread for later if the players pull it.
