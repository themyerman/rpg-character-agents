### Description
She has the wedge-and-bubble silhouette of every Scout you've ever seen, but the matte grey IISS paint has been sanded down to bare hull along the dorsal spine where someone cut access panels that don't appear on any factory diagram. Inside, the air smells faintly of citrus polymer — a civilian deodorizer fighting a losing war against three years of jump-drive ozone. The galley deck has *four* stateroom doors instead of the standard two, and the cargo bay is noticeably shorter than her tonnage promises; the aft bulkhead is welded, not bolted, and the welds are good but recent. Every surface in the cockpit has been touched a thousand times by the same pair of hands — the worn spots on the throttle, the armrest, the manual jump initiator all line up with a single pilot's habits.

### History
She rolled off the Mora yards three standard years ago as a stock IISS detached-duty courier, assigned briefly to deep survey work along the Spinward Marches before being released to her first registered owner, **Fatima Cloudwalker**, a retired Scout who took her detachment bonus and a J-4 hull and went looking for the kind of work that pays a J-4 hull. Cloudwalker ran her hard for eighteen months — long jumps, thin margins, the occasional bonded courier contract that nobody asked questions about. Somewhere in that time, at a yard nobody can quite name, she paid an engineer called **Manqué Puma** to do the modification: four tons of cargo space sacrificed for two additional staterooms, the bulkhead moved forward, the manifest never updated. Whether she was running passengers, fugitives, or something stranger, the paperwork doesn't say. The paperwork doesn't say anything.

Cloudwalker sold her — or appeared to sell her — through a broker named **Tunde Asamoah** at Rhylanor Highport six months ago. The bill of sale clears customs inspection but doesn't quite clear a forensic accountant; the price was low, the buyer was a shell, and Cloudwalker hasn't filed a flight plan anywhere in Imperial space since. *Spinward Star* changed hands twice more in quick succession before landing with her current operators, who got a J-4 hull for the price of a J-2 and didn't ask why.

### Quirks
- **The Jump Chorus:** At the moment of jump entry, the drive harmonic rises through a register that sounds — to every human ear that's heard it — like distant voices speaking just below the threshold of comprehension. Engineers have stopped recording it. New crew always try. Veterans put their headphones on thirty seconds before transition.
- **The Fifth Door:** One of the four staterooms has a second access hatch behind the closet panel, opening into what should be cargo space but isn't quite. The hatch is unpowered; you have to know it's there. Cloudwalker knew.
- **The Welded Bulkhead:** The aft cargo wall reads as solid on sensors but rings hollow when struck near the upper port corner. The crew has agreed not to cut it open. The agreement is renewed, silently, every time someone walks past it.
- **Throttle Memory:** The maneuver throttle has a hitch at 60% thrust — not a fault, a worn detent from someone who flew her at that exact setting for a thousand hours. New pilots overshoot until they learn it. After a month, they fly at 60% without thinking.
- **Clean Logs:** Her astrogation logs are immaculate going back exactly six months. Before that: nothing. Not corrupted — *absent*, with the timestamps cleanly rewritten. A competent forensic tech would notice in ten minutes. Nobody competent has looked.

### Crew Roles
- **Pilot/Captain** — She's a J-4 hull, which means she can outrun almost anything in civilian service and reach places most traders can't. The captain on this ship is making a choice every time they file a route: take the legitimate long-courier work that justifies the drive, or take the *other* work that justifies the extra staterooms.
- **Astrogator** — Plotting J-4 jumps is real work, and she has the fuel for exactly one before she's grounded. An astrogator on the *Spinward Star* needs to be good, and needs to be comfortable not asking what's in the welded bulkhead.
- **Engineer** — One person, because there's only room for one. Whoever takes the post inherits Manqué Puma's modifications and the harmonic in the drive. They will, eventually, work out what the voices are saying. Whether they tell anyone is their business.
- **Steward (optional)** — Four staterooms means up to six passengers in double occupancy. A steward on this ship is also, functionally, the person who decides which passengers get the stateroom with the second door.

### Current Situation
She's berthed at Rhylanor Highport, Bay 14-C, under a lien filed three weeks ago by a holding company called **Aramis Bonded Transit, LLC** — an entity none of the current crew has ever heard of, contracted with, or signed paper for. The lien is for Cr 340,000, which is suspiciously close to what the welded bulkhead would be worth if you opened it and the contents were what someone thinks they are. Port Authority has impounded her pending civil hearing in forty days. The crew can pay the lien (if they can find the money), contest it (if they want to be deposed about her history), or move her quietly (if they can clear the highport without filing a flight plan, which a J-4 Scout actually *can*). Someone is going to make a decision in the next two weeks. Someone else is watching to see which.

### GM Notes
*The welded bulkhead contains a cold-sleep low berth — one occupant, IISS-issue, manifest scrubbed. The occupant is a witness Fatima Cloudwalker was moving to a safehouse on Regina and never delivered, because somewhere along the route she realized the buyers, the broker, and the "safehouse" were all the same organization. She sold the ship to get the berth lost in the civilian registry, vanished to draw the heat, and trusted that whoever ended up with* Spinward Star *would either never look behind the welds or would look at exactly the right moment. Aramis Bonded Transit is the front. They've found the ship. They want it quietly — alive if possible, scrapped if not. The voices in the jump drive are a separate problem, and a much older one, and the crew will have time to worry about them later.*
