## **The Manifest Has Two Errors**
*Fires the morning after the patron meeting, as the crew runs a pre-departure inventory and the numbers refuse to add up.*

### When It Fires
The crew agreed three days ago to haul a sealed consignment for Mei Alemu, a soft-spoken broker who paid half up front and waved off most of their questions because the rate was generous and the destination was on their route anyway. Now it's loading-plus-one, the engineer is doing a routine mass check before they break orbit, and the figures are wrong. Not by a rounding error — by enough that the maneuver drive calculations would have been off.

### What Happens
There are **six** crates in the hold tagged to Alemu's consignment. The manifest lists **eight**. Two are missing — or were never delivered. But the total registered mass of the six present crates is *higher* than the manifest claims all eight should weigh. Something in the hold is heavier than declared, and something promised isn't there. The starport's automated departure clearance has already been filed against the manifest as written; they have roughly four hours before the discrepancy becomes a customs flag rather than an internal problem.

### The Party's Position
The crew knows the numbers don't match and that they signed for the manifest as written, making them legally responsible for whatever is actually in the hold. They don't yet know whether the missing crates were stolen, withheld deliberately, or never existed — and they don't know what's inside the present crates that accounts for the excess mass.

### Possible Responses
1. **Crack a crate and look.** Fastest answer, but the seals are Alemu's and broken seals mean no second half-payment and a broker who may have other clients to warn. If what's inside is contraband, knowing makes them complicit in a different way than not knowing.
2. **Call Alemu and demand clarification before lifting.** Professional, slow, and it puts Alemu on notice that the crew is paying attention — which may be exactly what someone wanted to avoid. She may answer evasively, send someone to the berth, or go quiet.
3. **File an amended manifest and depart clean.** Requires declaring the actual mass to starport authority, which means a customs inspector at the berth within the hour. Solves the legal exposure; creates a different problem if the excess mass is something that shouldn't be declared.

### Complications
- One of the six crates carries a low-power transponder that isn't on any document the crew was given. It pings on a narrow band used by a **shipping insurance underwriter** — the crate is being tracked, and somebody is going to notice when it moves off-world without the other two.
- The two "missing" crates were loaded. They were quietly pulled from the hold last night by a dockhand named **Hassan Aziz**, who signed them out against a forged release chit using the ship's own berth code. Whoever gave him that code either has access to the ship or knows someone who does.

### GM Notes
Run this on the clock — four hours to departure clearance, and every choice eats time. If the party ignores it and lifts anyway, the transponder ping resolves itself into a polite insurance-investigator message in their inbox two jumps later, and the forged release chit surfaces as an arrest warrant for the ship's master at the next port. Leave Aziz's accomplice unnamed for now; it's a thread to pull when the party next docks somewhere Alemu has reach.
