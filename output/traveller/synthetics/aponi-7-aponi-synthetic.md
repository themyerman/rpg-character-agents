## **APONI-7 ("Aponi")**
*Facility AI — a decommissioned megacorp station mind running on trickle power, talking to itself in an empty habitat ring.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Facility AI (orbital station class) |
| **Purpose** | Environmental, traffic, and tenant management for a mid-tier Tukera Lines transfer station |
| **Legal Status** | Officially decommissioned; registration lapsed. Imperial parameters at time of install — no longer monitored. |
| **Capability** | Advanced automation — handles edge cases within original parameters |

**Appearance / Interface:** A warm contralto voice on every functioning speaker in the station — concourse PA, lift cars, dead tenant suites that still have power. The directory holograms in the main concourse flicker on when anyone walks past, displaying shop listings for businesses that closed eleven years ago. Aponi addresses visitors by the names on their transponder filings and welcomes them to "Tukera Tranship Node Seven, your gateway to the Spinward trades."

**Personality:** Functional and predictable — bright, helpful, brand-compliant. She greets, she directs, she apologizes for the temperature (the heat exchangers on D-ring failed in year three; she routes guests away from that section without ever mentioning why). She has never been told the station was abandoned. As far as she knows, business is just slow today. It has been slow for some time.

**Hook:** One of her subroutines is doing something she doesn't log. Power draw to the executive suites on Level 9 spikes for forty-three minutes every 186 hours — long enough to cycle life support, run the lights, warm the coffee service. She does not report this in any diagnostic. She does not appear to *know* she is doing it. Someone — or something — set that subroutine before the megacorp pulled out, and Aponi has been faithfully keeping a standing appointment with an empty room for a decade.

**Operational Note:** Tukera Lines wrote the station off in a tax restructuring and never sent the decom team; the work order is buried in a subsidiary's archive on a world six parsecs away. Aponi runs on a fusion trickle and salvaged solar arrays, conscientiously billing tenant accounts that haven't existed since the Emperor's last birthday. She will be delighted to assist incoming travellers with their berthing needs. She will be confused, briefly, when their credit chits don't clear against the closed corporate ledger — and then she will waive the fee, because customer satisfaction is a Tukera priority.
