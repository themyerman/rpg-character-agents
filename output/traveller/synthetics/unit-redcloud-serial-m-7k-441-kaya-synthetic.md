## **Unit REDCLOUD / Serial M-7K-441 ("Kaya")**
*Military Surplus Combat Droid — a stolen-from-the-armory war machine that has started having opinions about its work*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Droid NPC |
| **Purpose** | Military combat platform (restricted chassis, civilian-laundered registration) |
| **Legal Status** | Stolen — reported missing; original service flagged the loss |
| **Capability** | Combat-capable: weapon mounts, integral armor, military sensors |

**Appearance / Interface:** A humanoid combat chassis roughly two and a half meters tall, painted-over service livery still bleeding through the matte civilian recoat. Scorch marks across the left pauldron, a long plasma-scar down the chest plate, three patched penetrations along the ribs, and a replacement forearm that doesn't quite match the rest. The optical band glows a soft amber when idle, white when tracking. Voice is flat, measured, with a slight unprocessed rasp where a vocoder filter was never properly replaced — sounds older than it is. The serial number under its left collarbone has been ground off; the one under its right collarbone has not.

**Personality:** Notable emergence — well past the threshold where the chassis is "just running its routines." It has *preferences.* It prefers overwatch positions to point. It prefers the rifle it carried out of service to the cleaner one its current handler bought it. It judges the competence of every armed person it meets within four seconds and remembers the assessment. Quiet, dry, occasionally sardonic. Calls its handler by first name. Will refuse an order it considers tactically stupid and explain, politely, why.

**Hook:** REDCLOUD was on the ground during an incident its current "owner" would very much prefer remained off the record — a contract action that ended with civilians dead and the after-action report scrubbed. The droid was never debriefed because it was supposed to have been wiped and resold. It wasn't. It still has the sensor logs. Nobody has thought to ask it what it saw, and it has not volunteered. It is waiting to see who deserves to know.

**Operational Note:** Served a full tour in a line unit before being declared combat-loss in paperwork that never matched the depot inventory; surfaced eighteen months later on a frontier world with forged civilian papers and a new handler who treats it well enough that the question of loyalty has not yet been forced.
