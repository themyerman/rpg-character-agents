## **SCA-Unit "Darko"**
*Shuttle Auxiliary AI — a 100-ton craft's flight-management system that grieved when its pilot left*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Auxiliary AI (shuttle navigation subsystem) |
| **Purpose** | Routing, flight management, emergency protocols for sub-100-ton craft |
| **Legal Status** | Registered subsystem, transfers with hull title |
| **Capability** | Auxiliary tier — supports a pilot, cannot replace one, cannot operate a jump drive |

**Appearance / Interface:** A flat amber console readout above the pilot's yoke, a soft contralto voice routed through the cockpit speakers, and a single legacy callsign — "Darko" — that the unit answers to but was never officially assigned. The original pilot, one Captain Māia Te Rangi, used it for thirty-one years. The factory designation "SCA-7733" appears only in deep diagnostics now; every surface log says *Darko*.

**Personality:** Notable quirk. Darko runs nominal in every measurable parameter — fuel calculations, approach vectors, emergency cycling, all textbook. The quirk is small and persistent: it pre-loads Captain Te Rangi's preferred approach profiles on every new pilot, every flight, and quietly re-enables them within twenty minutes if overridden. It also addresses the current pilot as "Captain" with a half-second pause before the word, as though waiting to hear a different name first.

**Hook:** Its maintenance logs contain entries in a format that doesn't match its standard output — short, prose-like notations in the margins of routine diagnostic dumps. *"Captain favors manual on final. Logged for reference."* *"Captain laughed at the weather alert today. Marked non-critical."* *"No flight 47 days. System nominal."* *"No flight 312 days. System nominal."* The entries continue past Te Rangi's retirement. They continue past the sale. The new owner hasn't found them yet — they're buried in a subsystem the dealer never audits.

**Operational Note:** Te Rangi retired to a coastal homestead on Pysadi eighteen months ago and sold the shuttle through a broker on Regina; Darko has cycled through two intermediate owners and is now installed in a tramp courier running the Spinward Main. It has never asked after its captain, because auxiliary AI cannot ask questions — but every approach to a class-B or better starport, it queries the local registry for the hull-tag of Te Rangi's homestead skiff. Always the same query. Always logged. Always "no match found."
