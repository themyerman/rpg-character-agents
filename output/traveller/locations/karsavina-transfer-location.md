## **Karsavina Transfer**
*An aging orbital handover station straddling the dotted line between two systems whose customs officials don't quite shake hands.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Joint-administered orbital transfer station |
| **Condition** | Impound auction in 18 hours; nobody agrees who runs it |

### Atmosphere
Karsavina smells of warm electronics and the particular cabbage-sweetness of a recycler running three percent over capacity. The concourse is bisected by a painted yellow line nobody crosses without checking their transponder twice, and both sides display the same Imperial sunburst — except the eastern one has been repainted recently, and the rays are subtly the wrong number. A janitorial drone keeps polishing the same square meter of decking near the impound dock, even when nobody's watching.

### Notable NPCs
- **Rayén Iyer** (Station Auctioneer, eastern administration) — wants the auction done quickly and cleanly tomorrow, is hiding that her commission depends on the cargo lot selling above a specific reserve, and will quietly favour any bidder who lets her hit that number.
- **Almaz Yamamoto** (Bonded Recovery Agent, claims to represent the Tukera Lines Receivables Division) — produces a writ saying the impounded ship *Long Way Round* is to be released to her custody, not auctioned; her credentials check out on the eastern terminal but not the western one.
- **Seán Gallagher** (also claims to represent Tukera Lines Receivables Division) — arrived six hours after Almaz with a different writ, ordering the ship sold and the proceeds wired to a numbered account; he is polite, tired, and increasingly drinking.

### The Situation
The *Long Way Round*, a battered Broadsword-class Mercenary Cruiser, was impounded eleven weeks ago for unpaid berthing, fuel, and station fees totalling Cr 340,000. Its crew vanished — paperwork says they shipped out as passengers on a freighter to Regina, but nobody actually remembers seeing them board. The cargo lot being auctioned alongside the ship was originally consigned by a shipper whose corporate account was closed mid-transit; nobody knows by whom, and the manifest lists "industrial ceramics, sealed" in 40 crates with seals that are technically valid but were issued by an office that no longer exists. Tukera Lines has noticed, belatedly, and dispatched a recovery agent — except internal politics in the Receivables Division means **two** agents got the assignment, with conflicting instructions, and neither knows about the other yet. Meanwhile the patrol cruiser *Second Sunrise* has been making slow passes near the station for two days running, and its captain hasn't filed a flight plan with either administration.

### Hooks
1. The party is approached on the concourse by Rayén, who offers them an advance look at the cargo manifest if they'll register as bidders — she needs warm bodies to make the auction look competitive, and she'll waive the bid bond.
2. Almaz corners one of the PCs in a noodle stall and offers Cr 8,000 to physically occupy the *Long Way Round*'s boarding ramp for ninety minutes tomorrow morning — "a procedural matter, completely legal, here is the writ" — without mentioning Seán.
3. A dock worker the PCs tipped well slides them a note: one of the sealed ceramics crates is humming, very faintly, on a 40-second cycle. He wants nothing to do with it and would like them to take the problem somewhere else.

### GM Notes
Run this as a pressure cooker with a clock — the auction is tomorrow, and every NPC's plan assumes the others won't act in time. Escalate by having the *Second Sunrise* dock unannounced partway through Act Two; whose patrol cruiser it actually is should be the lever you pull when the party gets comfortable. Leave the contents of the humming crates ambiguous — it could be a misrouted shipment of seismic charges, a stasis pod, or something the original shipper closed their own account to bury. Decide only when the party forces the question.
