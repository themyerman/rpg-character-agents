## **Tessek Junction Waystation**
*A bloated, overpriced jump-point platform clamped to a useless gas giant moon — the only fuel and air for three parsecs, and it knows it.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Jump-point waystation, sole operator on the route |
| **Condition** | Locked down under medical watch; a quarantine flag is up on an inbound ship |

### Atmosphere
The station smells faintly of ozone and orange-peel disinfectant — sprayed twice an hour through the concourse vents since the flag went up four shifts ago. Price boards flicker between Cr-denominations and a "transit surcharge" that climbs every two hours; a noodle stall is charging Cr85 for what is, unmistakably, ration paste with garnish. The wrongness: every screen displays the quarantine notice except the bonded auction terminal in Bay Six, which is cheerfully counting down in green as if nothing is happening at all.

### Notable NPCs
- **Stationmaster Itzcoatl Chalco** (Tessek Junction operations chief) — Wants the quarantine resolved before the route operator audits his profit margins; will quietly bend rules for travellers who can make the medical problem someone else's problem.
- **Lieutenant Moana Ngaropo** (Imperial Navy medical officer, attached from the patrol cruiser *Ardent Pilgrim*) — Suspects the inbound ship is carrying something worse than the manifest claims, and is one bad culture result away from welding the airlocks shut for ninety days.
- **Seán Murphy** (independent broker, perpetually sweating) — Is running the Bay Six auction on behalf of a shipper whose account was closed mid-transit; he is legally entitled to sell the lot but cannot produce the chain-of-custody paperwork a buyer would need to ever resell it.

### The Situation
The scout/courier *Far Horizon* came out of jump three days ago squawking a medical alert — two crew with something the autodoc couldn't name. Lt. Ngaropo, with the *Ardent Pilgrim* on standby, has locked down every airlock and is running cultures in a portable bio-lab she doesn't entirely trust. Meanwhile, the *Far Horizon's* shipper — a small house factor on the previous world — had their corporate account frozen for unrelated reasons (tax dispute, probably; embezzlement, possibly), which under waystation bylaws means the cargo lot in Bay Six goes to bonded auction in fourteen hours. Murphy is running that auction legally but cannot get authenticated transfer documents from a shipper who legally no longer exists. Whoever buys the lot owns it — and also owns a paperwork problem at every customs gate from here to the Marches. Chalco wants it gone before Ngaropo decides the cargo is part of the medical problem and impounds the whole bay.

### Hooks
1. Murphy approaches the party directly at the noodle stall: the auction reserve has been quietly dropped to a tenth of value, and he'll throw in his broker's commission if they bid. He does not mention the missing documents until after the hammer falls.
2. Lt. Ngaropo asks the party — specifically because they are *not* station staff and *not* Navy — to board the *Far Horizon* in soft suits and retrieve a tissue sample the autodoc refused to process. Hazard pay is real. The reason she isn't sending her own people is the part she won't explain.
3. Chalco's office pings the party's comms with a "transit surcharge waiver" offer: take a sealed package off-station on their next jump, no questions, and their docking fees vanish. The package is small. The waiver is large. The bay number on the pickup slip is Six.

### GM Notes
Run the three hooks as overlapping pressures, not a menu — the auction clock, the medical clock, and Chalco's clock should all be ticking in different directions, and any party action should accelerate at least one of them. Escalate by having Ngaropo's cultures come back ambiguous: not plague, not nothing, something *engineered-looking*. Keep what's actually in the Bay Six cargo lot ambiguous even to yourself until the party commits — let their paranoia choose whether it's contraband, evidence, or just unlucky machine parts whose owner ran afoul of the wrong tax assessor.
