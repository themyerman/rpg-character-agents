## **Kettleyard Transit**
*A bloated jump-point waystation orbiting nothing in particular, charging starport prices for vacuum and silence.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Jump-point waystation — independent, monopoly trade |
| **Condition** | Imperial audit in progress; everyone is being *helpful* |

### Atmosphere
Kettleyard hangs in deep space where two jump-1 routes kiss, a fat ring of welded-together hull sections lit from inside by yellow sodium lamps that flicker in a pattern someone once tried to fix and gave up on. The promenade smells of overheated electronics and a sweet floral disinfectant — far too much disinfectant, sprayed in long wet strips across decking that nobody is walking on. Every shopkeeper greets newcomers with the same precisely-worded phrase about "our commitment to fair trade practices," and the prices on the boards have all been freshly rewritten in cheaper ink than the dust-shadows beneath them suggest.

### Notable NPCs
- **Auditor-Lieutenant Sola Boateng** (Imperial Ministry of Commerce, Inspections Branch) — Three weeks into a six-week audit, riding the patrol cruiser *Outer Reach*; she knows the station's books are clean *now* and is quietly furious about what that means.
- **Mallku Quilaqueo** (Stationmaster, third-generation owner) — Wants the auditors gone, the books to stay closed, and the next jump point to keep paying him "transit courtesy fees"; will be effusively generous to anyone who can make a problem disappear without paperwork.
- **Prapat Setiawan** (Captain of the free trader *Persephone*, currently dirtside on the promenade) — The other crew. Warns the party off whatever Quilaqueo is about to offer them, won't say why in public, drinks too fast and watches the auditors' table the whole time.

### The Situation
Kettleyard makes its real money not from refuelling but from the jump-point beyond — a calibrated transit lane that requires a beacon-handshake from a private buoy operator camped on the far side, a soft-spoken Vargr called Kheghadze who has *opinions* about who gets to pass and what they should pay for the privilege. The arrangement has been profitable and quiet for two decades. Auditor Boateng's team can't prove the kickback structure because Quilaqueo scrubbed it the moment the cruiser pinged the system, but she's looking for one loose thread. Quilaqueo needs a discreet crew to run a package out to Kheghadze — partly payment, partly a message that the arrangement holds — and he needs it done while the auditors are still on station, because once they leave Kheghadze will assume the worst and start charging punitive rates. Setiawan took this same job two months ago. Something happened at the buoy. He came back with a half-empty cargo bay and a crewmember short, and he is trying, in his cautious way, to save the next idiots in line.

### Hooks
1. Quilaqueo invites the party to his private office — real wood panels, real coffee — and offers Cr 15,000 to deliver a sealed courier case to a buoy two hours out at the jump point. "A gesture of continued cordiality. No questions, no manifest, no audit trail."
2. Setiawan corners the party's pilot in the refresher queue and says quietly: "Whatever he's offering — the case isn't what he says it is. Ask him what happened to my engineer." Then walks away before they can respond.
3. Auditor Boateng's aide approaches the party at their table with a polite, official request: the Lieutenant would like a brief, *entirely voluntary* conversation about their travel plans and any commercial offers they may have received since docking.

### GM Notes
The squeeze is geometric — the party can't leave without transiting Kheghadze's jump point, which means they either play Quilaqueo's game, flip on him to Boateng (and then face the buoy operator alone), or find a third path through Setiawan. Escalate by having the *Outer Reach* delay departures "for routine inspection" the moment anyone tries to leave in a hurry. Keep what actually happened to Setiawan's engineer ambiguous — he believes Kheghadze killed her, but he was drunk and the airlock log is genuinely unclear, and that uncertainty is the whole reason he's still here drinking instead of filing a complaint.
