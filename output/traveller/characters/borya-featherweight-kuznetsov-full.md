### Career History

**Term 1 (age 18–22):** Qualified for the Scout Service on a borderline roll — the recruiter at the D-class port liked Borya's quiet competence and the fact that they already knew how to patch a vacc suit one-handed. Their first posting was unglamorous courier work along a frontier circuit, but they impressed an examiner enough to be bumped to Senior Scout. Mid-term, word came in by laser-mail: their grandfather Pádraig, the closest thing the Reach had to a town elder, had died in his sleep. Borya couldn't make the funeral. They learned what a one-way debt feels like.

**Term 2 (age 22–26):** Assigned to a deep-survey rotation, charting jump points in an underexplored cluster. A suit failure during EVA — a slow leak the diagnostics had logged as "nominal" — left them unconscious in vacuum for ninety-three seconds before their partner hauled them in. The decompression injury knocked a permanent point off their endurance and gave them a recurring sinus ache that announces weather changes. They came back to duty stubborn but slower, and were passed over for promotion. They started keeping a journal in a half-invented cipher language only they could read.

**Term 3 (age 26–30):** Paired on a long-range scout with Yasmin Hamdan — competent, charming, and ultimately faithless. Eight months into a two-year survey, Yasmin took the scoutship's only working lifeboat and a substantial fraction of the salvage manifest, leaving Borya with a crippled vessel near a barely-charted gas giant. Three months on minimum rations before a Naval picket happened through. The Service paid them out, gave them a medal nobody in the sector recognized, and quietly suggested they consider civilian life. They considered it.

### Backstory
Borya grew up in a thirty-person settlement under a whistling dome on a world too small to bend their bones into standard shape, and they left it the first chance they got — not to escape, but because the Scout Service was the only outfit that didn't care how much they could lift. The wound is Yasmin: not the betrayal itself but the months alone afterward, when Borya realized they'd written the partnership into the journal as something it had never been, and had to live with how badly they'd wanted it to be true. They're heading toward a quiet port now, with sixty thousand credits and a list of three names they'd like to find before the decade is out — Yasmin's is one of them, and they haven't yet decided what they want from her when they do.
