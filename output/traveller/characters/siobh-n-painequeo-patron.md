## **Siobhán Painequeo**
*Patron — a soft-spoken claims investigator who buys her own drinks*

| | |
|---|---|
| **UPP** | 7B5658 |
| **Apparent Affiliation** | Hortalez et Cie, independent insurance underwriters (mid-tier adjuster) |
| **Real Affiliation** | Hortalez et Cie — but acting on a personal mandate her supervisor never authorised |

**Appearance:** A compact woman in her early forties wearing a charcoal travel suit that's been pressed too many times, with a hand terminal clipped to her belt and reading glasses she keeps cleaning on her cuff. She drinks black tea, takes the seat with a view of the door, and waits for the crew to notice her before raising a hand — she does not mention how long she's been watching.

**The Pitch:** *"I'll be direct with you, because I think you'll appreciate that. A shipment left Regina nine days ago aboard a free trader called the* Ardent Promise*. It's carrying a consignment my employer insured at a value I am not allowed to disclose, and the consignee — the person waiting at the other end — is the subject of an ongoing fraud investigation. I need that shipment to not arrive. Intercept, board, hold the cargo in escrow, and wait for a Hortalez agent to take custody at Pixie. Nothing violent. Nothing illegal — I'll give you the paperwork. I just need it stopped before it changes hands."*

**The Job:** Locate the free trader *Ardent Promise* en route from Regina to Pixie (her flight plan is registered and public). Match jump, intercept at the destination's 100-diameter limit, board under colour of insurance lien, and impound the sealed cargo container — six tonnes, labelled "agricultural machinery components" — until a Hortalez claims officer arrives within 72 hours.

**The Payment:** Cr 45,000 on completion, paid through a Hortalez et Cie credit chit (verifiable, traceable, clean). Plus Cr 8,000 up front for fuel and legal filing. Plus a notarised letter of commendation from the underwriter — the kind of document that gets a tramp freighter cleared through customs without a strip search for years afterward.

**The Truth (Referee rolls 1d4 — only one is real):**
1. **Straightforward** — Siobhán is exactly who she says she is, the lien is valid, and the consignee really is under fraud investigation. The complication: the *Ardent Promise*'s captain is a decorated former Scout who will absolutely not let strangers board his ship on paperwork alone. Talking him down is the real job.
2. **One Layer Down** — Siobhán is a real Hortalez adjuster, but she filed this assignment herself without supervisor sign-off because the consignee defrauded her sister out of a family business six years ago. The lien is technically valid but procedurally premature; the crew is being used to settle a personal grudge inside a corporate uniform. Hortalez will honour the payment but will not back the crew if the consignee sues.
3. **The Real Story** — The "agricultural machinery components" are a prototype agricultural gene-stock the consignee legally purchased from a bankrupt Hortalez client. Siobhán's superiors want the genome back before it's catalogued in an open registry where they can no longer suppress it. Siobhán believes she's chasing a fraudster; she has been pointed at this target by her own management, who are using her conviction as cover. The crew is the second layer of deniability.
4. **The Reversal** — The consignee is a small cooperative on Pixie who legally bought drought-resistant grain stock from a failed agri-concern. Hortalez insured the seller's intellectual property and is now trying to claw back genetic material it no longer has any right to. Siobhán is sincere but wrong. The *Ardent Promise* is carrying food security for a marginal world, and the "fraud investigation" is a manufactured pretext filed three days ago. The crew, on figuring this out mid-boarding, will be holding farmers at gunpoint over seed.

**Why They'd Take It:** The money is real, the paperwork looks airtight, and Siobhán radiates the unshowy competence of someone who has done this fifty times and never lost a case.

**Connection:** **Bram Achterberg**, a dockside freight broker on Regina who has worked with Siobhán twice before on legitimate impound jobs and will vouch for her professionalism without hesitation — but who will also mention, if pressed over a second drink, that she "took some time off last year, family thing, came back quieter."
