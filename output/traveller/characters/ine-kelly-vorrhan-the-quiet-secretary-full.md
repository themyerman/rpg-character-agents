### Career History

**Term 1 (age 18–22):** Áine entered the Brennhal diplomatic corps as an Assistant, sent to ferry sealed dispatches between the oligarchy's twelve founding households. The work was tedious, the protocol crushing. She fell — quickly, badly — for a starport mechanic named Pell Doreth, whose grease-stained hands and unrepentant laughter made the recycled air taste different. The relationship was discovered, ended by family decree, and Pell was relocated offworld within a month. Áine learned, that year, exactly how much weight her surname carried, and exactly how little of it belonged to her.

**Term 2 (age 22–26):** Reassigned offworld as a gesture of mercy or punishment — even she wasn't sure — she stumbled into a buried scandal: an oligarch's third son had laundered Imperial credits through a charity for vacc-orphans. She filed no report. Instead she filed a memory, neatly indexed, for later use. The son became a quiet ally; she was promoted to Attaché, and her family stopped asking where she spent her evenings.

**Term 3 (age 26–30):** A rival faction tried to remove her with a sniper's slug at an embassy ball on Regina. The shot missed her sternum by four centimetres and killed the consul-general behind her. Áine spent three weeks in a hospital learning to breathe through bruised ribs and another three months learning who had paid for the rifle. She did not pursue revenge. She pursued leverage, and got it. She did not, however, advance — the incident left her marked, and superiors put her career on a slow burn.

**Term 4 (age 30–34):** Sent to a TL-9 fringe world as part of a trade renegotiation, she met a delegation of researchers who were not quite who they claimed to be. In a closed room, with the right questions, one of them touched her wrist and asked if she had ever felt objects move *toward* her when she was angry. She had. She had assumed everyone did. She did not advance that term either, but she came home with a name and an address she could not write down.

**Term 5 (age 34–38):** The name led her to a Psionic Institute operating out of a vacc-sealed warehouse three jumps from anywhere reputable. Her new mentor — patient, exacting, terrifyingly kind — began the testing. She was promoted to 3rd Secretary at her day job, which gave her cover. At night she learned what she was. She felt her body starting to slow: a stiffness in the left hand, a wheeze that wouldn't clear.

**Term 6 (age 38–42):** The Institute's training proper began, and it nearly broke her. Months of controlled testing — each talent probed, each failure costing something she would not get back — until what remained was precise and small and entirely hers. She can move a coin across a table without sweating. She cannot do much more than that, most days. Her advancement stalled — she was distracted, exhausted, hiding too much. Her END dropped again. She took her pension and walked away from both careers in the same week, with documentation in her pocket that called her a "licensed psionic technician" and a quiet certainty that someone, somewhere, would eventually ask to see it.

The testing process cost her nine points of that potential; what she has now is what the Institute left behind. PSI 12 undiminished would have been someone genuinely dangerous in a negotiating room — undetectable, effective, catastrophic in the right situation. She doesn't know she was that close to it.
- *Social situation:* She holds documentation as a "licensed psionic technician" — legally recognized in two subsectors and a serious complication everywhere else. The paper is real. Whether the Imperium honours it depends entirely on who's holding the paper at the time.

### Backstory
Áine grew up under glass on a world where forty families decide who breathes which air, and she was raised to be one of the deciders — diplomat, secretary, quiet hand on the lever. The wound is in her chest and in her past: a lover taken from her at twenty, a bullet that missed her at twenty-eight, lungs that have never forgiven the vacc-suit childhood, and a mentor who emptied her like a cup so she could learn to hold a single drop. She has retired, on paper; in practice she is waiting — for her Institute documentation to be tested, for the next person who needs a quiet secretary with a hidden gift and nothing left to lose. What she does not say, and what Adept Kor Yulen has never told her, is that she started with more raw potential than almost anyone he has trained — and that the version of her who might have walked through those doors at eighteen, untested and untouched, would have been something else entirely.
