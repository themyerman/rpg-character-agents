### Career History

**Term 1 (age 18–22):** Niamh started running messages between Balkanized cartel enclaves at fourteen — small, forgettable, vacc-suited like every other kid in the corridors. By eighteen she'd graduated to lifting cargo manifests for a mid-tier outfit called the Brass Hand. Her first real job — a refined-fuel skim at the B-class starport — went bad when port security caught the scent early. She'd have been pinned against a bulkhead with a stunner to her ribs if Joriman Vass hadn't dropped a smoke grenade in the service corridor and dragged her out by the collar. She made Hoodlum that year. She also started owing Vass favors she couldn't count.

**Term 2 (age 22–26):** The Brass Hand took a contract to move something nobody would name through three checkpoints — exactly the kind of work Niamh was getting good at. What she didn't know was that her crew chief, Petra Holcen, had already sold the route to a rival faction and needed someone to take the fall. The ambush came in a pressurized sublevel near Customs Dock 3. Niamh took a baton across the jaw, lost a tooth, and only escaped because Holcen's hired guns were sloppier than they were paid to be. She crawled through a maintenance vent, bartered her last clean ID for passage out, and was off Tessen Drift inside a week. Holcen is still alive. Niamh remembers her face every time she chews on the wrong side of her mouth.

### Connections

- **Enemy — Petra Holcen:** The Brass Hand crew chief who sold Niamh out to a rival faction and walked away clean; still operating somewhere on Tessen Drift, still pretending the ambush was somebody else's idea.

### Backstory
She was born in a pressurized warren on Tessen Drift, in a sector that changed flags four times before she was ten, and she learned early that loyalty is a currency you spend, not save. The wound is in her jaw and in her pride — Petra Holcen sold her out for a number she will probably never even learn, and that smallness eats at her more than the betrayal itself. She is off-world now with eleven thousand credits, a forged name, and a circled star she did not circle, and for the first time in her life nobody is telling her which corridor to walk down.
