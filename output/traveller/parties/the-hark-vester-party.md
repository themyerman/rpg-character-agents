## The *Hark-Vester* — Crew Brief
*Type-A2 Far Trader — patched in six colours, every one of them somebody else's*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Ship** | *Hark-Vester* (Type-A2 Far Trader, ex-Imperial surplus, twice renamed) |
| **Condition** | Worn but spaceworthy; jump drive runs hot, starboard cargo door sticks in cold ports |
| **What It Owes** | 312,000 Cr remaining on a salvage-title mortgage held by the Iruvani Mercantile Trust, plus an unwritten favour to a Scout Service supply clerk who lost her job covering for the original requisition |

### Crew
- **Captain Yelse Korrin** — Pilot / Owner-Aboard — Ex-Free Trader pilot who bought the ship at auction with a dead partner's insurance payout and has not slept a full eight hours since the ink dried.
- **Davrin "Tack" Molune** — Engineer / Gunner — A wiry mid-fifties drive tech mustered out of the Scouts after one too many quiet disagreements with his detachment commander; he kept a uniform patch and a grudge.
- **Sister Imeni Vau** — Medic / Steward — A field-trained chirurgeon who left a planetary relief order under unclear circumstances and now ministers to whatever wounded thing the cargo bay coughs up.

### How They Came Together
Yelse bought the *Hark-Vester* at a Scout Service surplus auction on Reskaal eighteen months ago — a hull her late partner Edro had spotted on the listings three weeks before he died of a stupid, preventable cardiac event in a dirtside bar. The insurance covered the down payment with enough left over for one jump's fuel. She had a ship and no crew.

Tack Molune was sitting in the same auction hall, half-drunk, watching his old detachment's courier go to a private bidder. He'd been the one who maintained that drive for eleven years. When Yelse asked who in the room could keep an A2 flying on a shoestring, three people pointed at him without looking up. He signed on for quarter-share and the right to do the wrenchwork his way; the supply clerk who'd quietly mis-filed the *Hark-Vester*'s service history to make the auction price reachable was his cousin, and that favour rides in the ledger unspoken.

Sister Imeni came aboard at Tashkir Down four months later, walking up the cargo ramp in a travel-stained habit with a medical kit and no luggage. She offered a year's labour in exchange for passage that kept going. Yelse, who had been bleeding crew and patience, did not ask the question on the tip of her tongue. Imeni has not asked to be put down at any specific port since.

### What Holds Them Together
The mortgage. Every eighty-four days the Iruvani Trust expects 11,400 Cr, and none of the three of them can make that number alone. The ship is the only thing any of them owns outright-ish, and selling shares would mean letting strangers vote on the bridge. So they fly, and they take the cargoes nobody clean wants, and they tell themselves it's temporary.

### The Fault Line
Tack thinks Yelse is still flying for Edro — taking sentimental routes through the Reskaal subsector when better-paying lanes run coreward — and he's started keeping a private log of refused contracts. Yelse, for her part, has noticed that Tack disappears for six hours every time they touch a Scout base and comes back with parts she didn't authorise and didn't pay for. Neither has said anything yet. The first time a Scout Service inspector boards looking for a serial number, that silence is going to detonate.

### Shared Secret
The third passenger berth has been welded shut from the inside of the bulkhead since Imeni came aboard. She did the welding. Yelse signed off on it. Tack ran the conduit around it without comment. None of them has spoken the word "reliquary" aloud, but Imeni visits the sealed compartment on the night of every jump exit, and the other two have learned the sound of her footsteps in the corridor and the discipline of not opening their cabin doors.

### First Session Hook
A cargo broker on Enroe — a woman named Selsa Hadrun who used to drink with Edro and who Yelse has been avoiding for a year — sends a private message offering 40,000 Cr to move four crates of "calibration equipment" to a Scout waystation in the Iresh Reach. The waystation is Tack's old posting. The manifest paperwork, when Imeni reviews it in her steward's role, lists a recipient name she recognises from the order she left. The contract pays exactly enough to clear two mortgage cycles, the route runs three jumps through the kind of empty where nobody asks questions, and every single member of the crew has a private reason to say no and a louder reason to say yes.
