## *Lost Causes* — Crew Brief
*Scout/Courier (modified) — a Service hand-me-down with too many secrets in her cargo manifest already*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Ship** | *Lost Causes* (Type-S Scout/Courier, Nasrin's retirement ship) |
| **Condition** | Worn but flightworthy; reactor still flinches when you push her, and the starboard airlock seal whistles in atmosphere |
| **What It Owes** | Nothing financially — but the Scout Service can recall her at any time, and they've started asking after her more often than is comfortable |

### Crew
- **Nasrin "Nas" al-Qadeer** — *Pilot / Astrogator / Owner* — Hands like quicksilver, four languages on her tongue, and a Service leash she pretends isn't there.
- **Major Aldric "Stoneface" Vehr** — *Gunner / Ship's Officer / Quiet Authority* — Twenty-four years of Marines, one-hundred-eighty-four names he recites each night, and a growing certainty that someone he used to salute wanted his men dead.
- **Séverine "Sev" Aldenberg-Vey** — *Broker / Face / Intelligence* — Retired Agent in evening wear, walking-around money in three currencies, and a blade hand that's only just begun to stiffen.
- **Korven "Half-Lung" Drask** — *Fixer / Muscle / Dockside Eyes* — Knows every back corridor in known space, breathes around a punctured lung, and is waiting for a ghost to find him.

### How They Came Together
Nasrin took the ship and ran courier work for two years out of Regina, picking up passengers when the Service's silences stretched long enough to worry her. Aldric was the first — booked Middle Passage out of Sector HQ the week after his retirement, carrying two duffels and a look she recognized from the Vilani crew who'd thawed her out of her dead scout. He paid in cash, asked no questions about her reactor, and when a customs official on Pyramus tried to shake them down on the second leg he was suddenly *there* between her and the desk in a way that ended the conversation. He never went home. She never asked him to.

Séverine came aboard on Regina, in a manner of speaking — she walked up the cargo ramp in a coat worth more than the ship's annual fuel budget, presented her unused High Passage, and asked if they took *informal* charters. Aldric knew an intelligence professional when he saw one and said so. Séverine smiled, said *good,* and stayed. She had just learned, through Daugherty, that Reyner Cortez had been quietly looking for her; she wanted a flag of convenience and a crew that wouldn't ask, and a Major and a desert-born pilot fit the requirement.

Korven they picked up on Thassik Station six months ago, because Séverine needed a fixer who knew the underside of three subsectors and the bartender pointed at the big man wheezing in the corner booth. He came aboard for one job, stayed for the next, and is still aboard because a message arrived at the next port saying *I found what you were looking for. Don't come alone* — and *Lost Causes* was going that direction anyway.

### What Holds Them Together
None of them has anywhere they'd rather be, and all four of them are being looked for by people they'd prefer not to be found by. The ship is the only address none of them officially has. They split jobs cleanly, they don't pry, and they have each, separately, made the calculation that the other three are useful enough to keep alive — which on this crew passes for love.

### The Fault Line
Aldric and Séverine. He has, by now, made her for what she is — Imperial intelligence, retired or otherwise — and he is becoming convinced, slowly and methodically the way he does everything, that the answer to *who signed the Kessar drop order* lives in a building she used to work in. She knows he's working up to asking. She has already decided she will lie to him when he does, because the truth of how that kind of operation gets approved would not survive contact with a man who recites the names of the dead nightly, and she is not yet willing to lose him as crew. When he finds out she lied — and he will — it will be very bad, and Nasrin will have to choose which of them stays on her ship.

### Shared Secret
They all know Korven has a stash. He's never said so; none of them has asked. But Aldric reads ground and reads men, Séverine reads everything, and Nasrin has flown him to all three of the starports where the money is and noticed that he goes for a walk alone at each one and comes back with nothing in his hands and something gone from his shoulders. They have collectively decided it isn't theirs to ask about — yet. The day someone shows up looking for it, that silence will become a conversation none of them wants to have.

### First Session Hook
The message that pulled Korven aboard has resolved into a destination: **Vard's Hinge**, a half-legal transfer station in a system that doesn't appear on civilian charts. The runaway — Korven's runaway, the kid he pulled out of a press-gang twelve years ago — wants a meeting, and claims to have *"what you were looking for,"* which Korven has not explained to anyone. Complicating matters: Séverine's quarterly pension payment cleared this morning into the Scout Service annuity, and twenty minutes later Nasrin received a Service recall ping requesting *Lost Causes* for a "routine availability check" at Regina, on a timeline that would put them three parsecs from where Korven needs to be. Aldric, reading the Service ping over Nasrin's shoulder, notes that the authorizing officer's name is one he last saw on a sealed Kessar-adjacent file. Three threads, one jump clock, and Vard's Hinge is in the wrong direction from all of them.
