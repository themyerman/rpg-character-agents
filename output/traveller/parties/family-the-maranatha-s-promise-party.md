# The *Maranatha's Promise* and Its Passengers: Mongoose Traveller 2e — Family
*Six branches of a sprawling merchant clan, bound by a freighter, a matriarch's tea, and the lies they tell each other to keep the ship flying.*

---

## **Adira Hamdan-Vos**
*Scholar (Senior Researcher) — A xenobotanist with calloused farmer's hands who treats every grudge like a perennial: patient, deep-rooted, and waiting for its season.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **UPP** | A663B4 |
| **Career** | Scholar, Senior Researcher |
| **Notable Skills** | Science (Biology) 3, Medic 1, Investigate 1 |

**Demeanor:** Broad-shouldered and quiet, with dirt permanently under her nails and a habit of staring at people the way she stares at unfamiliar specimens — too long, too still. She speaks softly because she learned early that nobody listens to loud women, and now she enjoys watching them lean in.
**Wants:** A sealed greenhouse berth on an outbound liner before her institute's auditors finish their review of the Kepwa-IV samples.
**Secret:** Three years ago she swapped tissue cultures with her younger brother Iosif to cover up that *his* doctoral dissertation was fraudulent. The fraud is now embedded in two pharmaceutical patents that fund the whole family.
**Hook:** She approaches the party in a portside cantina with a credit chip and a chilled biocase. She needs it walked — not shipped, *walked* — to a contact two systems over. No scanners. No questions. She'll pay double if nobody opens it.
**Connection:** Iosif Hamdan-Vos, her brother — brilliant, fragile, and entirely unaware that she's been protecting him since they were children. She would burn a city for him, and quietly resent him for the rest of her life.

## **Mireille Salinas-Devereaux**
*Drifter Wanderer — A wheezing scarecrow of a cousin who keeps showing up at the wrong airlock with the right warning.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **UPP** | 662265 |
| **Career** | Drifter (Wanderer) |
| **Notable Skills** | Streetwise 2, Recon 1, Stealth 1 |

**Demeanor:** Talks in half-finished sentences, like she lost the thread three stations ago. Bird-thin, perpetually short of breath, wears a heavy coat indoors and laughs at jokes nobody made.
**Wants:** A bunk on the family's ship for one jump — just one — and to be believed about something for once.
**Secret:** She isn't slow. The hypoxia damage from a vented cargo hold six years ago is real, but she exaggerates the fog because nobody interrogates someone they've written off. She's been quietly tracking a cousin's creditor across three subsectors.
**Hook:** Slides a data wafer across the table at the worst possible moment — mid-meal, mid-deal, mid-argument — and says, "I think they're going to kill Enrique. I think it's tonight."
**Connection:** **Enrique Salinas-Devereaux**, her younger brother and the only person in the family who ever stood up for her. He runs a failing hydroponics concern on a backwater and owes the wrong people. She'd walk into vacuum for him, and probably will.

## **Dr. Yelena Nwosu-Bogdanov**
*Scholar Associate Professor — Field surgeon who married into the family and is now the only one holding it together.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **UPP** | 79BA5B |
| **Career** | Scholar (Associate Professor) — practicing physician |
| **Notable Skills** | Medic 3, Science (biology) 2, Persuade 1 |

**Demeanor:** Calm in the way people are calm after they've stopped sleeping — soft voice, very still hands, eyes that finish a sentence three beats before you do. Smells faintly of antiseptic and the cheap black tea she drinks from a chipped enamel cup she carries everywhere.
**Wants:** A working cryoberth and twelve hours of uninterrupted access to a proper med bay — one of the family is sick and she's been treating it with field kits and lies.
**Secret:** She falsified the death certificate on a cousin two years ago. He didn't die of cardiac failure; she ended his suffering at his request, and the paperwork would unravel three planetary insurance claims and a custody case if anyone pulled the thread.
**Hook:** Approaches the party in a portside cantina with a folded credit chit and an unhurried question: *"How squeamish are you about transporting a patient who legally doesn't exist?"*
**Connection:** Her husband **Viktor Bogdanov** — the family's nominal patriarch, brilliant, charming, and showing the first tremors of a hereditary neurological disorder he refuses to acknowledge. She loves him and she is quietly, methodically lying to everyone in the family about how much time he has left.

## **Hanne Meles-Vaaro**
*Agent Field Supervisor — Smiles warmly at family dinners while calculating which sibling is most useful this quarter.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **UPP** | B72AAA |
| **Career** | Agent, Field Supervisor |
| **Notable Skills** | Deception-3, Persuade-2, Investigate-2 |

**Demeanor:** Disarmingly attentive — remembers the name of your childhood pet, asks the follow-up question no one else thought to ask. The eye contact lasts a beat too long, like they're reading subtitles off your face. Physically powerful (STR B) but moves carefully, almost gently, as if conscious that they don't always calibrate force correctly. The frail constitution (END 2) shows in a slight tremor and frequent medication; they treat the weakness as an interesting tactical variable rather than a vulnerability.
**Wants:** Custody of a younger family member — a teenage niece or nephew — under the pretext of "better schools off-world." The child is leverage in a long game Hanne hasn't fully explained to anyone, including themselves.
**Secret:** Killed the family matriarch four years ago. A pillow, a sedative, a forged DNR. The official cause was the cancer she already had. Hanne felt nothing then and feels nothing now — but does occasionally wonder if that's a problem they should solve, the way one wonders about an unfixed leak.
**Hook:** Hires the travellers as "neutral escorts" for a family reunion on a backwater world, claiming concerns about a stalker. The stalker is real — a private investigator the dead matriarch hired before she died, who is *very* close to the truth.
**Connection:** Their twin, **Riku Meles-Vaaro**, a clinic doctor who suspects everything and has said nothing. Hanne loves Riku in the only way they can — by never, ever using what they know about him. It's the closest thing to a moral line they have, and they're aware that's not saying much.

## **Tomás Adeyemi-Vasse**
*Entertainer (Artist) — A lounge singer with calloused hands and a voice that makes strangers cry into their drinks.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **UPP** | 8B6558 |
| **Career** | Entertainer, Artist |
| **Notable Skills** | Art (Performer) 3, Carouse 2, Persuade 1 |

**Demeanor:** Warm, theatrical, touches people on the arm when he laughs — and he laughs often, loud and a beat too long. Wears a battered acoustic resonator-guitar slung across his back like a child he refuses to put down. Up close, his eyes don't quite match the smile.
**Wants:** A clean berth off-world in the next 72 hours. He'll play for passage, cook, scrub plates — anything. No questions, please.
**Secret:** He has been quietly siphoning small sums from the family's shared accounts for three years to pay a private clinic treating his daughter's neurological condition. Nobody in the family knows the girl exists.
**Hook:** Approaches the PCs after a set, buys them a round, and "just happens" to mention he heard their ship has an empty stateroom. Offers to pay in rare pre-Imperial recordings he absolutely should not own.
**Connection:** His older sister **Renske Adeyemi-Vasse**, the family's matriarch and the one who raised him after their parents died — she'd forgive the theft in a heartbeat if he'd only ask. He cannot bear to ask. He'd rather run.

## **Captain Indira Vashenko-Arora**
*Merchant Captain — A grandmother who runs the family freighter like a sovereign nation, and her word is the only law that matters at the table.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **UPP** | 575999 |
| **Career** | Merchant Captain (retired, mostly) |
| **Notable Skills** | Broker 3, Persuade 2, Admin 2, Astrogation 1 |

**Demeanor:** Speaks softly because she has never needed to raise her voice — the cabin goes quiet when she enters. Always has tea brewing in a chipped enamel pot she's carried for forty years, and she pours for guests before she pours for herself.

**Wants:** To finalize succession before her heart gives out — and to make sure it's the *right* child, not the loudest one. She's been quietly testing them for years.

**Secret:** The family ship, *Maranatha's Promise*, isn't legally hers. Her late husband signed it over to a Tukera shell company decades ago to dodge a debt, and the paperwork was never reversed. If anyone audits the registry, the whole family loses everything.

**Hook:** She'll hire the characters for a "simple" cargo run that's actually a pretext — she wants them to observe one of her grandchildren on board and report back honestly on whether they're ready to inherit.

**Connection:** Her estranged eldest son, **Joaquim**, who left the family fifteen years ago after she chose his younger sister as first officer. She tracks his movements through brokers' gossip and has never stopped leaving a berth empty for him.

## Web of Connections
- **Adira / Mireille:** Adira sees Mireille as a security risk in a heavy coat — she's been quietly lobbying Indira to deny her a berth, unaware Mireille has already pieced together half the Kepwa-IV cover-up from overheard comms.
- **Adira / Yelena:** They are the family's two scientists and they do not speak unless they must; Yelena suspects the tissue-culture fraud and Adira knows she suspects, and they pass each other in corridors like duelists who've agreed on a date.
- **Adira / Hanne:** Hanne has known about Iosif's fraud for two years and has never mentioned it — Adira mistakes this for ignorance and is grateful, which is precisely how Hanne prefers to be owed.
- **Adira / Tomás:** They were close as children; she taught him to whistle bird calls in the cargo bay, and she's the one family member he genuinely cannot bring himself to steal from, which is why his accounts are short.
- **Adira / Indira:** Indira raised Adira after her parents died in a depressurization, and Adira's farmer's hands learned their patience from the old captain's — the grandmother is the only person Adira would confess to, and the only one she never will.
- **Mireille / Yelena:** Yelena treats Mireille's hypoxia and pretends not to notice that her cousin's lung scans haven't worsened in three years; they have a quiet understanding built entirely on what neither will say.
- **Mireille / Hanne:** Hanne finds Mireille fascinating — the only family member whose interior life Hanne genuinely cannot map — and has begun leaving small tests in her path to see what she does with them.
- **Mireille / Tomás:** Mireille has noticed the discrepancy in the shared accounts and has said nothing — she's been skimming the same fund herself for different reasons, and the last thing either of them needs is Indira doing an audit.
- **Mireille / Indira:** Indira is the only one who has ever spoken to Mireille like an adult; Mireille would die for her, and Indira knows it, and tries not to use it.
- **Yelena / Hanne:** Yelena signed the matriarch's death certificate four years ago and didn't look too closely at the pillow fibers — she suspects Hanne, Hanne knows she suspects, and they are unfailingly polite at every holiday.
- **Yelena / Tomás:** Tomás's daughter is Yelena's secret patient; Yelena has been falsifying the clinic records on her end while Tomás skims the family accounts on his, and neither has told the other they're both lying about the same child.
- **Yelena / Indira:** Indira has begun to suspect that her husband Viktor is sicker than Yelena admits, and has started watching her daughter-in-law over dinner the way she once watched weather fronts — looking for the tells before the storm shows itself.
- **Hanne / Tomás:** Hanne has known about the daughter for eighteen months and considers her a useful pressure point they have chosen, so far, not to use. Tomás senses something, plays louder when Hanne is in the room, and cannot explain why.
- **Hanne / Indira:** Indira doesn't know what Hanne did to the matriarch — but she knows something is wrong with the death the way a ship captain knows something is wrong with a seal: not by evidence, by feel. She watches Hanne with the same flat patience she uses for long burns.
- **Tomás / Indira:** He is her favorite and they both know it and neither will say it, and that shared silence is the warmest thing on the ship.

## GM Hooks
1. A Tukera Legal representative arrives at the docking bay with a thirty-year-old registry transfer document and a polite but firm request to discuss the *Maranatha's Promise* — the debt Indira's late husband buried has been found, and the family has 72 hours to produce a counter-claim or vacate the ship.
2. Mireille's warning about Enrique comes true: a pair of heavily built men board at the next port asking about "a debtor named Salinas" — they're one step behind, but one step is nothing on a ship with only so many places to hide.
3. The private investigator the dead matriarch hired — a methodical, soft-spoken Vargr named Ehksash — contacts the player characters directly, having identified them as recent passengers. She doesn't want money. She wants testimony. And she's already decided Hanne is guilty.
