## **Halver Ostrek**
*Patron — a tired man in a cheap coat who knows the manifest number of crate three*

| | |
|---|---|
| **UPP** | 653882 |
| **Apparent Affiliation** | Independent claims adjuster, Iruvani Mercantile Trust subcontract |
| **Real Affiliation** | *(referee's secret — see Truths 2 and 3)* |

**Appearance:** A narrow, grey-skinned man in his late fifties, wearing a Trust-logo lanyard that's the wrong shade of blue and a coat that's seen three planets this week. He's drinking caf, not alcohol, takes small careful sips like his stomach has opinions, and doesn't sit until invited — but when he does sit, he sits between the crew and the door.

**The Pitch:** *"Captain Korrin. No, please — finish your meal. I'll be quick. The Trust hired me to look into a paperwork discrepancy on your last run out to Iresh — nothing that affects your mortgage, your standing is fine, that's not why I'm here. The recipient on that manifest filed a loss claim against the carrier eleven days after you delivered. Four crates signed off, three crates opened. I'm not accusing anyone of anything; I think someone at the waystation is running a small skim and using transient ships to cover it. I'd like to hire you to do the same route again with a marked shipment, and tell me who signs for what."*

**The Job:** Run a second, smaller cargo (two crates, genuine calibration gear this time, with covert tracking seals Ostrek will install) from Enroe back to the Scout waystation in the Iresh Reach. Deliver normally. Note who handles the crates, who signs, and any deviation from the receiving protocol. Return to Enroe within forty days with the seal logs and a verbal account. He suggests using Selsa Hadrun as the booking broker again — "for consistency."

**The Payment:** 25,000 Cr on departure, 25,000 Cr on return with usable logs. Ostrek also offers, almost as an aside, to "see what he can do" about a serial-number query that's been flagged on the *Hark-Vester*'s starboard power coupling — a query he describes as "the sort of thing that resolves itself if nobody chases it."

**The Truth (Referee rolls 1d4 — only one is real):**

1. **Straightforward** — Ostrek is exactly the small-time claims investigator he says he is, hired by an Iruvani Trust subsidiary to chase a petty theft ring at Iresh waystation. The complication: the skimmer at the waystation is one of Tack's former detachment mates — a man Tack knows is feeding a sick child's medical bills and whom Tack will recognise on sight the moment they make the drop.

2. **One Layer Down** — Ostrek works for the Trust, but not the claims office. He's a *recovery* investigator, and the "loss claim" is a fiction; what he actually wants is independent confirmation of who's receiving freight at that waystation under that recipient name. The Trust has reason to think someone is moving Trust-collateralised goods off-books, and the *Hark-Vester*'s previous run is the cleanest data point they have. The crew aren't suspects — they're instruments. But the report Ostrek files will name them, and the recipient's faction will read it.

3. **The Real Story** — Ostrek is Scout Service Internal Affairs, deep-cover, three years into an investigation of a quiet contraband pipeline running through decommissioned waystations in the Iresh Reach. He knows exactly what was in the original four crates and he knows it wasn't calibration equipment. He's offering the crew a second run because he needs a known-dirty carrier to set up a controlled buy. The serial-number "favour" is real — IA can make that paperwork go away — and it's also leverage. If the crew run the job and it works, he walks away grateful. If they refuse, the *Hark-Vester*'s entire surplus-auction provenance gets pulled apart by an inspector with a name and a warrant, and Tack's cousin loses more than her job.

4. **The Reversal** — Ostrek is no longer with anyone. He was a Trust investigator and an IA stringer; six months ago his handler died and his contracts went quiet, and he has been freelancing his old credentials to settle a personal grievance. The recipient at the Iresh waystation is the officer who buried the case that ended Ostrek's career, and the "marked shipment" Ostrek wants the crew to deliver contains a forensic beacon that is also, technically, a frame — calibration gear cut with enough of a controlled compound to put the recipient away for fifteen years. Nothing Ostrek has said is a lie. He has not told the crew that when the waystation's security opens those crates, the *Hark-Vester*'s transponder will be the last vessel logged through the seal. Imeni's name is on the original manifest as steward of record.

**Why They'd Take It:** Because the money clears another mortgage cycle, because the serial-number favour is the first time anyone has offered to make that problem smaller instead of larger, and because every one of them already knows the route.

**Connection:** **Selsa Hadrun**, the cargo broker on Enroe. She's met Ostrek twice and didn't like him either time — "He asks about Edro," she'll tell Yelse if Yelse finally walks into her office and asks. "He asks about Edro like he's looking for a hole somebody left." She won't say more without a drink, and she will not put it in writing.
